Model 2 · Gaussian Wave Packet ($\Delta x$ & $\Delta p$ in time)
0.45 1.5 1.0 Δx = — Δp = — Δx·Δp = — (ħ/2 = —)

What this shows

A free Gaussian wave packet with initial width $\sigma_0$ and mean momentum $p_0$. It translates and spreads: $\sigma(t) = \sigma_0\,\sqrt{1 + \left(\frac{\hbar t}{2m\sigma_0^2}\right)^2}$. Momentum width stays constant: $\Delta p = \frac{\hbar}{2\sigma_0}$. Product $\Delta x\cdot\Delta p \ge \frac{\hbar}{2}$.
