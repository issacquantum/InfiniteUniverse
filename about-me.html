About Me

Born in Mexico City to Raúl Tabares Torres and Margarita Mendoza López, and brother to Ismael Isaias Tabares Mendoza, I grew up in a modest environment that demanded resilience. From my earliest days, my mind has been unusually active and curious, constantly exploring, questioning, and seeking connections in the world around me.

One of my earliest memories, one that has profoundly shaped my life and made me obsessed with the piano, was hearing a piano for the first time. I did not yet know what a piano was, nor where the sound came from; likely, it emanated from a distant radio. Years later, in my mid-teens, I discovered that the piece was Rachmaninoff’s Rhapsody on a Theme of Paganini, Op. 43, Variation 18. This early encounter with music became a touchstone for the way I experience the world: through patterns, resonance, and the pursuit of meaning.

From childhood onward, I have grappled with fundamental questions: Why do I exist? Why does anything exist? What is the purpose of life? How does the universe function? Conversations with my father offered early glimpses into these questions; one answer that stayed with me was his description of God as something without beginning or end—a concept that ignited my fascination with time, infinity, and the structure of reality.

I conceive of what many call “God” as consciousness itself, a universal awareness beyond the confines of specific religious interpretations. From this perspective, existence is an interconnected web in which we are not merely individual entities, but aspects of the whole. We are animals and plants, molecules and viruses, tools and instruments, the young and the old, the innocent and the violent. We are stars, black holes, and every manifestation of matter and energy. Consciousness experiences reality through every conceivable perspective, across an infinite array of configurations, encompassing the entirety of existence.

Beyond these profound explorations, my curiosity extends into other creative and intellectual realms. I find immense joy in "reading," though for me, this often translates into delving into physics books that present complex problems and concepts rather than typical literature. These academic texts fuel my analytical mind and push the boundaries of my understanding. Additionally, I engage in both manual and digital art forms, drawing and painting with conventional tools like pencil, charcoal, and paper, as well as digitally using iOS devices and Procreate. This creative expression serves as another avenue for exploring patterns, visualizing concepts, and bringing abstract ideas to life.

Playing Tekken 1-3 for the first time when I was five ignited my interest in technology, and I haven't stopped playing Tekken ever since. My fascination stemmed from its inherent complexity; the deeper the mechanics and strategies, the more drawn I am to understanding and mastering them. This inherent drive to unravel intricate systems and master complex strategies is a quality that seamlessly extends to my professional life, where I thrive on dissecting challenging problems and developing robust solutions. This pursuit of intricate systems became a recurring theme in my life.

I approach learning and problem-solving with rigor and persistence, often breaking down complex phenomena into fundamental principles. I am drawn to patterns, both in nature and abstract systems, and I enjoy constructing models, mathematical, computational, or conceptual, to explore hypotheses and uncover insights. My methods are iterative: observe, refine, and integrate, always seeking a deeper understanding of the systems I study.

Over the years, it has felt as if the universe itself has guided me toward the subjects and disciplines necessary to understand existence: physics, cosmology, mathematics, quantum mechanics, programming, and more. The quest to comprehend time, infinity, and the fundamental nature of reality continues to drive both my personal and intellectual life, manifesting in my projects focused on exploring the universe through experimental coding and theoretical modeling.

My intellectual pursuits span multiple disciplines, from cosmology and quantum mechanics to computer science and systems engineering. I explore these fields experimentally through coding projects, simulations, and theoretical modeling, seeking to visualize and understand the universe in novel ways. These endeavors are part of an ongoing effort to bridge abstract concepts with tangible exploration.

Existing is hard, yet it’s something I would repeat infinitely. I would love to experience every possible configuration of life, no matter how chaotic and difficult that would be. This boundless curiosity and an eagerness to embrace complexity, whether in deciphering cosmic laws, mastering new technologies, is the essence of my approach to every endeavor, fueling my desire for continuous learning and adaptation.

I am fascinated not just by answers, but by the questions themselves, and I see every challenge as an opportunity to expand understanding. My vision is to map the intricate interplay between reality, consciousness, and the cosmos, and to translate that knowledge into tools, models, and experiences that others can explore and learn from.
