This section is about systems work as part of my life, not as the purpose of the whole site. It explains how work, technology, and pressure became a path of survival and technical growth.

My professional career did not begin under favorable conditions. At 17, still a minor, the only work available to me was a factory job dedicated to manufacturing the disposable plastic cutlery commonly used at gatherings across Mexico. The conditions were harsh. The role required carrying heavy loads of materials repeatedly throughout the shift, and within the first week I sustained an injury. I lasted one week. That experience, brief and painful as it was, made one thing clear: my long-term path had to be technical and intellectual. I needed to build a future around analysis, systems, and problem solving rather than physical labor.

At 18, I relocated to Querétaro and began working at a Skechers retail store as a salesman. I was good at it. I moved up to assistant manager within a relatively short period of time. But the most consequential thing that happened at that job had nothing to do with footwear. A customer came in, and during our interaction he noticed I spoke fluent English. That small detail changed the direction of my career entirely. He connected me with a contact of his who was looking to fill an IT position. I interviewed, and I got the job.

What I had not fully recognized until that moment was how much my long interest in technology had prepared me. Computing, software, hardware, and the logic of how systems work were already familiar to me before that first IT role. That foundation proved to be entirely sufficient. From that point on, I have never left the field. Every position since has been within the technology sector, and each one has pushed me further.

Over the years my career has evolved considerably. I moved from general IT support into data analysis, and from there into systems engineering and data and systems integration. I currently work as a Data and Systems Integration Tier 2 Analyst at BlackLine, operating at the intersection of enterprise software, financial systems, and technical problem solving. The role demands precision, analytical thinking, and a thorough understanding of how complex systems communicate and fail, all of which align naturally with how I have always approached any problem placed in front of me. In plain terms, I help investigate why data or workflows fail, validate what the systems expect, and restore clean movement between tools and teams.

For a concise technical document, use my Knowledge and Systems Record. I keep it here because it belongs inside the systems-work context, not as the purpose of the whole site.

My interest in programming extends past the workplace because it helps me think with more precision. For this site and related work, the practical tools are VS Code, HTML, CSS, JavaScript, content structure, and interface logic. I am also studying Python, but I am not presenting it here as a core tool behind this site.

The wider scientific parts of my curiosity belong more naturally in Personal Cosmology. In this section, the career point is narrower: technology became the path toward stability, professional value, and a way forward that did not depend on physical labor.

The one resource I consistently find myself short of is time. Every day carries more that I want to learn, build, improve, and create than the hours available allow.
