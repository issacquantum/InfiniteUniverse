CHSH (Clauser-Horne-Shimony-Holt) Bell Inequality

Equation

$$ |S| = \left|E(a,b) - E(a,b') + E(a',b) + E(a',b')\right| \leq 2 $$

Literal Reading

absolute value of E of a comma b minus E of a comma b prime plus E of a prime comma b plus E of a prime comma b prime is less than or equal to 2.

Common Reading

The CHSH combination of correlation functions cannot exceed two for any local hidden-variable theory.

Symbols

\(S\): the CHSH combination built from four correlation measurements.

\(E(a,b)\): the correlation measured when observer A uses setting \(a\) and observer B uses setting \(b\).

\(a, a'\): two possible measurement settings on side A.

\(b, b'\): two possible measurement settings on side B.

\(\leq 2\): the upper bound that any local hidden-variable model must obey.

Meaning of the Equation

Classical local hidden-variable theories satisfy \(|S|\le2\). Quantum theory can reach the Tsirelson bound \(|S|\le2\sqrt2\).
