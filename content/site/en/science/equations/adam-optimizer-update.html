Adam Optimizer Update

Equation

$$ \theta_{t+1}=\theta_t-\eta\frac{\hat m_t}{\sqrt{\hat v_t}+\epsilon} $$

Literal Reading

theta sub t plus 1 equals theta sub t minus eta m sub hat sub t over of square root of v sub hat sub t plus epsilon.

Common Reading

Adam adapts the step size per parameter using running estimates of gradient mean and variance.

Symbols

\(\hat m_t\): bias-corrected first moment estimate of the gradient.

\(\hat v_t\): bias-corrected second moment estimate.

\(\eta\): learning rate.

\(\epsilon\): small constant preventing division by zero.

Meaning of the Equation

Adam changes each parameter with a step that reflects both recent gradient direction and gradient volatility. That is why it can move differently across parameters instead of using one identical learning rate everywhere.
