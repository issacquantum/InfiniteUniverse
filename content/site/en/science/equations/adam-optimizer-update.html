Adam Optimizer Update

Equation

$$ \theta_{t+1}=\theta_t-\eta\frac{\hat m_t}{\sqrt{\hat v_t}+\epsilon} $$

Literal Reading

Read it as: the parameters move by a learning rate times a bias-corrected first moment divided by a scaled second moment.

Common Reading

Adam adapts the step size per parameter using running estimates of gradient mean and variance.

Symbols

\(\hat m_t\): bias-corrected first moment estimate of the gradient.

\(\hat v_t\): bias-corrected second moment estimate.

\(\eta\): learning rate.

\(\epsilon\): small constant preventing division by zero.

Meaning of the Equation

This relation turns an algorithmic step into a precise rule. It identifies what is being updated, counted, or bounded, so the process can be checked by its state, cost, error, or learning behavior instead of only by a visual animation.
