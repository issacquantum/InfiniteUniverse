Strong Subadditivity

Equation

$$ S(ABC) + S(B) \le S(AB) + S(BC) $$

Literal Reading

The entropy of ABC plus the entropy of B is less than or equal to the entropy of AB plus the entropy of BC.

Physicist's Reading

A physicist would read it as a deep constraint on how correlations can be distributed among three quantum systems.

Symbol Breakdown

\(A\), \(B\), \(C\): three subsystems.

\(S(ABC)\): entropy of the combined system.

\(S(AB)\), \(S(BC)\): entropy of paired subsystems.

Physical Meaning

Strong subadditivity limits how information can be shared. It is one reason quantum correlations have a rigid structure rather than behaving like arbitrary pieces of hidden data.
