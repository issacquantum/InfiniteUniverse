Strong Subadditivity

Equation

$$ S(ABC) + S(B) \le S(AB) + S(BC) $$

Literal Reading

Read it as: the entropy of ABC plus the entropy of B is less than or equal to the entropy of AB plus the entropy of BC.

Common Reading

A deep constraint on how correlations can be distributed among three quantum systems.

Symbols

\(A\), \(B\), \(C\): three subsystems.

\(S(ABC)\): entropy of the combined system.

\(S(AB)\), \(S(BC)\): entropy of paired subsystems.

Meaning of the Equation

This relation describes a quantum state, transformation, probability, or correlation. It matters because quantum systems are not described by one definite classical value; the equation tells what can be predicted from amplitudes, operators, or density matrices.
