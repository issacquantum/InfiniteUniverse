Strong Subadditivity

Equation

$$ S(ABC)+S(B)\le S(AB)+S(BC) $$

Literal Reading

S of A B C plus S of B is less than or equal to S of A B plus S of B C.

Common Reading

Strong subadditivity is a fundamental consistency condition for quantum entropy across overlapping subsystems.

Symbols

\(A\), \(B\), \(C\): three subsystems.

\(S(ABC)\): entropy of the combined system.

\(S(AB)\), \(S(BC)\): entropy of paired subsystems.

Meaning of the Equation

This is a consistency law for how quantum correlations can be shared. It prevents entropy assignments that would make overlapping subsystems carry impossible amounts of shared information.
