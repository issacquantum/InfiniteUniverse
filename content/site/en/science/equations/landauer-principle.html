Landauer's Principle

Equation

$$ E_{\min} = k_B T \ln 2 $$

Literal Reading

Read it as: the minimum energy is equal to Boltzmann's constant times temperature times the natural logarithm of two.

Common Reading

The ideal heat cost of irreversibly erasing one logical bit.

Symbols

\(E_{\min}\): the minimum energy dissipated as heat.

\(k_B\): Boltzmann's constant.

\(T\): the temperature of the environment.

\(\ln 2\): the natural logarithm of two, appearing because one classical bit has two possible values.

Meaning of the Equation

Landauer's principle connects information erasure to thermodynamics. If a device irreversibly resets one unknown bit to a fixed value, the lost logical alternative must be paid for as heat in the environment. The value \(k_BT\ln2\) is an ideal lower bound under ideal thermodynamic conditions, not a claim that real computers always operate exactly at that cost.
