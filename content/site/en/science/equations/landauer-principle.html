Landauer's Principle

Equation

$$ E_{\min} = k_B T \ln 2 $$

What the Formula Says

The minimum energy cost equals Boltzmann's constant times temperature times the natural logarithm of two.

What It Means in Practice

This means the least heat that must be dissipated when one bit of information is erased irreversibly at temperature \(T\).

Symbols

\(E_{\min}\): the minimum energy dissipated as heat.

\(k_B\): Boltzmann's constant.

\(T\): the temperature of the environment.

\(\ln 2\): the natural logarithm of two, appearing because one classical bit has two possible values.

Why It Matters

Landauer's principle makes information physical. Erasing information is not just a logical operation; it has a thermodynamic cost tied to heat, entropy, and irreversibility.
