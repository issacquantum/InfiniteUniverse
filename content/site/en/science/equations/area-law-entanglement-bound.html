Area-Law Entanglement Bound

Equation

$$ S(A)=O(|\partial A|) $$

Literal Reading

Read it as: the entropy of region \(A\) grows on the order of the size of the boundary of \(A\).

Common Reading

Entanglement scales with the boundary, not the full volume.

Symbols

\(S(A)\): entanglement entropy of region \(A\).

\(\partial A\): the boundary of region \(A\).

\(|\partial A|\): size or area of that boundary.

\(O(\cdot)\): scaling up to constant factors.

Meaning of the Equation

An area law says the information linking a region to the outside is concentrated near the boundary. This is why some many-body quantum states can be compressed by tensor networks. It is not a universal rule for all quantum states; highly entangled states can violate it.
