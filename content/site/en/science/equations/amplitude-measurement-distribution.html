Amplitude Measurement Distribution

Equation

$$ |x\rangle=\frac{1}{\|x\|_2}\sum_i x_i|i\rangle $$

Literal Reading

Read it as: P open parenthesis i close parenthesis equals vertical bar alpha sub i vertical bar to the power of 2.

Common Reading

Measuring this state returns index \(i\) with probability \( |x_i|^2/\|x\|_2^2 \), so quantum measurement naturally performs length-square sampling.

Symbols

\(|x\rangle\): amplitude-encoded quantum state.

\(\|x\|_2\): Euclidean norm used to normalize the state.

\(x_i\): amplitude assigned to basis state \(|i\rangle\) before normalization.

\(|i\rangle\): computational-basis state corresponding to index \(i\).

Meaning of the Equation

Classical data are represented or sampled through quantum amplitudes and squared norms.
