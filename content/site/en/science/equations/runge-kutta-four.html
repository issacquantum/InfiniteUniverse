Runge Kutta Four

Equation

$$ y_{n+1}=y_n+\frac{h}{6}(k_1+2k_2+2k_3+k_4) $$

Literal Reading

Read it as: the next value equals the current value plus a weighted average of four slope estimates.

Common Reading

RK4 (fourth-order Runge-Kutta) (Runge-Kutta fourth-order method) samples the derivative within a step to get a more accurate update than Euler's method.

Symbols

\(h\): step size.

\(k_1,k_2,k_3,k_4\): slope estimates inside the current step.

\(y_n\) and \(y_{n+1}\): current and next states.

Meaning of the Equation

This relation turns an algorithmic step into a precise rule. It identifies what is being updated, counted, or bounded, so the process can be checked by its state, cost, error, or learning behavior instead of only by a visual animation.
