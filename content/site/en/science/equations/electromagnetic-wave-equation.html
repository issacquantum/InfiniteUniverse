Electromagnetic Wave Equation

Equation

$$\nabla^2\mathbf{E}-\frac{1}{c^2}\frac{\partial^2\mathbf{E}}{\partial t^2}=0$$

Literal Reading

Read it as: the Laplacian of E minus one over c squared times the second time derivative of E equals zero.

Common Reading

The electric field can propagate as a wave through empty space.

Symbols

  • \(\nabla^2\): spatial curvature of the field.
  • \(\partial^2/\partial t^2\): second time change.
  • \(c\): wave speed in vacuum.

Meaning of the Equation

This relation connects the mathematical state of a physical system to what can happen next or what can be measured. It is not just notation: it states how energy, fields, waves, or probabilities constrain the behavior of the system.
