Qubit Normalization Condition

Equation

$$ |\alpha|^2 + |\beta|^2 = 1 $$

What the Formula Says

The modulus squared of alpha plus the modulus squared of beta equals one.

What It Means in Practice

This means the total probability of all possible measurement outcomes for a single qubit must sum to one.

Symbols

\(|\alpha|^2\): the probability of measuring the qubit in state \(|0\rangle\).

\(|\beta|^2\): the probability of measuring the qubit in state \(|1\rangle\).

\(= 1\): the total probability is normalized, so no probability is missing or exceeds the allowed total.

Equation Meaning

This condition guarantees that the qubit state represents a valid physical system. Without normalization, the amplitudes would not define consistent measurement probabilities under the Born rule.
