Qubit Normalization Condition

Equation

$$ |\alpha|^2 + |\beta|^2 = 1 $$

Literal Reading

Read it as: the modulus squared of alpha plus the modulus squared of beta equals one.

Common Reading

The total probability of all possible measurement outcomes for a single qubit must sum to one.

Symbols

\(|\alpha|^2\): the probability of measuring the qubit in state \(|0\rangle\).

\(|\beta|^2\): the probability of measuring the qubit in state \(|1\rangle\).

\(= 1\): the total probability is normalized, so no probability is missing or exceeds the allowed total.

Meaning of the Equation

This relation describes a quantum state, transformation, probability, or correlation. It matters because quantum systems are not described by one definite classical value; the equation tells what can be predicted from amplitudes, operators, or density matrices.
