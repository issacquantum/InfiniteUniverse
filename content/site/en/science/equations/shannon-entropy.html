Shannon Entropy

Equation

$$ H(X) = -\sum_x p_x \log_2 p_x $$

Literal Reading

H of X equals minus the sum over each outcome of the probability of that outcome times the base two logarithm of that probability.

Common Reading

Short version: The average uncertainty, measured in bits, in a classical random variable.

Symbols

\(H(X)\): the Shannon entropy of the random variable \(X\).

\(p_x\): the probability of outcome \(x\).

\(\sum_x\): sum over all possible outcomes.

\(\log_2\): logarithm base two, so the entropy is measured in bits.

Meaning of the Equation

The average uncertainty, measured in bits, in a classical random variable.
