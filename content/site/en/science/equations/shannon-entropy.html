Shannon Entropy

Equation

$$ H(X) = -\sum_x p_x \log_2 p_x $$

Literal Reading

H of X equals minus sum sub x p of x log p of x.

Common Reading

Entropy measures uncertainty, disorder, or missing information in the relevant physical or informational state.

Symbols

\(H(X)\): the Shannon entropy of the random variable \(X\).

\(p_x\): the probability of outcome \(x\).

\(\sum_x\): sum over all possible outcomes.

\(\log_2\): logarithm base two, so the entropy is measured in bits.

Meaning of the Equation

Entropy is the expected information per symbol from a source. High entropy means lower predictability, so less compression is possible without losing information.
