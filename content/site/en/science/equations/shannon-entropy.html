Shannon Entropy

Equation

$$ H(X) = -\sum_x p_x \log_2 p_x $$

What the Formula Says

H of X equals minus the sum over each outcome of the probability of that outcome times the base two logarithm of that probability.

What It Means in Practice

This means the average uncertainty, measured in bits, in a classical random variable.

Symbols

\(H(X)\): the Shannon entropy of the random variable \(X\).

\(p_x\): the probability of outcome \(x\).

\(\sum_x\): sum over all possible outcomes.

\(\log_2\): logarithm base two, so the entropy is measured in bits.

Why It Matters

Shannon entropy measures how much information is missing before a classical outcome is known. Quantum information keeps this idea, but replaces probability distributions with density matrices.
