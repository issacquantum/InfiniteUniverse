Shannon Entropy

Equation

$$ H(X) = -\sum_x p_x \log_2 p_x $$

Literal Reading

Read it as: h of X equals minus the sum over each outcome of the probability of that outcome times the base two logarithm of that probability.

Common Reading

Entropy measures average uncertainty in a source.

Symbols

\(H(X)\): the Shannon entropy of the random variable \(X\).

\(p_x\): the probability of outcome \(x\).

\(\sum_x\): sum over all possible outcomes.

\(\log_2\): logarithm base two, so the entropy is measured in bits.

Meaning of the Equation

This relation measures uncertainty, correlation, capacity, or distinguishability. It tells how much information is present, lost, shared, or recoverable, so the concept becomes a quantity that can be compared rather than just described in words.
