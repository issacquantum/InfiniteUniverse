Hydrogen 3d₍z²₎ Wavefunction

Equation

$$ \psi_{320}(r,\theta,\phi) = \frac{1}{81\sqrt{6\pi}}\, r^2\, e^{-r/3}\,(3\cos^2\theta - 1) $$

What the Formula Says

Psi three two zero of r, theta, phi equals one over eighty one square root six pi, times r squared, times e to the minus r over three, times three cosine squared theta minus one.

What It Means in Practice

This means the real hydrogen 3d z squared stationary eigenfunction, labeled by the quantum numbers n equals 3, l equals 2, m equals 0.

Symbols

\(\psi_{320}\): the hydrogen wavefunction for the state \(n=3,\ell=2,m=0\).

\(r^2 e^{-r/3}\): the radial part, which sets how the state changes with distance.

\(3\cos^2\theta - 1\): the angular factor that creates the two lobes and the torus.

\(1/(81\sqrt{6\pi})\): the normalization prefactor for this state.

Why It Matters

This is the exact wavefunction being used for your current orbital model. Its radial and angular factors together generate the visible \(3d_{z^2}\) structure.
