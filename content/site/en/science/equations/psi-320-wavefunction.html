Hydrogen 3d₍z²₎ Wavefunction

Equation

$$ \psi_{320}(r,\theta,\phi) = \frac{1}{81\sqrt{6\pi}}\, r^2\, e^{-r/3}\,(3\cos^2\theta - 1) $$

Literal Reading

psi sub 320 of r comma theta comma phi proportional to r to the power of 2 e to the power of minus r over of 3a0 of 3cos to the power of 2 theta minus 1.

Common Reading

This gives the radial and angular shape of the hydrogen \(3d_{z^2}\)-type orbital, up to normalization.

Symbols

\(\psi_{320}\): the hydrogen wavefunction for the state \(n=3,\ell=2,m=0\).

\(r^2 e^{-r/3}\): the radial part, which sets how the state changes with distance.

\(3\cos^2\theta - 1\): the angular factor that creates the two lobes and the torus.

\(1/(81\sqrt{6\pi})\): the normalization prefactor for this state.

Meaning of the Equation

This wavefunction is the signed probability-amplitude shape for the hydrogen 3d z-squared state. Its nodes and angular structure determine the probability density seen in the orbital model.
