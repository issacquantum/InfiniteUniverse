Proton-Proton Chain Reaction

Equation

$$4\,{}^{1}\mathrm{H} \rightarrow {}^{4}\mathrm{He} + 2e^{+} + 2\nu_e + 2\gamma$$

Literal Reading

Read it as: four hydrogen one goes to helium four plus two positrons plus two electron neutrinos plus two photons.

Common Reading

Four hydrogen nuclei fuse into helium four, releasing positrons, electron neutrinos, and photons.

Symbols

\({}^{1}\mathrm{H}\): hydrogen-1 nucleus, a proton.

\({}^{4}\mathrm{He}\): helium-4 nucleus.

\(e^+\): positron.

\(\nu_e\): electron neutrino.

\(\gamma\): photon.

Meaning of the Equation

This relation connects microscopic structure, energy, probability, or reaction behavior to a measurable physical result. It shows which conditions control the process and what changes when temperature, energy, particles, or available states change.
