Proton-Proton Chain Reaction

Equation

$$ 4p\rightarrow{}^4\mathrm{He}+2e^++2\nu_e+\text{energy} $$

Literal Reading

Four protons produce helium four, two positrons, two electron neutrinos, and energy.

Common Reading

This is the net reaction of the proton-proton chain in stars.

Symbols

\(p\): proton or hydrogen nucleus.

\({}^{4}\mathrm{He}\): helium-4 nucleus.

\(e^+\): positron.

\(\nu_e\): electron neutrino.

energy: released binding energy.

Meaning of the Equation

Intermediate photons and steps occur inside the full chain; this net equation summarizes the overall conversion.
