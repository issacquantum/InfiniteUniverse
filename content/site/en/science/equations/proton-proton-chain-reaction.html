Proton-Proton Chain Reaction

Equation

$$4\,{}^{1}\mathrm{H} \rightarrow {}^{4}\mathrm{He} + 2e^{+} + 2\nu_e + 2\gamma$$

Literal Reading

4p goes to He minus 4 plus 2e to the power of plus plus 2 nu sub e plus energy.

Common Reading

Four hydrogen nuclei fuse into helium four, releasing positrons, electron neutrinos, and photons.

Symbols

\({}^{1}\mathrm{H}\): hydrogen-1 nucleus, a proton.

\({}^{4}\mathrm{He}\): helium-4 nucleus.

\(e^+\): positron.

\(\nu_e\): electron neutrino.

\(\gamma\): photon.

Meaning of the Equation

Nature selects the path whose action is stationary. This is a compact way to derive equations of motion.
