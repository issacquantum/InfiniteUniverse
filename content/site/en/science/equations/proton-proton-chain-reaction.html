Proton-Proton Chain Reaction

Equation

$$4\,{}^{1}\mathrm{H} \rightarrow {}^{4}\mathrm{He} + 2e^{+} + 2\nu_e + 2\gamma$$

What the Formula Says

Four hydrogen one goes to helium four plus two positrons plus two electron neutrinos plus two photons.

What It Means in Practice

This means four hydrogen nuclei fuse into helium four, releasing positrons, electron neutrinos, and photons.

Symbols

\({}^{1}\mathrm{H}\): hydrogen-1 nucleus, a proton.

\({}^{4}\mathrm{He}\): helium-4 nucleus.

\(e^+\): positron.

\(\nu_e\): electron neutrino.

\(\gamma\): photon.

Equation Meaning

This summarizes the dominant fusion chain in stars like the Sun. It shows how light nuclei combine into heavier nuclei and release energy, the process that powers ordinary stellar luminosity.
