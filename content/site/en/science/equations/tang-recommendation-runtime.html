Tang Recommendation Runtime

Equation

$$ \mathcal{O}(\mathrm{poly}(k)\log(mn)) $$

Literal Reading

Big O of a polynomial in \(k\) times the logarithm of \(mn\).

Physicist's Reading

A quantum information scientist would read this as a runtime that depends polynomially on the low-rank parameter but only logarithmically on the matrix dimensions, assuming the special sampling access model.

Symbol Breakdown

\(\mathcal{O}\): asymptotic scaling notation.

\(\mathrm{poly}(k)\): some polynomial function of the target rank \(k\).

\(k\): target low-rank dimension.

\(m,n\): dimensions of the data matrix.

\(\log(mn)\): logarithmic dependence on total matrix size.

Physical Meaning

This scaling is the force of Tang's result. It showed that, under comparable input assumptions, the claimed exponential quantum advantage for recommendation systems did not survive.
