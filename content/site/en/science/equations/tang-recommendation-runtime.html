Equation

$$ \mathcal{O}(\mathrm{poly}(k)\log(mn)) $$

Literal Reading

Read it as: big O of a polynomial in \(k\) times the logarithm of \(mn\).

Common Reading

Big O of a polynomial in \(k\) times the logarithm of \(mn\).

Symbols

\(\mathcal{O}\): asymptotic scaling notation.

\(\mathrm{poly}(k)\): some polynomial function of the target rank \(k\).

\(k\): target low-rank dimension.

\(m,n\): dimensions of the data matrix.

\(\log(mn)\): logarithmic dependence on total matrix size.

Meaning of the Equation

This relation turns an algorithmic step into a precise rule. It identifies what is being updated, counted, or bounded, so the process can be checked by its state, cost, error, or learning behavior instead of only by a visual animation.
