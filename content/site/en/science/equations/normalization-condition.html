Normalization Condition

Equation

$$ \int_{-\infty}^{\infty} |\Psi(\mathbf{r}, t)|^2 \, d^3r = 1 $$

Literal Reading

Read it as: the integral from minus infinity to infinity of absolute value of Psi of r, t squared, d cubed r, equals one.

Common Reading

The total probability of finding the particle somewhere in space must equal one.

Symbols

\(|\Psi|^2\): the probability density associated with the wavefunction.

\(d^3r\): the infinitesimal volume element in three-dimensional space.

\(1\): certainty that the particle exists somewhere in space.

Meaning of the Equation

This relation describes a quantum state, transformation, probability, or correlation. It matters because quantum systems are not described by one definite classical value; the equation tells what can be predicted from amplitudes, operators, or density matrices.
