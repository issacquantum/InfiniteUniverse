Normalization Condition

Equation

$$ \int_{-\infty}^{\infty} |\Psi(\mathbf{r}, t)|^2 \, d^3r = 1 $$

Literal Reading

The integral from minus infinity to infinity of absolute value of Psi of r, t squared, d cubed r, equals one.

Physicist's Reading

A physicist would read it as the total probability of finding the particle somewhere in space must equal one.

Symbol Breakdown

\(|\Psi|^2\): the probability density associated with the wavefunction.

\(d^3r\): the infinitesimal volume element in three-dimensional space.

\(1\): certainty that the particle exists somewhere in space.

Physical Meaning

A wavefunction only becomes physically meaningful when its total probability sums to one. Otherwise it is just an unscaled mathematical object.
