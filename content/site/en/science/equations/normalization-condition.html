Normalization Condition

Equation

$$ \int_{-\infty}^{\infty} |\Psi(\mathbf{r}, t)|^2 \, d^3r = 1 $$

Literal Reading

integral vertical bar psi open parenthesis x close parenthesis vertical bar to the power of 2 dx equals 1.

Common Reading

The total probability of finding the particle somewhere in space must equal one.

Symbols

\(|\Psi|^2\): the probability density associated with the wavefunction.

\(d^3r\): the infinitesimal volume element in three-dimensional space.

\(1\): certainty that the particle exists somewhere in space.

Meaning of the Equation

The total probability over all possible outcomes must equal one.
