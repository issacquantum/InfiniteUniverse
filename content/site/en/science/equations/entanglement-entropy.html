Entanglement Entropy

Equation

$$ S_A=-\operatorname{Tr}(\rho_A\log\rho_A),\qquad \rho_A=\operatorname{Tr}_B(\rho_{AB}) $$

Literal Reading

S sub A equals minus the trace of rho sub A log rho sub A, where rho sub A equals the trace over B of rho sub AB.

Common Reading

Entanglement entropy is the von Neumann entropy of a reduced state after tracing out the other subsystem.

Symbols

\(\rho_A\): the reduced density matrix for subsystem \(A\).

\(\rho_B\): the reduced density matrix for subsystem \(B\).

\(S\): von Neumann entropy.

Meaning of the Equation

For a pure joint state, this entropy measures how much subsystem A is entangled with subsystem B.
