Entanglement Entropy

Equation

$$ S(\rho_A) = S(\rho_B) $$

Literal Reading

The entropy of subsystem A equals the entropy of subsystem B.

Physicist's Reading

A physicist would read it as a pure bipartite entangled state gives both subsystems the same amount of reduced entropy.

Symbol Breakdown

\(\rho_A\): the reduced density matrix for subsystem \(A\).

\(\rho_B\): the reduced density matrix for subsystem \(B\).

\(S\): von Neumann entropy.

Physical Meaning

For a pure system split into two parts, the entropy of either part measures the same entanglement. The uncertainty is not in the whole state; it appears when each part is viewed alone.
