Eigenvalue Equation

Equation

$$A\mathbf{v}=\lambda\mathbf{v}$$

Literal Reading

A v equals lambda v.

Common Reading

Some special vectors keep their direction when a matrix acts on them; only their size changes.

Symbols

  • \(A\): the transformation matrix.
  • \(\mathbf{v}\): an eigenvector.
  • \(\lambda\): the eigenvalue, or scale factor.

Meaning of the Equation

Eigenvectors expose directions that the transformation does not rotate into another direction. The eigenvalue gives the multiplier along that direction, which is why these equations reveal stable modes, resonances, and measurable quantum values.
