Eigenvalue Equation

Equation

$$A\mathbf{v}=\lambda\mathbf{v}$$

Plain Meaning

Some special vectors keep their direction when a matrix acts on them; only their size changes.

Symbols

  • \(A\): the transformation matrix.
  • \(\mathbf{v}\): an eigenvector.
  • \(\lambda\): the eigenvalue, or scale factor.

How to Read It

Read it as a way to find the natural directions of a system. Those directions often reveal stable modes, resonances, energy levels, or important data axes.

What It Does Not Say

It does not say every vector is an eigenvector. Eigenvectors are special directions for a specific transformation.
