Trace Distance

Equation

$$ D(\rho,\sigma)=\frac{1}{2}\|\rho-\sigma\|_1 $$

Literal Reading

The trace distance between rho and sigma equals one half times the trace norm of rho minus sigma.

Physicist's Reading

A physicist would read it as the operational distinguishability between two quantum states.

Symbol Breakdown

\(D(\rho,\sigma)\): the trace distance between two states.

\(\rho,\sigma\): density matrices being compared.

\(\|\cdot\|_1\): the trace norm, equal to the sum of singular values.

\(\tfrac{1}{2}\): the normalization that keeps the distance between zero and one for quantum states.

Physical Meaning

Trace distance says how distinguishable two states are by the best possible measurement. Identical states have distance zero. Orthogonal states have distance one and can be perfectly told apart.
