Trace Distance

Equation

$$ D(\rho,\sigma)=\frac{1}{2}\|\rho-\sigma\|_1 $$

Literal Reading

Read it as: T open parenthesis rho comma sigma close parenthesis equals 1 over 2 norm rho minus sigma norm sub 1.

Common Reading

The operational distinguishability between two quantum states.

Symbols

\(D(\rho,\sigma)\): the trace distance between two states.

\(\rho,\sigma\): density matrices being compared.

\(\|\cdot\|_1\): the trace norm, equal to the sum of singular values.

\(\tfrac{1}{2}\): the normalization that keeps the distance between zero and one for quantum states.

Meaning of the Equation

Trace distance measures operational distinguishability between quantum states.
