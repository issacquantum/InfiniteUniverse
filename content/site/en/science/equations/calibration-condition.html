Calibration Condition

Equation

$$ P(\mathrm{correct}\mid \mathrm{confidence}=0.8)\approx 0.8 $$

Literal Reading

Read it as: P open parenthesis Y equals y vertical bar p equals y close parenthesis equals y.

Common Reading

When the model says 80 percent confidence many times, it should be right about 80 percent of those times.

Symbols

\(P(\mathrm{correct}\mid \mathrm{confidence}=0.8)\): the observed correctness rate among cases where the model reported 0.8 confidence.

\(\approx\): approximately equal, because calibration is measured statistically over many examples.

Meaning of the Equation

Predicted probabilities should match observed frequencies.
