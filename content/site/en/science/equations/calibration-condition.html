Calibration Condition

Equation

$$ P(\mathrm{correct}\mid \mathrm{confidence}=0.8)\approx 0.8 $$

Literal Reading

Read it as: the probability that the model is correct, given that it reported confidence 0.8, is approximately 0.8.

Common Reading

When the model says 80 percent confidence many times, it should be right about 80 percent of those times.

Symbols

\(P(\mathrm{correct}\mid \mathrm{confidence}=0.8)\): the observed correctness rate among cases where the model reported 0.8 confidence.

\(\approx\): approximately equal, because calibration is measured statistically over many examples.

Meaning of the Equation

Calibration checks whether confidence behaves like a reliable probability rather than just a persuasive number. A model can be accurate but poorly calibrated, or less accurate but honest about uncertainty. In real use, this matters because a confident wrong answer can be more dangerous than a visibly uncertain one.
