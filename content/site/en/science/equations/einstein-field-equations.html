Einstein Field Equations

Equation

$$G_{\mu\nu} = \frac{8\pi G}{c^4}T_{\mu\nu}$$

Literal Reading

G mu nu equals eight pi G over c to the fourth times T mu nu.

Physicist's Reading

A physicist reads this as spacetime curvature being related to the distribution of energy, momentum, pressure, and stress.

Symbol Breakdown

\(G_{\mu\nu}\): Einstein tensor, encoding curvature.

\(T_{\mu\nu}\): stress energy tensor, encoding matter and energy content.

\(G\): Newton's gravitational constant.

\(c\): speed of light.

Physical Meaning

This is the central equation of general relativity. It replaces gravity as a force with gravity as curved geometry. The simplified form shown here omits the cosmological constant term.
