Gibbs Hamiltonian Sample Complexity

Equation

$$ S=\mathcal{O}\!\left(\frac{\log N}{(\beta\varepsilon)^2}\right) $$

Literal Reading

The number of samples \(S\) scales like log \(N\) divided by beta times epsilon squared.

Physicist's Reading

For the high-temperature learning setting, estimating the Hamiltonian requires more samples when accuracy is tighter or the effective temperature makes the signal weaker.

Symbol Breakdown

\(S\): number of samples or copies required.

\(\mathcal{O}\): asymptotic scaling notation.

\(N\): system-size parameter.

\(\beta\): inverse temperature.

\(\varepsilon\): target accuracy.

Physical Meaning

This expression summarizes sample efficiency. It says the learning problem can scale only logarithmically in system size in the stated regime, but becomes harder when \(\beta\varepsilon\) is small.
