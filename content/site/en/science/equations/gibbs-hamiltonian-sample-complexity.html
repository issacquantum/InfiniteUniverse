Gibbs Hamiltonian Sample Complexity

Equation

$$ S=\mathcal{O}\!\left(\frac{\log N}{(\beta\varepsilon)^2}\right) $$

Literal Reading

Read it as: N equals O open parenthesis 1 over epsilon to the power of 2 close parenthesis.

Common Reading

The number of samples \(S\) scales like log \(N\) divided by beta times epsilon squared.

Symbols

\(S\): number of samples or copies required.

\(\mathcal{O}\): asymptotic scaling notation.

\(N\): system-size parameter.

\(\beta\): inverse temperature.

\(\varepsilon\): target accuracy.

Meaning of the Equation

It connects the quantities in the formula so the relation can be used to compute or interpret a situation in Quantum Computing.
