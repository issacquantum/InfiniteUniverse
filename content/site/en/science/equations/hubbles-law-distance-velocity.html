Hubble's Law

Equation

$$v = H_0 d$$

Literal Reading

v equals H0 d.

Common Reading

Recession velocity equals the Hubble constant times distance.

Symbols

\(v\): recessional velocity.

\(H_0\): the present-day Hubble constant.

\(d\): distance to the galaxy or source.

Meaning of the Equation

For nearby galaxies in the smooth expansion regime, farther galaxies recede faster. This is evidence of expanding space, not ordinary motion through a static space.
