Hubble's Law

Equation

$$v = H_0 d$$

Literal Reading

v equals H naught times d.

Physicist's Reading

A physicist would read it as recessional velocity is proportional to distance, with proportionality constant H naught.

Symbol Breakdown

\(v\): recessional velocity.

\(H_0\): the present-day Hubble constant.

\(d\): distance to the galaxy or source.

Physical Meaning

This is the simplest expression of cosmic expansion. The farther away a galaxy is, the faster it appears to recede. The relation does not mean galaxies are flying through static space in the ordinary sense. It means spacetime itself is expanding.
