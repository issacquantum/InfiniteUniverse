Hubble's Law

Equation

$$v = H_0 d$$

Literal Reading

v equals H0 d.

Common Reading

Recession velocity equals the Hubble constant times distance.

Symbols

\(v\): recessional velocity.

\(H_0\): the present-day Hubble constant.

\(d\): distance to the galaxy or source.

Meaning of the Equation

\(v=H_0d\) is the local low-redshift Hubble-flow approximation, not a universal velocity law for arbitrary cosmological distances.
