Shor Algorithm Polynomial Runtime

Equation

$$ \mathcal{O}\!\left((\log N)^3\right) $$

Literal Reading

T of n equals polynomial of log N.

Common Reading

The asymptotic runtime scaling for Shor-type factoring in terms of the size of the integer being factored.

Symbols

\(\mathcal{O}\): big-O notation, which describes scaling behavior rather than exact runtime.

\(\log N\): the bit-length scale associated with the integer \(N\).

\((\log N)^3\): cubic growth in the logarithm of the problem size, which is polynomial in input length.

Meaning of the Equation

Gate complexity depends on implementation and arithmetic model; \(O((\log N)^3)\) is a simplified common scaling, not a hardware runtime guarantee.
