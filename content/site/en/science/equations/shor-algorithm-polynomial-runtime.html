Shor Algorithm Polynomial Runtime

Equation

$$ \mathcal{O}\!\left((\log N)^3\right) $$

What the Formula Says

Big O of log N cubed.

What It Means in Practice

This means the asymptotic runtime scaling for Shor-type factoring in terms of the size of the integer being factored.

Symbols

\(\mathcal{O}\): big-O notation, which describes scaling behavior rather than exact runtime.

\(\log N\): the bit-length scale associated with the integer \(N\).

\((\log N)^3\): cubic growth in the logarithm of the problem size, which is polynomial in input length.

Equation Meaning

This expression is what makes Shor's algorithm historically important. It shows that integer factoring can scale polynomially with input length on a quantum computer, in sharp contrast with the subexponential scaling of the best known classical methods.
