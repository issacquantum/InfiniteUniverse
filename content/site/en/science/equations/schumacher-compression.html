Schumacher Compression

Equation

$$ n\ \text{signals} \longrightarrow nS(\rho)\ \text{qubits} $$

What the Formula Says

n signals can be compressed into approximately n times S of rho qubits.

What It Means in Practice

This means the quantum source-coding theorem: a long stream from a known quantum source can be compressed at a rate given by the von Neumann entropy of its average state.

Symbols

\(n\): the number of emitted quantum signals.

\(\rho\): the source's average density matrix.

\(S(\rho)\): the von Neumann entropy of the source.

\(nS(\rho)\): the asymptotic number of qubits needed to preserve the source output with high fidelity.

Why It Matters

Schumacher compression is the quantum analog of Shannon compression. It does not compress an arbitrary single unknown qubit. It compresses long sequences from a known statistical source by keeping the typical quantum subspace and discarding redundant description.
