Schumacher Compression

Equation

$$ n\ \text{signals} \longrightarrow nS(\rho)\ \text{qubits} $$

Literal Reading

n signals can be compressed into approximately n times S of rho qubits.

Common Reading

Short version: The quantum source-coding theorem: a long stream from a known quantum source can be compressed at a rate given by the von Neumann entropy of its average state.

Symbols

\(n\): the number of emitted quantum signals.

\(\rho\): the source's average density matrix.

\(S(\rho)\): the von Neumann entropy of the source.

\(nS(\rho)\): the asymptotic number of qubits needed to preserve the source output with high fidelity.

Meaning of the Equation

The quantum source-coding theorem: a long stream from a known quantum source can be compressed at a rate given by the von Neumann entropy of its average state.
