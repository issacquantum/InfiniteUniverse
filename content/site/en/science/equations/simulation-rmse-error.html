Simulation RMSE (root mean square error)

Equation

$$ RMSE=\sqrt{\frac{1}{N}\sum_{i=1}^{N}(y_i-\hat{y}_i)^2} $$

Literal Reading

Read it as: root mean square error is the square root of the average squared difference between reference values and simulated values.

Common Reading

This gives one compact measure of how far a simulation is from the data or reference solution it claims to reproduce.

Symbols

\(y_i\): reference or measured value.

\(\hat{y}_i\): simulated value.

\(N\): number of compared points.

Meaning of the Equation

This relation turns an algorithmic step into a precise rule. It identifies what is being updated, counted, or bounded, so the process can be checked by its state, cost, error, or learning behavior instead of only by a visual animation.
