Planck Blackbody Law

Equation

$$ B(\nu, T) = \frac{2h\nu^3}{c^2} \cdot \frac{1}{e^{\frac{h\nu}{k_B T}} - 1} $$

Literal Reading

B of nu, T equals two h nu cubed over c squared, times one over e to the h nu over k sub B T minus one.

Common Reading

Short version: The blackbody spectral radiance is given by Planck's distribution in frequency space.

Symbols

\(B(\nu,T)\): the spectral radiance of the blackbody.

\(h\): Planck's constant.

\(\nu\): the radiation frequency.

\(c\): the speed of light.

\(k_B\): Boltzmann's constant.

\(e^{h\nu/(k_B T)}\): the exponential factor that suppresses high-frequency emission.

Meaning of the Equation

The blackbody spectral radiance is given by Planck's distribution in frequency space.
