Planck Blackbody Law

Equation

$$ B_\nu(\nu,T)=\frac{2h\nu^3}{c^2}\frac{1}{e^{h\nu/(k_BT)}-1} $$

Literal Reading

B sub nu of nu and T equals two h nu cubed over c squared times one over e to the h nu over k sub B T minus one.

Common Reading

This is the frequency form of Planck’s blackbody law.

Symbols

\(B_\nu(\nu,T)\): spectral radiance per unit frequency.

\(h\): Planck's constant.

\(\nu\): the radiation frequency.

\(c\): the speed of light.

\(k_B\): Boltzmann's constant.

\(e^{h\nu/(k_B T)}\): the exponential factor that suppresses high-frequency emission.

Meaning of the Equation

The frequency label \(B_\nu\) must not be mixed with the wavelength form \(B_\lambda\), which has a different formula.
