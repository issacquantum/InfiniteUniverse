Electron-Positron Pair Production

Equation

$$\gamma + \gamma \rightarrow e^- + e^+$$

Literal Reading

Gamma plus gamma goes to e minus plus e plus.

Common Reading

Short version: Two photons can produce an electron-positron pair when enough energy is available.

Symbols

\(\gamma\): photon.

\(e^-\): electron.

\(e^+\): positron.

\(\rightarrow\): transforms into or produces.

Meaning of the Equation

Two photons can produce an electron-positron pair when enough energy is available.
