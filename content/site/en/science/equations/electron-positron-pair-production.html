Electron-Positron Pair Production

Equation

$$\gamma + \gamma \rightarrow e^- + e^+$$

What the Formula Says

Gamma plus gamma goes to e minus plus e plus.

What It Means in Practice

This means two photons can produce an electron-positron pair when enough energy is available.

Symbols

\(\gamma\): photon.

\(e^-\): electron.

\(e^+\): positron.

\(\rightarrow\): transforms into or produces.

Equation Meaning

This reaction is a direct example of energy turning into matter. If the incoming photons carry enough energy, that energy can appear as the rest mass and motion of a particle-antiparticle pair.
