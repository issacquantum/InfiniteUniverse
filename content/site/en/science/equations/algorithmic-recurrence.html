Algorithmic Recurrence

Equation

$$ T(n)=aT(n/b)+f(n) $$

Literal Reading

The time for input size n equals a subproblems of size n over b plus the extra work f of n.

Physicist's Reading

This describes divide and conquer algorithms, where a problem is split into smaller pieces and then combined.

Symbol Breakdown

\(T(n)\): running time for size \(n\).

\(a\): number of subproblems.

\(n/b\): size of each subproblem.

\(f(n)\): work outside the recursive calls.

Physical Meaning

Recurrences expose the skeleton of recursive algorithms and make their scaling analyzable.
