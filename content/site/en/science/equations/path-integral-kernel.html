Path Integral Kernel

Equation

$$K(b,a)=\int\mathcal{D}x\,e^{iS[x]/\hbar}$$

What the Formula Says

K from a to b equals a sum over paths weighted by e to the i action over h bar.

What It Means in Practice

This means the amplitude for moving from one state or event to another by summing over histories.

Symbols

\(K(b,a)\): transition amplitude or kernel.

\(\mathcal{D}x\): formal integration over paths.

\(S[x]\): action of a path.

\(\hbar\): reduced Planck constant.

Equation Meaning

The path integral does not remove time in ordinary quantum mechanics. It rewrites evolution as interference among possible histories.
