Path Integral Kernel

Equation

$$K(b,a)=\int\mathcal{D}x\,e^{iS[x]/\hbar}$$

Literal Reading

Read it as: k from b comma a equals the integral over paths of e to the i action of x divided by h bar.

Common Reading

A quantum amplitude comes from adding contributions from possible histories.

Symbols

\(K(b,a)\): transition amplitude or kernel.

\(\mathcal{D}x\): formal integration over paths.

\(S[x]\): action of a path.

\(\hbar\): reduced Planck constant.

Meaning of the Equation

The kernel is the quantum amplitude for going from one event or state to another. Instead of choosing one classical path first, the formalism adds contributions from possible paths, with each contribution weighted by the action of that path.
