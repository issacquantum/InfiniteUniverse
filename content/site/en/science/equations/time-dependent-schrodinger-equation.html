Time-Dependent Schrödinger Equation

Equation

$$ i\hbar \frac{\partial}{\partial t}\Psi(\mathbf{r}, t) = \hat{H}\Psi(\mathbf{r}, t) $$

Literal Reading

Read it as: i h bar partial over partial t of Psi of r, t equals H hat times Psi of r, t.

Common Reading

The Hamiltonian drives how a quantum state changes in time.

Symbols

\(i\): the imaginary unit, required by the complex structure of quantum evolution.

\(\hbar\): the reduced Planck constant, which sets the quantum scale.

\(\partial/\partial t\): the partial derivative with respect to time.

\(\Psi(\mathbf{r},t)\): the wavefunction, which encodes the quantum state.

\(\hat{H}\): the Hamiltonian operator, representing the total energy of the system.

\(\mathbf{r}\): the spatial position vector.

\(t\): time.

Meaning of the Equation

This relation connects the mathematical state of a physical system to what can happen next or what can be measured. It is not just notation: it states how energy, fields, waves, or probabilities constrain the behavior of the system.
