Amplitude-Encoded Classical Vector

Equation

$$ |x\rangle=\frac{1}{\|x\|}\sum_i x_i|i\rangle $$

Literal Reading

Common Reading

Encoding a classical vector into quantum amplitudes.

Symbols

\(|x\rangle\): the normalized quantum state representing the classical vector \(x\).

\(x_i\): the \(i\)-th entry of the classical vector.

\(|i\rangle\): the computational-basis state labeled by index \(i\).

\(\sum_i\): the sum over all vector indices.

\(\|x\|\): the vector norm, included so the quantum state has total probability one.

Meaning of the Equation

Classical data are represented or sampled through quantum amplitudes and squared norms.
