Amplitude-Encoded Classical Vector

Equation

$$ |x\rangle=\frac{1}{\|x\|}\sum_i x_i|i\rangle $$

Literal Reading

The quantum state \( |x\rangle \) equals one over the norm of \(x\), times the sum over \(i\) of \(x_i\) multiplying basis state \( |i\rangle \).

Physicist's Reading

A physicist would read this as encoding a classical vector into quantum amplitudes. The entries of the vector become the coefficients of the computational-basis states.

Symbol Breakdown

\(|x\rangle\): the normalized quantum state representing the classical vector \(x\).

\(x_i\): the \(i\)-th entry of the classical vector.

\(|i\rangle\): the computational-basis state labeled by index \(i\).

\(\sum_i\): the sum over all vector indices.

\(\|x\|\): the vector norm, included so the quantum state has total probability one.

Physical Meaning

This equation is central to Tang's critique because the notation hides a cost. Loading classical data into amplitudes can be expensive, so a quantum speedup that assumes this state appears for free may be overstating the quantum advantage.
