Big O Notation

Equation

$$ f(n)=O(g(n)) $$

What the Formula Says

f of n is Big O of g of n.

What It Means in Practice

The growth of \(f(n)\) is bounded above by a constant multiple of \(g(n)\) for sufficiently large inputs.

Symbols

\(f(n)\): resource use being analyzed, such as time or memory.

\(g(n)\): comparison growth function.

\(n\): input size.

Why It Matters

Big O ignores small constant details and focuses on scalability. It asks what happens when the input becomes large.
