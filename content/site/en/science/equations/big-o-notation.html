Big O Notation

Equation

$$ f(n)=O(g(n)) $$

Literal Reading

Read it as: f of n is Big O of g of n.

Common Reading

The growth of \(f(n)\) is bounded above by a constant multiple of \(g(n)\) for sufficiently large inputs.

Symbols

\(f(n)\): resource use being analyzed, such as time or memory.

\(g(n)\): comparison growth function.

\(n\): input size.

Meaning of the Equation

This relation turns an algorithmic step into a precise rule. It identifies what is being updated, counted, or bounded, so the process can be checked by its state, cost, error, or learning behavior instead of only by a visual animation.
