Second Law Entropy Inequality

Equation

$$dS\geq \frac{\delta Q}{T}$$

Literal Reading

Read it as: dS is greater than or equal to heat added reversibly divided by temperature.

Common Reading

In an irreversible process, entropy increases by more than the reversible heat-flow part alone.

Symbols

  • \(dS\): entropy change.
  • \(\delta Q\): heat transferred.
  • \(T\): absolute temperature.

Meaning of the Equation

This relation measures uncertainty, correlation, capacity, or distinguishability. It tells how much information is present, lost, shared, or recoverable, so the concept becomes a quantity that can be compared rather than just described in words.
