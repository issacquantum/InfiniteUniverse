Second Law Entropy Inequality

Equation

$$ dS\ge \frac{\delta Q}{T} $$ $$ \Delta S_{\rm universe}\ge0 $$

Literal Reading

dS is greater than or equal to delta Q over T, and the change in entropy of the universe is greater than or equal to zero.

Common Reading

For an isolated total system, entropy does not decrease; for a heat-exchanging system, \(dS\ge\delta Q/T\) is the Clausius inequality.

Symbols

  • \(dS\): entropy change.
  • \(\delta Q\): heat transferred.
  • \(T\): absolute temperature.

Meaning of the Equation

The statement depends on what system boundary is being used: isolated total systems and heat-exchanging subsystems are described differently.
