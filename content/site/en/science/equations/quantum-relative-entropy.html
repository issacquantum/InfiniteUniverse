Quantum Relative Entropy

Equation

$$ S(\rho \,\|\, \sigma) = \operatorname{Tr}(\rho \log_2 \rho) - \operatorname{Tr}(\rho \log_2 \sigma) $$

Literal Reading

D of rho norm sigma equals trace of rho of log rho minus log sigma.

Common Reading

Entropy measures uncertainty, disorder, or missing information in the relevant physical or informational state.

Symbols

\(\rho\): the state being compared.

\(\sigma\): the reference state.

\(\operatorname{Tr}\): trace operation.

\(\log_2\): logarithm base two.

Meaning of the Equation

It measures how distinguishable one probability or quantum state is from another.
