Quantum Relative Entropy

Equation

$$ S(\rho \,\|\, \sigma) = \operatorname{Tr}(\rho \log_2 \rho) - \operatorname{Tr}(\rho \log_2 \sigma) $$

What the Formula Says

The relative entropy of rho with respect to sigma equals the trace of rho log rho minus the trace of rho log sigma.

What It Means in Practice

This means a measure of how distinguishable the state \(\rho\) is from the reference state \(\sigma\).

Symbols

\(\rho\): the state being compared.

\(\sigma\): the reference state.

\(\operatorname{Tr}\): trace operation.

\(\log_2\): logarithm base two.

Why It Matters

Quantum relative entropy is central to distinguishability, thermodynamics, and information processing. It is not a simple geometric distance, but it tells how costly it is to mistake one quantum state for another in an informational sense.
