Quantum Relative Entropy

Equation

$$ S(\rho \,\|\, \sigma) = \operatorname{Tr}(\rho \log_2 \rho) - \operatorname{Tr}(\rho \log_2 \sigma) $$

Literal Reading

The relative entropy of rho with respect to sigma equals the trace of rho log rho minus the trace of rho log sigma.

Common Reading

Short version: A measure of how distinguishable the state \(\rho\) is from the reference state \(\sigma\).

Symbols

\(\rho\): the state being compared.

\(\sigma\): the reference state.

\(\operatorname{Tr}\): trace operation.

\(\log_2\): logarithm base two.

Meaning of the Equation

A measure of how distinguishable the state \(\rho\) is from the reference state \(\sigma\).
