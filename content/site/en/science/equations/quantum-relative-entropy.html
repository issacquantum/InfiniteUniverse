Quantum Relative Entropy

Equation

$$ D(\rho \,\|\, \sigma) = \operatorname{Tr}(\rho \log_2 \rho) - \operatorname{Tr}(\rho \log_2 \sigma) $$

Literal Reading

D of rho relative to sigma equals trace of rho log base two rho minus trace of rho log base two sigma.

Common Reading

Quantum relative entropy is a divergence or distinguishability measure between quantum states.

Symbols

\(D\): quantum relative entropy.

\(\rho\): the state being compared.

\(\sigma\): the reference state.

\(\operatorname{Tr}\): trace operation.

\(\log_2\): logarithm base two.

Meaning of the Equation

It is not symmetric and is not a metric, so it should not be described as an ordinary distance.
