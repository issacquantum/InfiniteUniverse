Gravitational Redshift

Equation

$$\frac{\Delta f}{f} = \frac{\Delta\Phi}{c^2}$$

What the Formula Says

Delta f over f equals delta Phi over c squared.

What It Means in Practice

This means the fractional frequency shift caused by a difference in gravitational potential in the weak field limit.

Symbols

\(\Delta f\): change in measured frequency.

\(f\): reference frequency.

\(\Delta\Phi\): difference in gravitational potential.

\(c\): speed of light.

Why It Matters

Light climbing out of a gravitational field loses frequency, while light falling deeper gains frequency. This effect is measured in laboratory experiments, astronomical observations, and precision timing systems.
