Gravitational Redshift

Equation

$$\frac{\Delta f}{f} = \frac{\Delta\Phi}{c^2}$$

Literal Reading

Read it as: delta f over f equals delta Phi over c squared.

Common Reading

The fractional frequency shift caused by a difference in gravitational potential in the weak field limit.

Symbols

\(\Delta f\): change in measured frequency.

\(f\): reference frequency.

\(\Delta\Phi\): difference in gravitational potential.

\(c\): speed of light.

Meaning of the Equation

This relation connects geometry, motion, horizons, or gravitational sources. It tells what clocks, distances, curvature, or observers would measure, so the physical claim is tied to spacetime structure rather than to a picture or metaphor.
