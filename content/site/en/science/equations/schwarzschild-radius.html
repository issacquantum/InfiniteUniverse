Schwarzschild Radius

Equation

$$r_s = \frac{2GM}{c^2}$$

What the Formula Says

r sub s equals two G M divided by c squared.

What It Means in Practice

This means the horizon radius of a non rotating uncharged black hole.

Symbols

\(r_s\): Schwarzschild radius.

\(G\): Newton's gravitational constant.

\(M\): black hole mass.

\(c\): speed of light.

Why It Matters

If a mass is compressed inside this radius, the event horizon forms. The equation shows that horizon size grows directly with mass.

Scope and Limits

This radius applies to the ideal Schwarzschild solution: spherical, nonrotating, uncharged, and isolated. Real astrophysical black holes rotate, so this is the clean baseline rather than the whole story.
