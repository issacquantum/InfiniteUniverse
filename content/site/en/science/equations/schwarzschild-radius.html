Schwarzschild Radius

Equation

$$r_s = \frac{2GM}{c^2}$$

Literal Reading

r sub s equals 2GM over c to the power of 2.

Common Reading

The Schwarzschild radius marks the horizon size for a non-rotating black hole.

Symbols

\(r_s\): Schwarzschild radius.

\(G\): Newton's gravitational constant.

\(M\): black hole mass.

\(c\): speed of light.

Meaning of the Equation

Compressing mass inside this radius creates a black-hole horizon.

Scope and Limits

This radius applies to the ideal Schwarzschild solution: spherical, nonrotating, uncharged, and isolated. Real astrophysical black holes rotate, so this is the clean baseline rather than the whole story.
