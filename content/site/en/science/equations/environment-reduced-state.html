Environment Reduced State

Equation

$$ \rho_S=\operatorname{Tr}_E(\rho_{SE}) $$

Literal Reading

The reduced state of system S equals the trace over the environment E of the joint state of system plus environment.

Common Reading

Short version: The rule for describing what remains locally accessible when a system is entangled with an environment that is not being tracked in full detail.

Symbols

\(\rho_S\): the reduced density matrix of the system.

\(\rho_{SE}\): the joint density matrix of the system and its environment.

\(\operatorname{Tr}_E\): the partial trace over environmental degrees of freedom.

Meaning of the Equation

The rule for describing what remains locally accessible when a system is entangled with an environment that is not being tracked in full detail.
