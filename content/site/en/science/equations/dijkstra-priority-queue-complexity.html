Dijkstra Priority Queue Complexity

Equation

$$ O((V+E)\log V) $$

What the Formula Says

The runtime grows with vertices plus edges, multiplied by the logarithm of the vertex count.

What It Means in Practice

Using a priority queue makes shortest-path exploration scale with graph size while paying a logarithmic cost for ordering candidates.

Symbols

\(V\): number of vertices or nodes.

\(E\): number of edges.

\(\log V\): cost of priority queue operations in common heap implementations.

Equation Meaning

Dijkstra's algorithm is efficient when edge weights are nonnegative. The priority queue keeps attention on the next closest unsettled node.
