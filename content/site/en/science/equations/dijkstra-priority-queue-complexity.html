Dijkstra Priority Queue Complexity

Equation

$$ O((V+E)\log V) $$

Literal Reading

Read it as: the runtime grows with vertices plus edges, multiplied by the logarithm of the vertex count.

Common Reading

Using a priority queue makes shortest-path exploration scale with graph size while paying a logarithmic cost for ordering candidates.

Symbols

\(V\): number of vertices or nodes.

\(E\): number of edges.

\(\log V\): cost of priority queue operations in common heap implementations.

Meaning of the Equation

This relation turns an algorithmic step into a precise rule. It identifies what is being updated, counted, or bounded, so the process can be checked by its state, cost, error, or learning behavior instead of only by a visual animation.
