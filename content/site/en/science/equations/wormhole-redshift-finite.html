Finite Redshift Condition

Equation

$$ |\Phi(r)|<\infty $$

Literal Reading

Read it as: Φ open parenthesis r close parenthesis finite everywhere.

Common Reading

If \(\Phi(r)\) diverges or makes \(e^{2\Phi(r)}\) vanish, the geometry behaves like an event horizon instead of a traversable passage.

Symbols

\(\Phi(r)\): redshift function in the Morris-Thorne metric.

\(e^{2\Phi(r)}\): factor controlling gravitational time dilation in the metric.

Meaning of the Equation

Light climbing out of gravity loses frequency and is redshifted.
