Finite Redshift Condition

Equation

$$ |\Phi(r)|<\infty $$

What the Formula Says

The redshift function must remain finite everywhere relevant to the throat and passage.

What It Means in Practice

If \(\Phi(r)\) diverges or makes \(e^{2\Phi(r)}\) vanish, the geometry behaves like an event horizon instead of a traversable passage.

Symbols

\(\Phi(r)\): redshift function in the Morris-Thorne metric.

\(e^{2\Phi(r)}\): factor controlling gravitational time dilation in the metric.

Equation Meaning

A traversable wormhole cannot hide the throat behind a horizon. The redshift must stay controlled so signals and travelers can pass both ways.
