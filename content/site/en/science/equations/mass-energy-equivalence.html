Mass-Energy Equivalence

Equation

$$E = mc^2$$

What the Formula Says

E equals m c squared.

What It Means in Practice

This means energy equals mass times the speed of light squared.

Symbols

\(E\): energy.

\(m\): mass.

\(c\): speed of light in vacuum.

Why It Matters

This equation says mass is a concentrated form of energy. Because \(c^2\) is enormous, even a small amount of mass corresponds to a very large amount of energy. It is central to nuclear physics, particle creation, and stellar energy generation.
