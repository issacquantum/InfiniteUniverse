Mass-Energy Equivalence

Equation

$$E = mc^2$$

Literal Reading

Read it as: E equals m c to the power of 2.

Common Reading

Energy equals mass multiplied by the speed of light squared.

Symbols

\(E\): energy.

\(m\): mass.

\(c\): speed of light in vacuum.

Meaning of the Equation

A small amount of mass corresponds to an enormous amount of energy because the conversion factor is \(c^2\), the speed of light squared. The equation does not mean ordinary objects automatically release all of that energy; it states the rest-energy associated with mass.
