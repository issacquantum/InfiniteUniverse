Wormhole Flare-Out Condition

Equation

$$ b(r_0)=r_0,\qquad b'(r_0)<1 $$

Literal Reading

At the throat radius \(r_0\), the shape function equals the radius, and its slope must be less than one.

Physicist's Reading

This says the spatial geometry opens outward at the throat instead of pinching shut.

Symbol Breakdown

\(b(r)\): shape function controlling the wormhole's radial geometry.

\(r_0\): throat radius, the narrowest allowed radius.

\(b'(r_0)\): slope of the shape function at the throat.

Physical Meaning

The flare-out condition is one reason traversable wormholes require exotic stress energy. Ordinary positive-energy matter tends to focus geodesics instead of holding a throat open.
