Page-Wootters Stationary State

Equation

$$H_{\mathrm{tot}}|\Psi\rangle = 0,\qquad |\psi(t)\rangle \propto \langle t|\Psi\rangle$$

Literal Reading

Read it as: the total Hamiltonian annihilates the universal state, while a subsystem state is obtained by conditioning on a clock reading.

Common Reading

A relational time construction: the whole can be stationary while subsystems show evolution through correlations.

Symbols

\(H_{\mathrm{tot}}\): total Hamiltonian.

\(|\Psi\rangle\): global state.

\(|t\rangle\): idealized clock state.

\(|\psi(t)\rangle\): conditional subsystem state.

Meaning of the Equation

This relation describes a quantum state, transformation, probability, or correlation. It matters because quantum systems are not described by one definite classical value; the equation tells what can be predicted from amplitudes, operators, or density matrices.
