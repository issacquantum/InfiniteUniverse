Page-Wootters Stationary State

Equation

$$H_{\mathrm{tot}}|\Psi\rangle = 0,\qquad |\psi(t)\rangle \propto \langle t|\Psi\rangle$$

What the Formula Says

The total Hamiltonian annihilates the universal state, while a subsystem state is obtained by conditioning on a clock reading.

What It Means in Practice

This means a relational time construction: the whole can be stationary while subsystems show evolution through correlations.

Symbols

\(H_{\mathrm{tot}}\): total Hamiltonian.

\(|\Psi\rangle\): global state.

\(|t\rangle\): idealized clock state.

\(|\psi(t)\rangle\): conditional subsystem state.

Why It Matters

The Page-Wootters idea shows how time can be reconstructed from internal correlations, but it depends on idealized clock and factorization assumptions.
