Page-Wootters Stationary State

Equation

$$H_{\mathrm{tot}}|\Psi\rangle = 0,\qquad |\psi(t)\rangle \propto \langle t|\Psi\rangle$$

Literal Reading

the total Hamiltonian acting on Psi equals zero, and the subsystem state at t is proportional to the projection of Psi onto the clock state t.

Common Reading

A relational time construction: the whole can be stationary while subsystems show evolution through correlations.

Symbols

\(H_{\mathrm{tot}}\): total Hamiltonian.

\(|\Psi\rangle\): global state.

\(|t\rangle\): idealized clock state.

\(|\psi(t)\rangle\): conditional subsystem state.

Meaning of the Equation

The construction shows how subsystem time can arise by conditioning a stationary total state on a clock reading. The full equation has no external time parameter, but correlations between the clock and the rest of the system can reproduce effective evolution.
