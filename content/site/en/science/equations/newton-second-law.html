Newton's Second Law

Equation

$$\mathbf{F}=m\mathbf{a}$$

Literal Reading

F equals m times a.

Common Reading

The net force acting on an object equals its mass times its acceleration.

Symbols

  • \(\mathbf{F}\): total force on the object.
  • \(m\): mass.
  • \(\mathbf{a}\): acceleration.

Meaning of the Equation

Acceleration is the motion response produced by the total imbalance of forces. The same push changes a light object more than a heavy one because mass resists acceleration.
