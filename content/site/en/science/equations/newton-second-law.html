Newton's Second Law

Equation

$$\mathbf{F}=m\mathbf{a}$$

Literal Reading

Net force equals mass times acceleration.

Physicist's Reading

A force changes motion. In classical mechanics, this turns physical causes into equations that predict trajectories.

Symbol Breakdown

\(\mathbf{F}\): net force vector.

\(m\): inertial mass.

\(\mathbf{a}\): acceleration vector.

Physical Meaning

The same force produces less acceleration on a larger mass. The equation works in the classical domain: speeds far below light and systems large compared with quantum scales.
