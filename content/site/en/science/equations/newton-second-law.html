Newton's Second Law

Equation

$$\mathbf{F}=m\mathbf{a}$$

Plain Meaning

Force is what changes motion. More force gives more acceleration; more mass resists acceleration.

Symbols

  • \(\mathbf{F}\): net force on the object.
  • \(m\): mass.
  • \(\mathbf{a}\): acceleration.

How to Read It

Use the net force, not one individual force. If all forces cancel, acceleration is zero even if forces are present.

Meaning in Context

The equation means net force controls acceleration in classical mechanics. Relativistic, quantum, or variable-mass contexts require the appropriate extended model.
