Newton's Second Law

Equation

$$\mathbf{F}=m\mathbf{a}$$

Plain Meaning

Force is what changes motion. More force gives more acceleration; more mass resists acceleration.

Symbols

  • \(\mathbf{F}\): net force on the object.
  • \(m\): mass.
  • \(\mathbf{a}\): acceleration.

How to Read It

Use the net force, not one individual force. If all forces cancel, acceleration is zero even if forces are present.

What It Does Not Say

It does not apply unchanged at relativistic speeds, quantum scales, or in situations where mass changes with the system being modeled.
