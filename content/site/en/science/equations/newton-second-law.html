Newton's Second Law

Equation

$$\mathbf{F}=m\mathbf{a}$$

Literal Reading

Read it as: vector F equals mass m times vector a.

Common Reading

Force equals mass times acceleration.

Symbols

  • \(\mathbf{F}\): total force on the object.
  • \(m\): mass.
  • \(\mathbf{a}\): acceleration.

Meaning of the Equation

In ordinary classical mechanics, the total push or pull on an object determines how quickly its velocity changes. A stronger force changes motion more strongly; a larger mass makes the same force produce less acceleration.
