Escape Velocity

Equation

$$v_e = \sqrt{\frac{2GM}{r}}$$

Literal Reading

Escape velocity equals the square root of two G M divided by r.

Physicist's Reading

A physicist reads this as the minimum speed needed to escape a spherical gravitating body without further propulsion.

Symbol Breakdown

\(v_e\): escape velocity.

\(G\): Newton's gravitational constant.

\(M\): mass of the gravitating object.

\(r\): distance from the object's center.

Physical Meaning

This is the Newtonian doorway into the black hole idea. If the escape speed reaches light speed at a certain radius, no ordinary signal can escape from inside that radius.

Scope and Limit

The equation is Newtonian, so it is best used as intuition. General relativity replaces escape-speed language with horizon geometry and causal structure.
