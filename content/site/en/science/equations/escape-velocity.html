Escape Velocity

Equation

$$v_e = \sqrt{\frac{2GM}{r}}$$

Literal Reading

Read it as: escape velocity equals the square root of two G M divided by r.

Common Reading

The minimum speed needed to escape a spherical gravitating body without further propulsion.

Symbols

\(v_e\): escape velocity.

\(G\): Newton's gravitational constant.

\(M\): mass of the gravitating object.

\(r\): distance from the object's center.

Meaning of the Equation

This relation connects geometry, motion, horizons, or gravitational sources. It tells what clocks, distances, curvature, or observers would measure, so the physical claim is tied to spacetime structure rather than to a picture or metaphor.

Scope and Limits

The equation is Newtonian, so it is best used as intuition. General relativity replaces escape-speed language with horizon geometry and causal structure.
