Escape Velocity

Equation

$$v_e = \sqrt{\frac{2GM}{r}}$$

Literal Reading

v sub esc equals square root of 2GM over r.

Common Reading

The minimum speed needed to escape a spherical gravitating body without further propulsion.

Symbols

\(v_e\): escape velocity.

\(G\): Newton's gravitational constant.

\(M\): mass of the gravitating object.

\(r\): distance from the object's center.

Meaning of the Equation

Escape speed is not about reaching a height; it is about total mechanical energy becoming zero or positive. At that speed, an object can coast away forever in the ideal no-drag, no-thrust case.

Scope and Limits

The equation is Newtonian, so it is best used as intuition. General relativity replaces escape-speed language with horizon geometry and causal structure.
