Unitary Gate Condition

Equation

$$ U^\dagger U = I $$

What the Formula Says

U dagger times U equals the identity operator.

What It Means in Practice

This means the condition that an ideal quantum gate preserves inner products, amplitudes, and total probability.

Symbols

\(U\): the quantum gate or circuit operation.

\(U^\dagger\): the conjugate transpose of \(U\), which reverses the operation for a unitary gate.

\(I\): the identity operator, meaning no probability is lost or gained.

Equation Meaning

Unitary gates make quantum evolution reversible until measurement occurs. They can redistribute amplitude and phase, but they cannot change the total norm of the state. That is why interference can be engineered without violating probability conservation.
