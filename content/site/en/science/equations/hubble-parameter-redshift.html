Hubble Parameter and Redshift

Equation

$$ H(z)=H_0\sqrt{\Omega_r(1+z)^4+\Omega_m(1+z)^3+\Omega_k(1+z)^2+\Omega_\Lambda} $$

Literal Reading

H of z equals H naught times the square root of omega r times one plus z to the fourth, plus omega m times one plus z cubed, plus omega k times one plus z squared, plus omega Lambda.

Common Reading

\(H(z)\) gives the cosmic expansion rate at redshift \(z\).

Symbols

\(H(z)\): expansion rate at redshift \(z\).

\(H_0\): present-day Hubble constant.

\(\Omega_r\): radiation density parameter.

\(\Omega_m\): matter density parameter.

\(\Omega_k\): curvature density parameter.

\(\Omega_\Lambda\): dark-energy density parameter.

\(z\): redshift.

Meaning of the Equation

Radiation, matter, curvature, and dark energy scale differently with \(1+z\) in an FLRW model.
