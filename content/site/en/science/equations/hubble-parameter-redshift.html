Hubble Parameter and Redshift

Equation

$$H(z) = H_0 \sqrt{\Omega_m (1+z)^3 + \Omega_r (1+z)^4 + \Omega_\Lambda}$$

Literal Reading

H of z equals H0 square root of omega sub m of 1 plus z to the power of 3 plus omega sub r of 1 plus z to the power of 4 plus omega sub Lambda plus omega sub k of 1 plus z to the power of 2.

Common Reading

The Hubble parameter at redshift z depends on the matter, radiation, and dark-energy density fractions.

Symbols

\(H(z)\): expansion rate at redshift \(z\).

\(H_0\): present-day Hubble constant.

\(\Omega_m\): matter density parameter.

\(\Omega_r\): radiation density parameter.

\(\Omega_\Lambda\): dark-energy density parameter.

\(z\): redshift.

Meaning of the Equation

Redshift acts as a label for cosmic scale factor history. The equation lets different eras be compared because radiation, matter, curvature, and dark energy scale differently.
