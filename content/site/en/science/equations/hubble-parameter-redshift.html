Hubble Parameter and Redshift

Equation

$$H(z) = H_0 \sqrt{\Omega_m (1+z)^3 + \Omega_r (1+z)^4 + \Omega_\Lambda}$$

What the Formula Says

H of z equals H naught times the square root of omega m times one plus z cubed plus omega r times one plus z to the fourth plus omega lambda.

What It Means in Practice

This means the Hubble parameter at redshift z depends on the matter, radiation, and dark-energy density fractions.

Symbols

\(H(z)\): expansion rate at redshift \(z\).

\(H_0\): present-day Hubble constant.

\(\Omega_m\): matter density parameter.

\(\Omega_r\): radiation density parameter.

\(\Omega_\Lambda\): dark-energy density parameter.

\(z\): redshift.

Why It Matters

This equation tells us how the cosmic expansion rate changes across history. The powers of \(1+z\) show that radiation dominated the earliest universe, matter dominated later, and dark energy dominates at late times.
