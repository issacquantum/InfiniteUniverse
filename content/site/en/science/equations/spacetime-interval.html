Spacetime Interval

Equation

$$ds^2 = \eta_{\mu\nu}\,dx^\mu dx^\nu$$

Literal Reading

Read it as: s to the power of 2 equals minus c to the power of 2t to the power of 2 plus x to the power of 2 plus y to the power of 2 plus z to the power of 2.

Common Reading

The spacetime interval stays invariant between reference frames.

Symbols

\(ds^2\): infinitesimal spacetime interval.

\(\eta_{\mu\nu}\): Minkowski metric tensor.

\(dx^\mu\) and \(dx^\nu\): infinitesimal coordinate differences.

\(\mu,\nu\): spacetime indices summed over by convention.

Meaning of the Equation

The spacetime interval classifies separations and remains invariant between observers.
