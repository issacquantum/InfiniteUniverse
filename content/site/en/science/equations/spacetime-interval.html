Spacetime Interval

Equation

$$ds^2 = \eta_{\mu\nu}\,dx^\mu dx^\nu$$

Literal Reading

d s squared equals eta mu nu times d x mu times d x nu.

Common Reading

Short version: The invariant separation between nearby events in flat spacetime.

Symbols

\(ds^2\): infinitesimal spacetime interval.

\(\eta_{\mu\nu}\): Minkowski metric tensor.

\(dx^\mu\) and \(dx^\nu\): infinitesimal coordinate differences.

\(\mu,\nu\): spacetime indices summed over by convention.

Meaning of the Equation

The invariant separation between nearby events in flat spacetime.
