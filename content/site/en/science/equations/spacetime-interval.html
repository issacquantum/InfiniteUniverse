Spacetime Interval

Equation

$$ds^2 = \eta_{\mu\nu}\,dx^\mu dx^\nu$$

Literal Reading

Read it as: d s squared equals eta mu nu times d x mu times d x nu.

Common Reading

The spacetime interval stays invariant between reference frames.

Symbols

\(ds^2\): infinitesimal spacetime interval.

\(\eta_{\mu\nu}\): Minkowski metric tensor.

\(dx^\mu\) and \(dx^\nu\): infinitesimal coordinate differences.

\(\mu,\nu\): spacetime indices summed over by convention.

Meaning of the Equation

This relation connects geometry, motion, horizons, or gravitational sources. It tells what clocks, distances, curvature, or observers would measure, so the physical claim is tied to spacetime structure rather than to a picture or metaphor.
