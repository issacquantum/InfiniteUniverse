Gibbs State Density Matrix

Equation

$$ \rho_\beta=\frac{e^{-\beta H}}{Z},\qquad Z=\operatorname{Tr}(e^{-\beta H}),\qquad \beta=\frac{1}{k_B T} $$

Literal Reading

rho sub beta equals e to the minus beta H divided by Z, where Z equals the trace of e to the minus beta H and beta equals one over k sub B T.

Common Reading

The density matrix for a quantum system in thermal equilibrium at temperature \(T\).

Symbols

\(\rho_\beta\): thermal density matrix.

\(H\): Hamiltonian of the system.

\(\beta\): inverse temperature parameter.

\(Z\): partition function, which normalizes the state.

\(\mathrm{Tr}\): trace operation.

\(k_B\): Boltzmann constant.

\(T\): temperature.

Meaning of the Equation

The partition function \(Z\) normalizes the density matrix so its trace is one, and \(\beta\) sets the temperature scale.
