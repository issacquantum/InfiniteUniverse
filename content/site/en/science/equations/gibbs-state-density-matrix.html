Gibbs State Density Matrix

Equation

$$ \rho_\beta=\frac{e^{-\beta H}}{Z},\qquad Z=\mathrm{Tr}(e^{-\beta H}),\qquad \beta=\frac{1}{k_BT} $$

Literal Reading

Read it as: the Gibbs state \(\rho_\beta\) equals the exponential of negative beta times the Hamiltonian divided by \(Z\), where \(Z\) is the trace of that exponential and \(\beta\) is inverse thermal energy.

Common Reading

The density matrix for a quantum system in thermal equilibrium at temperature \(T\).

Symbols

\(\rho_\beta\): thermal density matrix.

\(H\): Hamiltonian of the system.

\(\beta\): inverse temperature parameter.

\(Z\): partition function, which normalizes the state.

\(\mathrm{Tr}\): trace operation.

\(k_B\): Boltzmann constant.

\(T\): temperature.

Meaning of the Equation

This relation describes a quantum state, transformation, probability, or correlation. It matters because quantum systems are not described by one definite classical value; the equation tells what can be predicted from amplitudes, operators, or density matrices.
