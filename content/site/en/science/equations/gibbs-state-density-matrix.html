Gibbs State Density Matrix

Equation

$$ \rho_\beta=\frac{e^{-\beta H}}{Z},\qquad Z=\mathrm{Tr}(e^{-\beta H}),\qquad \beta=\frac{1}{k_BT} $$

Literal Reading

The Gibbs state \(\rho_\beta\) equals the exponential of negative beta times the Hamiltonian divided by \(Z\), where \(Z\) is the trace of that exponential and \(\beta\) is inverse thermal energy.

Physicist's Reading

A physicist would read this as the density matrix for a quantum system in thermal equilibrium at temperature \(T\).

Symbol Breakdown

\(\rho_\beta\): thermal density matrix.

\(H\): Hamiltonian of the system.

\(\beta\): inverse temperature parameter.

\(Z\): partition function, which normalizes the state.

\(\mathrm{Tr}\): trace operation.

\(k_B\): Boltzmann constant.

\(T\): temperature.

Physical Meaning

The Gibbs state is the thermal fingerprint of a Hamiltonian. Tang-related Hamiltonian-learning work asks how much of \(H\) can be recovered from copies of \(\rho_\beta\).
