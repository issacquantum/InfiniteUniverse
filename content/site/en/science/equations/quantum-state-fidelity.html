Quantum State Fidelity

Equation

$$ F(\rho, \sigma) = \left(\operatorname{Tr}\sqrt{\sqrt{\rho}\,\sigma\,\sqrt{\rho}}\right)^2 $$

Literal Reading

F of rho comma sigma equals open bracket trace square root of square root of rho sigma square root of rho close bracket to the power of 2.

Common Reading

A measure of how close two quantum states are, especially when noise or imperfect operations are involved.

Symbols

\(F(\rho,\sigma)\): the fidelity between two states.

\(\rho\), \(\sigma\): density matrices being compared.

\(\sqrt{\rho}\): the positive square root of the density matrix.

\(\operatorname{Tr}\): trace operation.

Meaning of the Equation

Fidelity measures how close two quantum states are.
