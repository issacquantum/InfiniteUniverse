Quantum State Fidelity

Equation

$$ F(\rho, \sigma) = \left(\operatorname{Tr}\sqrt{\sqrt{\rho}\,\sigma\,\sqrt{\rho}}\right)^2 $$

Literal Reading

Fidelity between rho and sigma equals the square of the trace of the square root of square root rho, sigma, square root rho.

Physicist's Reading

A physicist would read it as a measure of how close two quantum states are, especially when noise or imperfect operations are involved.

Symbol Breakdown

\(F(\rho,\sigma)\): the fidelity between two states.

\(\rho\), \(\sigma\): density matrices being compared.

\(\sqrt{\rho}\): the positive square root of the density matrix.

\(\operatorname{Tr}\): trace operation.

Physical Meaning

Fidelity is used to judge how well a gate, memory, channel, or experiment preserves a quantum state. Perfect agreement gives fidelity one; fully distinguishable states give the lowest values.
