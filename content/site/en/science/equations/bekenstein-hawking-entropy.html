Bekenstein Hawking Entropy

Equation

$$S_{BH} = \frac{k_B c^3 A}{4G\hbar}$$

Literal Reading

Black hole entropy equals k sub B times c cubed times area divided by four G h bar.

Physicist's Reading

A physicist reads this as entropy being proportional to horizon area rather than volume.

Symbol Breakdown

\(S_{BH}\): black hole entropy.

\(A\): horizon area.

\(k_B\): Boltzmann constant.

\(\hbar\): reduced Planck constant.

Physical Meaning

This is one of the deepest hints that gravity, quantum theory, and information are connected. The information capacity of a black hole scales with its surface, not its interior volume.

Scope and Limit

The formula gives the thermodynamic entropy associated with a stationary horizon. It does not directly tell us which microscopic degrees of freedom store the information, which is why it remains central to quantum gravity.
