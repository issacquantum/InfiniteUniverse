Bekenstein Hawking Entropy

Equation

$$S_{BH} = \frac{k_B c^3 A}{4G\hbar}$$

Literal Reading

S sub BH equals k sub B c to the power of 3 A over of 4G h minus bar.

Common Reading

Entropy measures uncertainty, disorder, or missing information in the relevant physical or informational state.

Symbols

\(S_{BH}\): black hole entropy.

\(A\): horizon area.

\(k_B\): Boltzmann constant.

\(\hbar\): reduced Planck constant.

Meaning of the Equation

It connects the quantities in the formula so the relation can be used to compute or interpret a situation in Black Holes.

Scope and Limits

The formula gives the thermodynamic entropy associated with a stationary horizon. It does not directly tell us which microscopic degrees of freedom store the information, which is why it remains central to quantum gravity.
