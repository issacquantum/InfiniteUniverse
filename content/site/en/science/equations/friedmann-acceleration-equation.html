Friedmann Acceleration Equation

Equation

$$\frac{\ddot{a}}{a} = - \frac{4\pi G}{3}(\rho + 3p) + \frac{\Lambda}{3}$$

Literal Reading

a sub ddot over a equals minus 4 pi G of rho plus 3p over c to the power of 2 over 3 plus Lambda c to the power of 2 over 3.

Common Reading

The expansion rate of the universe is determined by energy density, curvature, pressure, and the cosmological constant.

Symbols

\(\ddot{a}\): second time derivative of the scale factor.

\(a\): scale factor.

\(\rho\): energy density.

\(p\): pressure.

\(G\): gravitational constant.

\(\Lambda\): cosmological constant.

Meaning of the Equation

This equation governs whether cosmic expansion speeds up or slows down. Density and pressure tend to decelerate expansion, while a positive cosmological constant can accelerate it.
