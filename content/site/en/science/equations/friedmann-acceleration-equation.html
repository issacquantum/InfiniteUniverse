Friedmann Acceleration Equation

Equation

$$\frac{\ddot{a}}{a} = - \frac{4\pi G}{3}(\rho + 3p) + \frac{\Lambda}{3}$$

Literal Reading

A double dot over a equals minus four pi G over three times rho plus three p, plus lambda over three.

Physicist's Reading

A physicist would read it as the cosmic acceleration depends on energy density, pressure, and the cosmological constant.

Symbol Breakdown

\(\ddot{a}\): second time derivative of the scale factor.

\(a\): scale factor.

\(\rho\): energy density.

\(p\): pressure.

\(G\): gravitational constant.

\(\Lambda\): cosmological constant.

Physical Meaning

This equation decides whether cosmic expansion is slowing down or speeding up. Ordinary matter and radiation tend to decelerate expansion, while a sufficiently positive cosmological constant drives acceleration.
