Friedmann Acceleration Equation

Equation

$$\frac{\ddot a}{a}=-\frac{4\pi G}{3}\left(\rho+\frac{3p}{c^2}\right)+\frac{\Lambda c^2}{3}$$

Literal Reading

double dot a over a equals minus 4 pi G over 3 times rho plus 3p over c squared, plus Lambda c squared over 3.

Common Reading

Positive pressure and density decelerate expansion; a positive cosmological constant accelerates expansion.

Symbols

\(\ddot{a}\): second time derivative of the scale factor.

\(a\): scale factor.

\(\rho\): mass density / energy density divided by \(c^2\).

\(p\): pressure.

\(G\): gravitational constant.

\(c\): speed of light.

\(\Lambda\): cosmological constant.

Meaning of the Equation

This equation governs whether cosmic expansion speeds up or slows down. Density and pressure tend to decelerate expansion, while a positive cosmological constant can accelerate it.
