Heisenberg Uncertainty Principle

Equation

$$ \Delta x \, \Delta p \geq \frac{\hbar}{2} $$

What the Formula Says

Delta x times delta p is greater than or equal to h bar over two.

What It Means in Practice

This means the position uncertainty times the momentum uncertainty cannot be smaller than h bar over two.

Symbols

\(\Delta x\): the uncertainty or spread in position.

\(\Delta p\): the uncertainty or spread in momentum.

\(\hbar/2\): the minimum quantum bound for the product of those spreads.

Equation Meaning

This relation does not describe bad instruments. It says quantum states themselves cannot assign arbitrarily sharp values to position and momentum at the same time.
