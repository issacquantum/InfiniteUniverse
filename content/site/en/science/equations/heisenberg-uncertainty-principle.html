Heisenberg Uncertainty Principle

Equation

$$ \Delta x \, \Delta p \geq \frac{\hbar}{2} $$

Literal Reading

Delta x times delta p is greater than or equal to h bar over two.

Common Reading

Short version: The position uncertainty times the momentum uncertainty cannot be smaller than h bar over two.

Symbols

\(\Delta x\): the uncertainty or spread in position.

\(\Delta p\): the uncertainty or spread in momentum.

\(\hbar/2\): the minimum quantum bound for the product of those spreads.

Meaning of the Equation

The position uncertainty times the momentum uncertainty cannot be smaller than h bar over two.
