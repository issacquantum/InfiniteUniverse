Heisenberg Uncertainty Principle

Equation

$$ \Delta x \, \Delta p \geq \frac{\hbar}{2} $$

Literal Reading

Delta x Delta p greater than or equal to h minus bar over 2.

Common Reading

The product of state spreads for noncommuting observables has a lower bound; this is not merely measurement-device disturbance.

Symbols

\(\Delta x\): the uncertainty or spread in position.

\(\Delta p\): the uncertainty or spread in momentum.

\(\hbar/2\): the minimum quantum bound for the product of those spreads.

Meaning of the Equation

The limit is built into wave structure itself. A state squeezed tightly in position needs many momentum components, so momentum becomes less sharply defined even with perfect instruments.
