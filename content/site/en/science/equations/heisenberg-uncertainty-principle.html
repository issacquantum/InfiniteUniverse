Heisenberg Uncertainty Principle

Equation

$$ \Delta x \, \Delta p \geq \frac{\hbar}{2} $$

Literal Reading

Read it as: delta x times delta p is greater than or equal to h bar over two.

Common Reading

Position and momentum cannot both be made arbitrarily sharp.

Symbols

\(\Delta x\): the uncertainty or spread in position.

\(\Delta p\): the uncertainty or spread in momentum.

\(\hbar/2\): the minimum quantum bound for the product of those spreads.

Meaning of the Equation

This relation connects the mathematical state of a physical system to what can happen next or what can be measured. It is not just notation: it states how energy, fields, waves, or probabilities constrain the behavior of the system.
