Scalar Field Lagrangian

Equation

$$\mathcal{L}=\frac{1}{2}\partial_\mu\phi\,\partial^\mu\phi-\frac{1}{2}m^2\phi^2$$

Plain Meaning

This is the basic rule for a free scalar field: one term tracks field variation, the other gives a mass scale.

Symbols

  • \(\mathcal{L}\): Lagrangian density.
  • \(\phi\): scalar field.
  • \(\partial_\mu\): spacetime derivative.
  • \(m\): mass parameter.

How to Read It

Use it as the simplest field-theory starting point before adding interactions.

Meaning in Context

The expression gives the energy-and-motion structure for a simple scalar field. More elaborate fields add spin, gauge structure, fermions, and interaction terms.
