Hydrogen Hamiltonian in Atomic Units

Equation

$$ \left(-\frac{1}{2}\nabla^2 - \frac{1}{r}\right)\psi = E\psi $$

Literal Reading

H equals minus 1 over 2 nabla to the power of 2 minus 1 over r.

Common Reading

The hydrogen stationary Schrödinger equation written in atomic units, where the kinetic and Coulomb terms are simplified.

Symbols

\(-\nabla^2/2\): the kinetic-energy operator in atomic units.

\(-1/r\): the Coulomb attraction in atomic units.

\(\psi\): the hydrogen wavefunction.

\(E\): the corresponding energy eigenvalue.

atomic units: a system where several physical constants are set to one to simplify the equation.

Meaning of the Equation

It connects the quantities in the formula so the relation can be used to compute or interpret a situation in Quantum Mechanics.
