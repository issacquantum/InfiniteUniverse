Hydrogen Hamiltonian in Atomic Units

Equation

$$ \left(-\frac{1}{2}\nabla^2 - \frac{1}{r}\right)\psi = E\psi $$

Literal Reading

Open parenthesis minus one half nabla squared minus one over r close parenthesis, psi, equals E psi.

Physicist's Reading

A physicist would read it as the hydrogen stationary Schrödinger equation written in atomic units, where the kinetic and Coulomb terms are simplified.

Symbol Breakdown

\(-\nabla^2/2\): the kinetic-energy operator in atomic units.

\(-1/r\): the Coulomb attraction in atomic units.

\(\psi\): the hydrogen wavefunction.

\(E\): the corresponding energy eigenvalue.

atomic units: a system where several physical constants are set to one to simplify the equation.

Physical Meaning

This is the hydrogen problem stripped to its cleanest mathematical form. It isolates the essential geometry of the atom.
