Semiclassical WKB (Wentzel-Kramers-Brillouin) Time Emergence

Equation

$$ \Psi[h,\phi]\approx A[h]e^{iMS_0[h]/\hbar}\chi[h,\phi],\qquad i\hbar\,\partial_{t_{\mathrm{WKB}}}\chi\approx\hat H_m\chi $$

Literal Reading

The universal wave functional is approximated by a rapidly varying gravitational phase times a matter state, and the matter state obeys an approximate Schrödinger equation with respect to WKB time.

Common Reading

A short way to say it: In semiclassical quantum gravity, a nearly classical geometry can behave like a clock for matter fields. Time is recovered approximately from correlations with the gravitational background.

Symbols

\(\Psi[h,\phi]\): wave functional of geometry \(h\) and matter fields \(\phi\).

\(S_0[h]\): leading gravitational Hamilton-Jacobi action.

\(\chi[h,\phi]\): matter-sector state on the approximate geometry.

\(t_{\mathrm{WKB}}\): emergent semiclassical time parameter.

\(\hat H_m\): matter Hamiltonian.
