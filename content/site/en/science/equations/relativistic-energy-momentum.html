Relativistic Relation Between Energy and Momentum

Equation

$$E^2 = (pc)^2 + (mc^2)^2$$

What the Formula Says

Energy squared equals the square of the quantity p c plus the square of the quantity m c squared.

What It Means in Practice

This means the invariant relation connecting total energy, momentum, and rest mass.

Symbols

\(E\): total relativistic energy.

\(p\): relativistic momentum.

\(m\): rest mass.

\(c\): speed of light.

Why It Matters

This equation applies to massive and massless particles. For a particle at rest, the momentum term vanishes. For light, the mass term vanishes and energy is tied directly to momentum.
