Jarzynski Equality

Equation

$$\langle e^{-\beta W}\rangle=e^{-\beta\Delta F}$$

Literal Reading

Read it as: the average of e to the negative beta W equals e to the negative beta delta F.

Common Reading

Many nonequilibrium work measurements can reveal an equilibrium free-energy difference.

Symbols

  • \(W\): work done in one trial.
  • \(\Delta F\): free-energy difference.
  • \(\beta\): inverse thermal energy, usually \(1/(k_BT)\).
  • \(\langle\cdot\rangle\): average over many trials.

Meaning of the Equation

This relation connects microscopic structure, energy, probability, or reaction behavior to a measurable physical result. It shows which conditions control the process and what changes when temperature, energy, particles, or available states change.
