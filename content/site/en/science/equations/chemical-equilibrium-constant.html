Chemical Equilibrium Constant

Equation

$$K=\frac{\prod_i a_{\mathrm{products},i}^{\nu_i}}{\prod_j a_{\mathrm{reactants},j}^{\nu_j}}$$

Literal Reading

Read it as: K equals the product of product activities raised to their powers, divided by the product of reactant activities raised to their powers.

Common Reading

At equilibrium, the activities of products and reactants settle into a characteristic ratio.

Symbols

  • \(K\): equilibrium constant.
  • \(a\): activity, an effective concentration.
  • \(\nu\): stoichiometric coefficient.

Meaning of the Equation

This relation connects microscopic structure, energy, probability, or reaction behavior to a measurable physical result. It shows which conditions control the process and what changes when temperature, energy, particles, or available states change.
