Chemical Equilibrium Constant

Equation

$$K=\frac{\prod_i a_{\mathrm{products},i}^{\nu_i}}{\prod_j a_{\mathrm{reactants},j}^{\nu_j}}$$

Plain Meaning

At equilibrium, the activities of products and reactants settle into a characteristic ratio.

Symbols

  • ▣\(K\): equilibrium constant.
  • ▣\(a\): activity, an effective concentration.
  • ▣\(\nu\): stoichiometric coefficient.

How to Read It

Large \(K\) favors products; small \(K\) favors reactants.

What It Does Not Say

It does not say how quickly equilibrium is reached.
