Hadamard Gate Matrix

Equation

$$ H = \frac{1}{\sqrt{2}}\begin{pmatrix} 1 & 1 \\ 1 & -1 \end{pmatrix} $$

Literal Reading

H equals one over square root of two times the matrix with entries one, one, one, and minus one.

Physicist's Reading

A physicist would read it as the single-qubit Hadamard operator, which rotates the computational basis into equal superpositions.

Symbol Breakdown

\(H\): the Hadamard gate.

\(\frac{1}{\sqrt{2}}\): the normalization factor that keeps the operation unitary.

\(\begin{pmatrix} 1 & 1 \\ 1 & -1 \end{pmatrix}\): the matrix form of the transformation in the \(|0\rangle, |1\rangle\) basis.

Physical Meaning

The Hadamard gate is central because it creates interference-ready superpositions from basis states. Many quantum algorithms begin with it to spread amplitude across multiple computational paths before later gates shape those amplitudes into a useful answer.
