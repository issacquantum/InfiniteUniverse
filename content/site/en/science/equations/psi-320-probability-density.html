Hydrogen 3d₍z²₎ Probability Density

Equation

$$ |\psi_{320}(r,\theta,\phi)|^2 = \frac{1}{39366\pi}\, r^4\, e^{-2r/3}\,(3\cos^2\theta - 1)^2 $$

Literal Reading

absolute value of psi sub 320 of r theta phi squared equals one over 39366 pi times r to the fourth times e to the minus 2r over 3 times 3 cosine squared theta minus 1 squared.

Common Reading

Squaring the wavefunction gives the spatial probability density for finding the electron.

Symbols

\(|\psi_{320}|^2\): the probability density, not the wavefunction itself.

\(r^4 e^{-2r/3}\): the radial probability weighting.

\((3\cos^2\theta - 1)^2\): the angular probability factor that preserves the orbital shape while removing sign information.

\(1/(39366\pi)\): the normalization constant for the density.

Meaning of the Equation

Squaring the wavefunction gives where detections are more or less likely for the 3d z-squared orbital. The lobes and torus are probability structure, not a literal electron path.
