Hydrogen 3d₍z²₎ Probability Density

Equation

$$ |\psi_{320}(r,\theta,\phi)|^2 = \frac{1}{39366\pi}\, r^4\, e^{-2r/3}\,(3\cos^2\theta - 1)^2 $$

Literal Reading

Absolute value of psi three two zero of r, theta, phi squared equals one over thirty nine thousand three hundred sixty six pi, times r to the fourth, times e to the minus two r over three, times three cosine squared theta minus one squared.

Physicist's Reading

A physicist would read it as the spatial probability density for the hydrogen 3d z squared state.

Symbol Breakdown

\(|\psi_{320}|^2\): the probability density, not the wavefunction itself.

\(r^4 e^{-2r/3}\): the radial probability weighting.

\((3\cos^2\theta - 1)^2\): the angular probability factor that preserves the orbital shape while removing sign information.

\(1/(39366\pi)\): the normalization constant for the density.

Physical Meaning

This is the quantity the orbital model samples. It indicates where the electron is more likely or less likely to be found upon measurement.
