Decohered Record State

Equation

$$ \rho_X \approx \sum_\alpha p_\alpha |\alpha\rangle\langle\alpha| $$

Literal Reading

Read it as: rho sub X is approximately equal to the sum over alpha of p alpha times ket alpha bra alpha.

Common Reading

After decoherence, the local state behaves like a weighted mixture of stable record alternatives.

Symbols

\(\rho_X\): the reduced state of the local record-bearing subsystem.

\(\approx\): approximately equal, because small interference terms are ignored at the effective branch level.

\(\sum_\alpha\): the sum over the stable record alternatives.

\(p_\alpha\): the branch weight or probability weight for record alternative \(\alpha\).

\(|\alpha\rangle\langle\alpha|\): the projector onto one stable record state.

Meaning of the Equation

The equation describes the effective classical-looking form of a local state after its environment has suppressed usable interference between different record alternatives. It is not a collapse law. It says that, for a finite observer inside a branch, later memory and action are organized around one stable record history rather than around a visible superposition of incompatible histories.
