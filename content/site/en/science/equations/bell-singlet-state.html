Bell Singlet State

Equation

$$ |\Psi^{-}\rangle = \frac{1}{\sqrt{2}}\left(|\uparrow\downarrow\rangle - |\downarrow\uparrow\rangle\right) $$

What the Formula Says

Psi minus equals one over square root of two times ket up down minus ket down up.

What It Means in Practice

This means the Bell singlet state is an equal superposition of up-down and down-up, with a relative minus sign.

Symbols

\(|\Psi^{-}\rangle\): the Bell singlet state, one of the standard maximally entangled two-particle states.

\(1/\sqrt{2}\): the normalization factor that keeps the total probability equal to one.

\(|\uparrow\downarrow\rangle\): the first particle is spin up and the second is spin down along the chosen measurement axis.

\(|\downarrow\uparrow\rangle\): the first particle is spin down and the second is spin up.

minus sign: the relative phase that distinguishes this Bell state from the other Bell combinations.

Why It Matters

This state describes two spin-\(\tfrac{1}{2}\) particles with total spin zero. Neither particle has its own definite spin value before measurement, but if both are measured along the same axis their outcomes are always opposite. The individual results are random. The anticorrelation is perfect.
