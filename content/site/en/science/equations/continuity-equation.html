Continuity Equation

Equation

$$ \frac{\partial |\Psi|^2}{\partial t} + \nabla \cdot \mathbf{J} = 0 $$

Literal Reading

Partial of absolute value of Psi squared with respect to t, plus nabla dot J, equals zero.

Physicist's Reading

A physicist would read it as the time change of probability density plus the divergence of probability current equals zero.

Symbol Breakdown

\(\partial |\Psi|^2 / \partial t\): the local rate of change of probability density.

\(\nabla \cdot \mathbf{J}\): the divergence of the probability current.

\(0\): the statement that probability is neither created nor destroyed locally.

Physical Meaning

This equation expresses local conservation of probability. Probability can move from place to place, but it cannot appear or vanish from nowhere.
