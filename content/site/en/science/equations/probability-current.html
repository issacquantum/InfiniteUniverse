Probability Current

Equation

$$ \mathbf{J} = \frac{\hbar}{2mi}\left(\Psi^*\nabla\Psi - \Psi\nabla\Psi^*\right) $$

What the Formula Says

J vector equals h bar over two m i, times Psi star grad Psi minus Psi grad Psi star.

What It Means in Practice

This means the probability current density built from the wavefunction and its spatial gradients.

Symbols

\(\mathbf{J}\): the probability current density vector.

\(\hbar\): the reduced Planck constant.

\(m\): the particle mass.

\(i\): the imaginary unit.

\(\Psi^*\): the complex conjugate of the wavefunction.

\(\nabla\Psi\): the spatial gradient of the wavefunction.

Why It Matters

This equation tells you how probability flows through space. It is the quantum analogue of a current in a fluid or in electrodynamics.
