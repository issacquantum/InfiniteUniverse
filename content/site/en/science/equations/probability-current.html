Probability Current

Equation

$$ \mathbf{J} = \frac{\hbar}{2mi}\left(\Psi^*\nabla\Psi - \Psi\nabla\Psi^*\right) $$

Literal Reading

j equals of h minus bar over 2mi the quantity psi to the power of times nabla psi minus psi nabla psi to the power of times.

Common Reading

The probability current density built from the wavefunction and its spatial gradients.

Symbols

\(\mathbf{J}\): the probability current density vector.

\(\hbar\): the reduced Planck constant.

\(m\): the particle mass.

\(i\): the imaginary unit.

\(\Psi^*\): the complex conjugate of the wavefunction.

\(\nabla\Psi\): the spatial gradient of the wavefunction.

Meaning of the Equation

This expression is for a spinless nonrelativistic particle with standard Schrödinger dynamics.
