Probability Current

Equation

$$ \mathbf{J} = \frac{\hbar}{2mi}\left(\Psi^*\nabla\Psi - \Psi\nabla\Psi^*\right) $$

Literal Reading

Read it as: j vector equals h bar over two m i, times Psi star grad Psi minus Psi grad Psi star.

Common Reading

The probability current density built from the wavefunction and its spatial gradients.

Symbols

\(\mathbf{J}\): the probability current density vector.

\(\hbar\): the reduced Planck constant.

\(m\): the particle mass.

\(i\): the imaginary unit.

\(\Psi^*\): the complex conjugate of the wavefunction.

\(\nabla\Psi\): the spatial gradient of the wavefunction.

Meaning of the Equation

This relation connects the mathematical state of a physical system to what can happen next or what can be measured. It is not just notation: it states how energy, fields, waves, or probabilities constrain the behavior of the system.
