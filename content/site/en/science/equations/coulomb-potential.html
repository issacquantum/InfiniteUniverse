Coulomb Potential

Equation

$$ V(r) = -\frac{e^2}{4\pi\epsilon_0 r} $$

What the Formula Says

V of r equals minus e squared over four pi epsilon naught r.

What It Means in Practice

This means the attractive electrostatic potential energy between the electron and the proton.

Symbols

\(V(r)\): the potential energy as a function of distance.

\(e\): the elementary charge.

\(\epsilon_0\): the permittivity of free space.

\(r\): the distance from the nucleus.

negative sign: the interaction is attractive, not repulsive.

Why It Matters

This potential defines the hydrogen atom problem. It tells the Schrödinger equation what energetic landscape the electron moves in.
