Coulomb Potential

Equation

$$ V(r) = -\frac{e^2}{4\pi\epsilon_0 r} $$

Literal Reading

Read it as: V open parenthesis r close parenthesis equals minus e to the power of 2 over open parenthesis 4 pi epsilon 0 r close parenthesis.

Common Reading

The attractive electrostatic potential energy between the electron and the proton.

Symbols

\(V(r)\): the potential energy as a function of distance.

\(e\): the elementary charge.

\(\epsilon_0\): the permittivity of free space.

\(r\): the distance from the nucleus.

negative sign: the interaction is attractive, not repulsive.

Meaning of the Equation

The electron in hydrogen feels an attractive inverse-distance electric potential.
