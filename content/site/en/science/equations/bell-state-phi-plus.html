Bell State Phi Plus

Equation

$$ |\Phi^{+}\rangle = \frac{|00\rangle + |11\rangle}{\sqrt{2}} $$

What the Formula Says

Phi plus ket equals zero zero ket plus one one ket, all over square root of two.

What It Means in Practice

This means one of the maximally entangled Bell states, where two qubits are equally shared between the outcomes zero zero and one one.

Symbols

\(|\Phi^{+}\rangle\): the specific Bell state being named.

\(|00\rangle\): both qubits in state zero.

\(|11\rangle\): both qubits in state one.

\(\sqrt{2}\): the normalization factor that keeps the total probability equal to one.

\(+\): the two basis states add coherently with the same relative phase.

Equation Meaning

This state is a standard resource in quantum information. Measuring either qubit immediately fixes the correlated outcome of the other, and that shared structure cannot be reproduced by any separable two-qubit state.
