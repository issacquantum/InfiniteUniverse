Galilean Transformations

Equation

$$x' = x - vt,\qquad t' = t$$

Literal Reading

x prime equals x minus v t, and t prime equals t.

Physicist's Reading

A physicist reads this as the classical transformation between two inertial frames moving at constant relative velocity.

Symbol Breakdown

\(x\): position measured in the original frame.

\(x'\): position measured in the moving frame.

\(v\): relative velocity between frames.

\(t\) and \(t'\): time measured in each frame.

Physical Meaning

This equation represents the Newtonian assumption that time is universal. Observers may disagree about position, but they share the same time coordinate. Relativity replaces this assumption because light speed is not compatible with universal time.
