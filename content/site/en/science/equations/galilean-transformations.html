Galilean Transformations

Equation

$$x' = x - vt,\qquad t' = t$$

Literal Reading

Read it as: x prime equals x minus v t, and t prime equals t.

Common Reading

The classical transformation between two inertial frames moving at constant relative velocity.

Symbols

\(x\): position measured in the original frame.

\(x'\): position measured in the moving frame.

\(v\): relative velocity between frames.

\(t\) and \(t'\): time measured in each frame.

Meaning of the Equation

This relation connects geometry, motion, horizons, or gravitational sources. It tells what clocks, distances, curvature, or observers would measure, so the physical claim is tied to spacetime structure rather than to a picture or metaphor.
