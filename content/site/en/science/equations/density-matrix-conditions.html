Density Matrix Conditions

Equation

$$ \rho\succeq 0,\qquad \mathrm{Tr}(\rho)=1 $$

Literal Reading

Read it as: rho greater than or equal to 0 comma trace open parenthesis rho close parenthesis equals 1.

Common Reading

A valid mixed quantum state must assign nonnegative probabilities to measurements and must have total probability one.

Symbols

\(\rho\): density matrix representing a mixed quantum state.

\(\succeq 0\): positive semidefinite condition.

\(\mathrm{Tr}(\rho)\): trace of the density matrix.

\(1\): total probability normalization.

Meaning of the Equation

A density matrix represents pure states, mixed states, and statistical uncertainty.
