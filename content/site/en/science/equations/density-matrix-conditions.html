Density Matrix Conditions

Equation

$$ \rho\succeq 0,\qquad \mathrm{Tr}(\rho)=1 $$

What the Formula Says

The density matrix \(\rho\) is positive semidefinite, and its trace equals one.

What It Means in Practice

A valid mixed quantum state must assign nonnegative probabilities to measurements and must have total probability one.

Symbols

\(\rho\): density matrix representing a mixed quantum state.

\(\succeq 0\): positive semidefinite condition.

\(\mathrm{Tr}(\rho)\): trace of the density matrix.

\(1\): total probability normalization.

Why It Matters

These are the basic mathematical conditions for a physical mixed state. Tang's tomography-related work studies when useful information about such states can be learned without reconstructing everything.
