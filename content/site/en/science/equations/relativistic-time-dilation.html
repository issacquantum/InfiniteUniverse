Relativistic Time Dilation

Equation

$$\Delta t' = \gamma \Delta t$$

What the Formula Says

Delta t prime equals gamma times delta t.

What It Means in Practice

This means moving clocks being measured to run slow relative to the observer's frame.

Symbols

\(\Delta t\): time interval measured in the clock's rest frame.

\(\Delta t'\): time interval measured from the relatively moving frame.

\(\gamma\): Lorentz factor.

Why It Matters

Time dilation is not a mechanical defect in a clock. It is a property of spacetime measurements. The effect is confirmed by particle lifetimes, atomic clock experiments, and systems operating at high speed.
