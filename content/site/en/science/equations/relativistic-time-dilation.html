Relativistic Time Dilation

Equation

$$\Delta t' = \gamma \Delta t$$

Literal Reading

Delta t equals gamma Delta τ.

Common Reading

Moving clocks being measured to run slow relative to the observer's frame.

Symbols

\(\Delta t\): time interval measured in the clock's rest frame.

\(\Delta t'\): time interval measured from the relatively moving frame.

\(\gamma\): Lorentz factor.

Meaning of the Equation

The effect is geometric, not a mechanical defect in the clock. Different paths through spacetime accumulate different amounts of proper time, so observers can disagree consistently.
