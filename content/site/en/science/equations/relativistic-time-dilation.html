Relativistic Time Dilation

Equation

$$\Delta t' = \gamma \Delta t$$

Literal Reading

Read it as: delta t prime equals gamma times delta t.

Common Reading

Moving clocks being measured to run slow relative to the observer's frame.

Symbols

\(\Delta t\): time interval measured in the clock's rest frame.

\(\Delta t'\): time interval measured from the relatively moving frame.

\(\gamma\): Lorentz factor.

Meaning of the Equation

This relation connects geometry, motion, horizons, or gravitational sources. It tells what clocks, distances, curvature, or observers would measure, so the physical claim is tied to spacetime structure rather than to a picture or metaphor.
