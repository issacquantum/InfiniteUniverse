Hamiltonian Time Evolution

Equation

$$ U(t)=e^{-iHt/\hbar} $$

Literal Reading

Read it as: the time-evolution operator \(U(t)\) equals the exponential of negative \(i\) times the Hamiltonian times time divided by \(\hbar\).

Common Reading

The rule that the Hamiltonian generates unitary time evolution for a closed quantum system.

Symbols

\(U(t)\): unitary time-evolution operator.

\(H\): Hamiltonian, the energy and interaction operator.

\(t\): elapsed time.

\(i\): imaginary unit.

\(\hbar\): reduced Planck constant.

Meaning of the Equation

This relation connects the mathematical state of a physical system to what can happen next or what can be measured. It is not just notation: it states how energy, fields, waves, or probabilities constrain the behavior of the system.
