Hamiltonian Time Evolution

Equation

$$ U(t)=e^{-iHt/\hbar} $$

Literal Reading

U open parenthesis t close parenthesis equals e to the power of open brace minus iHt over h minus bar close brace.

Common Reading

The rule that the Hamiltonian generates unitary time evolution for a closed quantum system.

Symbols

\(U(t)\): unitary time-evolution operator.

\(H\): Hamiltonian, the energy and interaction operator.

\(t\): elapsed time.

\(i\): imaginary unit.

\(\hbar\): reduced Planck constant.

Meaning of the Equation

Given an initial quantum state, \(U(t)\) tells how that state changes after time \(t\). The Hamiltonian sets the energy-dependent phases, and the unitary form preserves total probability instead of creating or deleting it.
