Hamiltonian Time Evolution

Equation

$$ U(t)=e^{-iHt/\hbar} $$

What the Formula Says

The time-evolution operator \(U(t)\) equals the exponential of negative \(i\) times the Hamiltonian times time divided by \(\hbar\).

What It Means in Practice

This means the rule that the Hamiltonian generates unitary time evolution for a closed quantum system.

Symbols

\(U(t)\): unitary time-evolution operator.

\(H\): Hamiltonian, the energy and interaction operator.

\(t\): elapsed time.

\(i\): imaginary unit.

\(\hbar\): reduced Planck constant.

Equation Meaning

Hamiltonian learning tries to infer \(H\). If \(H\) is known, this equation predicts how the quantum system evolves in time.
