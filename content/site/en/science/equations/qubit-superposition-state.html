Qubit Superposition State

Equation

$$ |\psi\rangle = \alpha|0\rangle + \beta|1\rangle $$

Literal Reading

Read it as: psi ket equals alpha times zero ket plus beta times one ket.

Common Reading

A qubit can combine zero and one with complex amplitudes.

Symbols

\(|\psi\rangle\): the full quantum state of the qubit.

\(\alpha\): the complex amplitude attached to the basis state \(|0\rangle\).

\(\beta\): the complex amplitude attached to the basis state \(|1\rangle\).

\(|0\rangle\): the computational basis state zero.

\(|1\rangle\): the computational basis state one.

Meaning of the Equation

This relation describes a quantum state, transformation, probability, or correlation. It matters because quantum systems are not described by one definite classical value; the equation tells what can be predicted from amplitudes, operators, or density matrices.
