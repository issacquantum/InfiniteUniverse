L2-Norm (Euclidean Norm) Matrix Sampling

Equation

$$ \Pr[i]=\frac{\|A_{i,*}\|_2^2}{\|A\|_F^2},\qquad \Pr[j\mid i]=\frac{|A_{ij}|^2}{\|A_{i,*}\|_2^2} $$

What the Formula Says

The probability of choosing row \(i\) is its squared row norm divided by the squared Frobenius norm of the matrix. After row \(i\) is chosen, the probability of choosing column \(j\) is the squared entry divided by that row's squared norm.

What It Means in Practice

This is the matrix version of length-square sampling: sample large-energy rows first, then large-energy entries within the selected row.

Symbols

\(A\): the matrix being sampled.

\(A_{i,*}\): row \(i\) of the matrix.

\(A_{ij}\): entry in row \(i\), column \(j\).

\(\|A_{i,*}\|_2\): \(\ell^2\) norm of row \(i\).

\(\|A\|_F\): Frobenius norm of the whole matrix.

\(\Pr[j\mid i]\): probability of choosing \(j\) after \(i\) has already been chosen.

Equation Meaning

This access model is powerful because it lets a classical algorithm focus on statistically important rows and columns without reading the whole matrix. It is also restrictive because the data structure must support this sampling efficiently.
