L2-Norm (Euclidean Norm) Matrix Sampling

Equation

$$ \Pr[i]=\frac{\|A_{i,*}\|_2^2}{\|A\|_F^2},\qquad \Pr[j\mid i]=\frac{|A_{ij}|^2}{\|A_{i,*}\|_2^2} $$

Literal Reading

Read it as: the probability of choosing row \(i\) is its squared row norm divided by the squared Frobenius norm of the matrix. After row \(i\) is chosen, the probability of choosing column \(j\) is the squared entry divided by that row's squared norm.

Common Reading

This is the matrix version of length-square sampling: sample large-energy rows first, then large-energy entries within the selected row.

Symbols

\(A\): the matrix being sampled.

\(A_{i,*}\): row \(i\) of the matrix.

\(A_{ij}\): entry in row \(i\), column \(j\).

\(\|A_{i,*}\|_2\): \(\ell^2\) norm of row \(i\).

\(\|A\|_F\): Frobenius norm of the whole matrix.

\(\Pr[j\mid i]\): probability of choosing \(j\) after \(i\) has already been chosen.

Meaning of the Equation

The matrix version of length-square sampling: sample large-energy rows first, then large-energy entries within the selected row.
