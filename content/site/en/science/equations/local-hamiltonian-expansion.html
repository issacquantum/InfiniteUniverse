Local Hamiltonian Expansion

Equation

$$ H=\sum_{a=1}^{m}\lambda_a E_a $$

What the Formula Says

The Hamiltonian \(H\) equals the sum from \(a=1\) to \(m\) of coefficient \(\lambda_a\) times local term \(E_a\).

What It Means in Practice

A many-body Hamiltonian can be decomposed into local interaction terms, each with an unknown strength.

Symbols

\(H\): full Hamiltonian of the system.

\(E_a\): known local observable or interaction term.

\(\lambda_a\): coefficient measuring the strength of term \(E_a\).

\(a\): index labeling interaction terms.

\(m\): total number of terms.

Equation Meaning

Hamiltonian learning often means estimating the coefficients \(\lambda_a\), and sometimes the structure of the \(E_a\) terms, from measurements or time evolution.
