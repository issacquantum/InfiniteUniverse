Local Hamiltonian Expansion

Equation

$$ H=\sum_{a=1}^{m}\lambda_a E_a $$

Literal Reading

Read it as: H equals sum sub i H sub i.

Common Reading

A many-body Hamiltonian can be decomposed into local interaction terms, each with an unknown strength.

Symbols

\(H\): full Hamiltonian of the system.

\(E_a\): known local observable or interaction term.

\(\lambda_a\): coefficient measuring the strength of term \(E_a\).

\(a\): index labeling interaction terms.

\(m\): total number of terms.

Meaning of the Equation

The total energy operator is written as a sum of smaller interaction pieces rather than one opaque all-at-once object. In quantum complexity, that structure lets researchers ask whether the lowest possible energy of a many-part system can be checked or computed efficiently.
