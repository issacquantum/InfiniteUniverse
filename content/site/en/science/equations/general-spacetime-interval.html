General Spacetime Interval

Equation

$$ds^2 = g_{\mu\nu}\,dx^\mu dx^\nu$$

Literal Reading

d s squared equals g mu nu times d x mu times d x nu.

Physicist's Reading

A physicist reads this as the local geometric interval between nearby events in curved spacetime.

Symbol Breakdown

\(ds^2\): infinitesimal spacetime interval.

\(g_{\mu\nu}\): metric tensor of the spacetime geometry.

\(dx^\mu\) and \(dx^\nu\): infinitesimal coordinate separations.

Physical Meaning

The metric tells clocks, rulers, and light cones how to behave locally. In general relativity, this interval is the basic object from which proper time and causal structure are read.
