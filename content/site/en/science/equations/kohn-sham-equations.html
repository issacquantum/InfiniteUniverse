Kohn-Sham Equations

Equation

$$\left[-\frac{\hbar^2}{2m}\nabla^2+V_{\mathrm{eff}}(\mathbf{r})\right]\psi_i=\epsilon_i\psi_i$$

Literal Reading

Read it as: the effective one-electron Hamiltonian acting on psi i equals epsilon i times psi i.

Common Reading

A hard many-electron problem is replaced by easier one-electron-like equations with an effective potential.

Symbols

  • \(\psi_i\): Kohn-Sham orbital.
  • \(V_{\mathrm{eff}}\): effective potential.
  • \(\epsilon_i\): orbital energy parameter.
  • \(\nabla^2\): spatial curvature operator.

Meaning of the Equation

This relation connects the mathematical state of a physical system to what can happen next or what can be measured. It is not just notation: it states how energy, fields, waves, or probabilities constrain the behavior of the system.
