Kohn-Sham Equations

Equation

$$\left[-\frac{\hbar^2}{2m}\nabla^2+V_{\mathrm{eff}}(\mathbf{r})\right]\psi_i=\epsilon_i\psi_i$$

Plain Meaning

A hard many-electron problem is replaced by easier one-electron-like equations with an effective potential.

Symbols

  • ▣\(\psi_i\): Kohn-Sham orbital.
  • ▣\(V_{\mathrm{eff}}\): effective potential.
  • ▣\(\epsilon_i\): orbital energy parameter.
  • ▣\(\nabla^2\): spatial curvature operator.

How to Read It

This is the practical engine behind much density-functional-theory calculation.

What It Does Not Say

It depends on approximations to exchange and correlation. It is not exact for every chemical problem.
