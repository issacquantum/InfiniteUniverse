Kohn-Sham Equations

Equation

$$\left[-\frac{\hbar^2}{2m}\nabla^2+V_{\mathrm{eff}}(\mathbf{r})\right]\psi_i=\epsilon_i\psi_i$$

Literal Reading

open bracket minus 1 over 2 nabla to the power of 2 plus V sub eff of r close bracket psi sub i equals epsilon sub i psi sub i.

Common Reading

A hard many-electron problem is replaced by easier one-electron-like equations with an effective potential.

Symbols

  • \(\psi_i\): Kohn-Sham orbital.
  • \(V_{\mathrm{eff}}\): effective potential.
  • \(\epsilon_i\): orbital energy parameter.
  • \(\nabla^2\): spatial curvature operator.

Meaning of the Equation

The equations trade the full interacting many-electron problem for an effective one-electron problem. The difficult physics is hidden in the exchange-correlation term, which must be approximated.
