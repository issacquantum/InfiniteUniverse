No Cloning and Linearity

Equation

$$ U\left(|\psi\rangle|0\rangle\right) = |\psi\rangle|\psi\rangle $$

Literal Reading

U acting on psi ket and zero ket equals psi ket copied into two psi kets.

Physicist's Reading

A physicist would read it as the kind of operation a perfect universal quantum copier would need to perform.

Symbol Breakdown

\(U\): a hypothetical unitary operation that would perform the copying.

\(|\psi\rangle\): an arbitrary unknown quantum state.

\(|0\rangle\): a blank target state prepared to receive the copy.

\(|\psi\rangle|\psi\rangle\): two identical copies of the original state.

Physical Meaning

This equation represents the impossible goal of a universal quantum cloning machine. Quantum evolution is linear, so no single unitary operation can copy every possible unknown state perfectly. This is why quantum information cannot be duplicated the way classical information can.
