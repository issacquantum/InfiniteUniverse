Pure State Hilbert Normalization

Equation

$$ |\psi\rangle\in\mathcal{H},\qquad \langle\psi|\psi\rangle=1 $$

What the Formula Says

The state \( |\psi\rangle \) belongs to Hilbert space \(\mathcal{H}\), and its inner product with itself equals one.

What It Means in Practice

A pure quantum state is a normalized vector in Hilbert space.

Symbols

\(|\psi\rangle\): pure quantum state vector.

\(\mathcal{H}\): Hilbert space of possible states.

\(\langle\psi|\psi\rangle\): inner product of the state with itself.

\(1\): normalization condition ensuring total probability is one.

Equation Meaning

Quantum state learning must respect normalization. For many qubits, the Hilbert space becomes exponentially large, which is why Tang's state-learning work focuses on structure rather than arbitrary full-state reconstruction.
