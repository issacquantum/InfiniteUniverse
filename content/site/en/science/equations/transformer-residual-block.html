Transformer Residual Block

Equation

$$ y=\operatorname{LayerNorm}(x+\operatorname{Sublayer}(x)) $$

Literal Reading

Read it as: the output is layer normalization applied to the original input plus the sublayer's transformation of that input.

Common Reading

The residual path lets information and gradients move through deep transformer stacks without forcing every layer to relearn the identity map.

Symbols

\(x\): incoming representation.

\(\operatorname{Sublayer}(x)\): attention or feed-forward transformation.

\(\operatorname{LayerNorm}\): normalization that stabilizes activations.

Meaning of the Equation

This relation turns an algorithmic step into a precise rule. It identifies what is being updated, counted, or bounded, so the process can be checked by its state, cost, error, or learning behavior instead of only by a visual animation.
