Transformer Residual Block

Equation

$$ y=\operatorname{LayerNorm}(x+\operatorname{Sublayer}(x)) $$

Literal Reading

y equals LayerNorm of x plus Sublayer of x.

Common Reading

A residual block adds a learned transformation back to the input so information and gradients can pass through deep networks more easily.

Symbols

\(x\): incoming representation.

\(\operatorname{Sublayer}(x)\): attention or feed-forward transformation.

\(\operatorname{LayerNorm}\): normalization that stabilizes activations.

Meaning of the Equation

The residual connection preserves the incoming representation while the block adds a learned change. This helps deep transformer stacks remain trainable and keeps information from being overwritten too easily.
