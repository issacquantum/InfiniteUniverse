Derivative Notation

Equation

$$\frac{df}{dx}$$

Literal Reading

Read it as: d f over d x.

Common Reading

This asks how fast the quantity \(f\) changes when \(x\) changes by a tiny amount.

Symbols

  • \(f\): the quantity or function being studied.
  • \(x\): the input or variable being changed.
  • \(d/dx\): the operation of taking a local rate of change.

Meaning of the Equation

This relation defines how one mathematical quantity is built from another. It gives a rule for change, accumulation, transformation, or iteration, which is why the same structure can be used inside physics, computation, and modeling.
