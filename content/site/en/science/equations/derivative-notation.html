Derivative Notation

Equation

$$\frac{df}{dx}$$

Plain Meaning

This asks how fast the quantity \(f\) changes when \(x\) changes by a tiny amount.

Symbols

  • \(f\): the quantity or function being studied.
  • \(x\): the input or variable being changed.
  • \(d/dx\): the operation of taking a local rate of change.

How to Read It

Read it as slope at one point, not just average change across a long interval. In physics, this is how position becomes velocity and velocity becomes acceleration.

Meaning in Context

The derivative means local rate of change. It tells how fast a quantity changes at a particular input.
