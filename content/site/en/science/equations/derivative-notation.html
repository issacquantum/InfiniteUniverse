Derivative Notation

Equation

$$\frac{df}{dx}$$

Literal Reading

Common Reading

This asks how fast the quantity \(f\) changes when \(x\) changes by a tiny amount.

Symbols

  • \(f\): the quantity or function being studied.
  • \(x\): the input or variable being changed.
  • \(d/dx\): the operation of taking a local rate of change.

Meaning of the Equation

The derivative is the local slope seen when a curve is zoomed in until it looks linear. It becomes the language of velocity, gradients, and differential equations.
