P and NP (nondeterministic polynomial time) Definitions

Equation

$$ P=\bigcup_{k\ge1}\mathrm{TIME}(n^k),\qquad NP=\bigcup_{k\ge1}\mathrm{NTIME}(n^k) $$

Literal Reading

Read it as: \(P\) contains problems solvable in polynomial time. \(NP\) contains problems verifiable by nondeterministic polynomial time, equivalently problems with efficiently checkable certificates.

Common Reading

This separates finding a solution from checking one.

Symbols

\(\mathrm{TIME}(n^k)\): deterministic computation bounded by a polynomial.

\(\mathrm{NTIME}(n^k)\): nondeterministic polynomial-time computation.

\(\bigcup_{k\ge1}\): union over all polynomial degrees.

Meaning of the Equation

This relation turns an algorithmic step into a precise rule. It identifies what is being updated, counted, or bounded, so the process can be checked by its state, cost, error, or learning behavior instead of only by a visual animation.
