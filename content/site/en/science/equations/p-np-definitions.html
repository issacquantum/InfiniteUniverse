P and NP (nondeterministic polynomial time) Definitions

Equation

$$ P=\bigcup_{k\ge1}\mathrm{TIME}(n^k),\qquad NP=\bigcup_{k\ge1}\mathrm{NTIME}(n^k) $$

What the Formula Says

\(P\) contains problems solvable in polynomial time. \(NP\) contains problems verifiable by nondeterministic polynomial time, equivalently problems with efficiently checkable certificates.

What It Means in Practice

This separates finding a solution from checking one. The unresolved question is whether every efficiently checkable solution is also efficiently findable.

Symbols

\(\mathrm{TIME}(n^k)\): deterministic computation bounded by a polynomial.

\(\mathrm{NTIME}(n^k)\): nondeterministic polynomial-time computation.

\(\bigcup_{k\ge1}\): union over all polynomial degrees.

Why It Matters

The distinction marks a boundary between problems we can solve efficiently and problems where proposed answers can be checked efficiently.
