Proper Time

Equation

$$d\tau = \frac{1}{c}\sqrt{-ds^2}$$

Literal Reading

dτ equals ds over c.

Common Reading

Proper time is the time measured along one worldline.

An ideal clock is a theoretical local clock that keeps perfect time along its own worldline and does not disturb the system being measured. It represents the clean limit of a real clock, not a magical device or a particular clock design.

Symbols

\(d\tau\): infinitesimal proper time.

\(ds^2\): spacetime interval using the \((-+++)\) sign convention.

\(c\): speed of light.

Meaning of the Equation

Proper time is the clock time accumulated along one particular worldline. It is path-dependent and is the local time measured by an ideal clock carried with the object.
