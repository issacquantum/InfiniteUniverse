Schwarzschild Metric

Equation

$$ds^2 = -\left(1 - \frac{2GM}{rc^2}\right)c^2dt^2 + \left(1 - \frac{2GM}{rc^2}\right)^{-1}dr^2 + r^2d\Omega^2$$

What the Formula Says

The spacetime interval is built from a time term, a radial term, and an angular term around a spherical mass.

What It Means in Practice

This means the geometry outside a spherical non rotating mass in general relativity.

Symbols

\(ds^2\): spacetime interval.

\(M\): central mass.

\(r\): radial coordinate.

\(t\): time coordinate measured far from the mass.

\(d\Omega^2\): angular part of the spherical geometry.

Why It Matters

This metric is the mathematical foundation of the simplest black hole. It shows how mass changes the measurement of time, radius, and geometry around it.

Scope and Limits

The coordinate singularity at \(r=2GM/c^2\) is not a curvature singularity. The metric is exact outside an ideal spherical nonrotating mass, but different coordinates are needed to describe horizon crossing cleanly.
