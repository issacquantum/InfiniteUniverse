Schwarzschild Metric

Equation

$$ds^2 = -\left(1 - \frac{2GM}{rc^2}\right)c^2dt^2 + \left(1 - \frac{2GM}{rc^2}\right)^{-1}dr^2 + r^2d\Omega^2$$

Literal Reading

Read it as: the spacetime interval is built from a time term, a radial term, and an angular term around a spherical mass.

Common Reading

The geometry outside a spherical non rotating mass in general relativity.

Symbols

\(ds^2\): spacetime interval.

\(M\): central mass.

\(r\): radial coordinate.

\(t\): time coordinate measured far from the mass.

\(d\Omega^2\): angular part of the spherical geometry.

Meaning of the Equation

This relation connects geometry, motion, horizons, or gravitational sources. It tells what clocks, distances, curvature, or observers would measure, so the physical claim is tied to spacetime structure rather than to a picture or metaphor.

Scope and Limits

The coordinate singularity at \(r=2GM/c^2\) is not a curvature singularity. The metric is exact outside an ideal spherical nonrotating mass, but different coordinates are needed to describe horizon crossing cleanly.
