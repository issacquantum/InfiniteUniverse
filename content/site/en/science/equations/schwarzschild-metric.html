Schwarzschild Metric

Equation

$$ds^2 = -\left(1 - \frac{2GM}{rc^2}\right)c^2dt^2 + \left(1 - \frac{2GM}{rc^2}\right)^{-1}dr^2 + r^2d\Omega^2$$

Literal Reading

ds to the power of 2 equals minus of 1 minus r sub s over r c to the power of 2dt to the power of 2 plus of 1 minus r sub s over r to the power of minus 1 dr to the power of 2 plus r to the power of 2d omega to the power of 2.

Common Reading

The geometry outside a spherical non rotating mass in general relativity.

Symbols

\(ds^2\): spacetime interval.

\(M\): central mass.

\(r\): radial coordinate.

\(t\): time coordinate measured far from the mass.

\(d\Omega^2\): angular part of the spherical geometry.

Meaning of the Equation

This describes Schwarzschild geometry for a static, spherically symmetric, uncharged, nonrotating mass. The coordinates are singular at \(r=r_s\), but the horizon is not a curvature singularity.

Scope and Limits

The coordinate singularity at \(r=2GM/c^2\) is not a curvature singularity. The metric is exact outside an ideal spherical nonrotating mass, but different coordinates are needed to describe horizon crossing cleanly.
