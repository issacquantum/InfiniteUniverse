Maxwell's Equations

Equation

$$\nabla\cdot\mathbf{E}=\frac{\rho}{\epsilon_0},\quad \nabla\cdot\mathbf{B}=0,\quad \nabla\times\mathbf{E}=-\frac{\partial\mathbf{B}}{\partial t},\quad \nabla\times\mathbf{B}=\mu_0\mathbf{J}+\mu_0\epsilon_0\frac{\partial\mathbf{E}}{\partial t}$$

Literal Reading

Read it as: the four differential Maxwell equations connect electric fields, magnetic fields, charge density, current, and changing fields.

Common Reading

A short way to say it: These four equations describe how electric and magnetic fields are sourced and how they change each other.

Symbols

  • \(\rho\): charge density.
  • \(\mathbf{J}\): current density.
  • \(\nabla\cdot\): divergence, measuring sources or sinks.
  • \(\nabla\times\): curl, measuring circulation.

Meaning of the Equation

These four equations describe how electric and magnetic fields are sourced and how they change each other.
