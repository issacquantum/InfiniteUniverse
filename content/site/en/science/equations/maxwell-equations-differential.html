Maxwell's Equations

Equation

$$\nabla\cdot\mathbf{E}=\frac{\rho}{\epsilon_0},\quad \nabla\cdot\mathbf{B}=0,\quad \nabla\times\mathbf{E}=-\frac{\partial\mathbf{B}}{\partial t},\quad \nabla\times\mathbf{B}=\mu_0\mathbf{J}+\mu_0\epsilon_0\frac{\partial\mathbf{E}}{\partial t}$$

Literal Reading

Read it as: nabla dot E equals rho over epsilon 0 comma nabla dot B equals 0 comma nabla cross E equals minus partial B over partial t comma nabla cross B equals mu 0 J plus mu 0 epsilon 0 partial E over partial t.

Common Reading

The divergence and curl relations of the electric and magnetic fields determine how charges, currents, and changing fields produce electromagnetism.

Symbols

  • \(\rho\): charge density.
  • \(\mathbf{J}\): current density.
  • \(\nabla\cdot\): divergence, measuring sources or sinks.
  • \(\nabla\times\): curl, measuring circulation.

Meaning of the Equation

Charges create electric fields, magnetic monopoles are absent, changing magnetic fields create electric curls, and currents or changing electric fields create magnetic curls.
