Relativistic Particle Action

Equation

$$S=-mc\int\sqrt{-g_{\mu\nu}dx^\mu dx^\nu}=-mc^2\int d\tau$$

Literal Reading

S equals negative m c times the integral of the square root of negative g mu nu d x mu d x nu, which equals negative m c squared times the integral of d tau.

Common Reading

The action for a free massive relativistic particle.

Symbols

\(S\): action.

\(m\): particle mass.

\(g_{\mu\nu}\): spacetime metric.

\(dx^\mu\): infinitesimal displacement along the worldline.

\(d\tau\): proper time accumulated along a timelike path.

Meaning of the Equation

The action is proportional to the proper time accumulated along the particle worldline. Extremizing it gives the geodesic path for a free massive particle, so free motion is described by spacetime geometry rather than by a separate Newtonian time background.
