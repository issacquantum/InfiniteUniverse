Relativistic Particle Action

Equation

$$S = -mc\int ds = -mc^2\int d\tau$$

What the Formula Says

S equals negative m c times the integral of d s, which also equals negative m c squared times the integral of d tau.

What It Means in Practice

This means the action for a free massive relativistic particle.

Symbols

\(S\): action.

\(m\): particle mass.

\(ds\): spacetime line element along the worldline.

\(d\tau\): proper time, with \(ds=c\,d\tau\) for a timelike path.

Why It Matters

Extremizing this action gives the geodesic equation. In plain language, a freely falling massive body follows the path dictated by spacetime geometry.
