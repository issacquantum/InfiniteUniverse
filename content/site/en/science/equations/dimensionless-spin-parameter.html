Dimensionless Spin Parameter

Equation

$$\chi = \frac{cJ}{GM^2}$$

Literal Reading

Chi equals c times angular momentum divided by G times mass squared.

Physicist's Reading

A physicist reads this as the normalized spin of a Kerr black hole.

Symbol Breakdown

\(\chi\): dimensionless spin parameter.

\(J\): angular momentum.

\(M\): black hole mass.

\(G\): gravitational constant.

\(c\): speed of light.

Physical Meaning

For an ideal Kerr black hole, \(0 \leq \chi \leq 1\). Higher spin means stronger frame dragging, a smaller prograde ISCO (innermost stable circular orbit), and more rotational energy available outside the horizon.
