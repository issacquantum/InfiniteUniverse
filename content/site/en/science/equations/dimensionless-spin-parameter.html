Dimensionless Spin Parameter

Equation

$$\chi = \frac{cJ}{GM^2}$$

Literal Reading

Read it as: chi equals c times angular momentum divided by G times mass squared.

Common Reading

The normalized spin of a Kerr black hole.

Symbols

\(\chi\): dimensionless spin parameter.

\(J\): angular momentum.

\(M\): black hole mass.

\(G\): gravitational constant.

\(c\): speed of light.

Meaning of the Equation

This relation states how the named quantities depend on each other. Its purpose is to turn a conceptual statement into a rule that can be calculated, compared, or tested inside the model where it is used.

Scope and Limits

The parameter is defined for Kerr-like compact objects. Measurements of \(\chi\) depend on model assumptions about disks, spectra, or gravitational waves, so the number is physical but not directly visible by itself.
