Euler Global Error Order

Equation

$$ \|e_N\|=O(h) $$

Literal Reading

global error equals O of h.

Common Reading

If the step size is cut in half, the final Euler error should roughly cut in half for a smooth stable problem.

Symbols

\(\|e_N\|\): magnitude of the final accumulated error.

\(O(h)\): first-order scaling with step size.

\(h\): numerical time step.

Meaning of the Equation

Euler is first order: for smooth stable problems, shrinking the step size improves the final error roughly linearly. It is simple and useful, but accuracy improves slowly compared with higher-order methods.
