Euler Global Error Order

Equation

$$ \|e_N\|=O(h) $$

Literal Reading

The total error after many Euler steps scales linearly with the step size.

Physicist's Reading

If the step size is cut in half, the final Euler error should roughly cut in half for a smooth stable problem.

Symbol Breakdown

\(\|e_N\|\): magnitude of the final accumulated error.

\(O(h)\): first-order scaling with step size.

\(h\): numerical time step.

Physical Meaning

Euler is useful for teaching and quick estimates, but it becomes inaccurate quickly when dynamics bend strongly between steps.
