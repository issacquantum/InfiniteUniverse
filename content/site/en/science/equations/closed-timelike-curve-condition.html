Closed Timelike Curve Condition

Equation

$$ \Delta t_{\rm mouths} > \frac{L}{c} $$

What the Formula Says

If the time shift between mouths exceeds the light-travel time through ordinary space, a closed timelike route can appear.

What It Means in Practice

A traversable wormhole with differently aged mouths can become a time-machine spacetime unless chronology protection prevents it.

Symbols

\(\Delta t_{\rm mouths}\): time offset between the two wormhole mouths.

\(L\): ordinary spatial separation between mouths.

\(c\): speed of light.

Equation Meaning

This is a schematic causality condition, not a construction manual. It explains why wormholes immediately raise questions about closed timelike curves and chronology protection.
