Vacuum Energy Integral

Equation

$$\rho_{\mathrm{vac}}\sim \frac{1}{2}\int\frac{d^3k}{(2\pi)^3}\hbar\omega_k$$

Literal Reading

rho sub vac equals of 1 over 2 integral d to the power of 3k over of 2 pi to the power of 3 h minus bar ω sub k.

Common Reading

The formal zero point energy contribution of quantum fields.

Symbols

\(\rho_{\mathrm{vac}}\): vacuum energy density.

\(k\): momentum mode.

\(\omega_k\): mode frequency.

\(\hbar\): reduced Planck constant.

Meaning of the Equation

The formal sum over zero-point modes diverges without a cutoff or renormalization. When gravity is included, this becomes part of the cosmological-constant problem.
