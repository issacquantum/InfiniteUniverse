Hydrogen Energy Levels

Equation

$$ E_n = -\frac{13.6 \text{ eV}}{n^2}, \quad n = 1, 2, 3, \ldots $$

Literal Reading

Read it as: e sub n equals minus thirteen point six electron volts over n squared, where n equals one, two, three, and so on.

Common Reading

The bound-state energy of hydrogen is minus thirteen point six electron volts divided by the square of the principal quantum number.

Symbols

\(E_n\): the allowed energy of the state labeled by \(n\).

\(13.6\,\text{eV}\): the ground-state binding energy of hydrogen.

\(n\): the principal quantum number, which can take the values 1, 2, 3, and so on.

Meaning of the Equation

This relation connects the mathematical state of a physical system to what can happen next or what can be measured. It is not just notation: it states how energy, fields, waves, or probabilities constrain the behavior of the system.
