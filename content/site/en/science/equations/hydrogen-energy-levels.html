Hydrogen Energy Levels

Equation

$$ E_n = -\frac{13.6 \text{ eV}}{n^2}, \quad n = 1, 2, 3, \ldots $$

What the Formula Says

E sub n equals minus thirteen point six electron volts over n squared, where n equals one, two, three, and so on.

What It Means in Practice

This means the bound-state energy of hydrogen is minus thirteen point six electron volts divided by the square of the principal quantum number.

Symbols

\(E_n\): the allowed energy of the state labeled by \(n\).

\(13.6\,\text{eV}\): the ground-state binding energy of hydrogen.

\(n\): the principal quantum number, which can take the values 1, 2, 3, and so on.

Why It Matters

The electron in hydrogen cannot have arbitrary energy. It can only occupy a discrete ladder of bound energies that approach zero as \(n\) increases.
