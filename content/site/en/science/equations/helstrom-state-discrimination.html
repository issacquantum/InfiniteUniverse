Helstrom State Discrimination

Equation

$$ P_{\mathrm{succ}}^{\max}=\frac{1}{2}\left(1+D(\rho_0,\rho_1)\right) $$

Literal Reading

The maximum success probability equals one half times one plus the trace distance between rho zero and rho one.

Physicist's Reading

A physicist would read this as the best possible single-shot discrimination probability for two equally likely quantum states.

Symbol Breakdown

\(P_{\mathrm{succ}}^{\max}\): maximum probability of guessing the state correctly.

\(\rho_0,\rho_1\): the two possible quantum states.

\(D(\rho_0,\rho_1)\): the trace distance between the two states.

Physical Meaning

Non-orthogonal quantum states cannot be perfectly identified from one copy. The Helstrom bound gives the best possible success rate allowed by quantum mechanics, no matter how clever the measurement is.
