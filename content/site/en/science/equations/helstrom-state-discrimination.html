Helstrom State Discrimination

Equation

$$ P_{\mathrm{succ}}^{\max}=\frac{1}{2}\left(1+D(\rho_0,\rho_1)\right) $$

Literal Reading

The maximum success probability equals one half times one plus the trace distance between rho zero and rho one.

Common Reading

Short version: The best possible single-shot discrimination probability for two equally likely quantum states.

Symbols

\(P_{\mathrm{succ}}^{\max}\): maximum probability of guessing the state correctly.

\(\rho_0,\rho_1\): the two possible quantum states.

\(D(\rho_0,\rho_1)\): the trace distance between the two states.

Meaning of the Equation

The best possible single-shot discrimination probability for two equally likely quantum states.
