Free Particle Wavefunction

Equation

$$ \psi(\mathbf{r}) = Ae^{i\mathbf{k}\cdot\mathbf{r}} $$

What the Formula Says

Psi of r equals A times e to the i k dot r.

What It Means in Practice

This means a plane-wave solution for a free particle with wavevector k.

Symbols

\(\psi(\mathbf{r})\): the particle's wavefunction.

\(A\): the amplitude of the wave.

\(e^{i\mathbf{k}\cdot\mathbf{r}}\): the complex plane-wave phase factor.

\(\mathbf{k}\): the wavevector, related to momentum.

\(\mathbf{r}\): the position vector.

Why It Matters

A free particle with definite momentum is described by a plane wave that extends through space rather than by a sharply localized point.
