Free Particle Wavefunction

Equation

$$ \psi(\mathbf{r}) = Ae^{i\mathbf{k}\cdot\mathbf{r}} $$

Literal Reading

psi of x comma t equals A e to the power of i of kx minus ωt.

Common Reading

A plane-wave solution for a free particle with wavevector k.

Symbols

\(\psi(\mathbf{r})\): the particle's wavefunction.

\(A\): the amplitude of the wave.

\(e^{i\mathbf{k}\cdot\mathbf{r}}\): the complex plane-wave phase factor.

\(\mathbf{k}\): the wavevector, related to momentum.

\(\mathbf{r}\): the position vector.

Meaning of the Equation

A free quantum particle can be represented as a traveling complex wave.
