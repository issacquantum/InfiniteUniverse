Comparison Sort Lower Bound

Equation

$$ \Omega(n\log_2 n) $$

What the Formula Says

Any comparison sort requires at least on the order of \(n\log_2 n\) comparisons in the worst case.

What It Means in Practice

This is an information limit: sorting must distinguish among \(n!\) possible input orders, and comparisons reveal limited information.

Symbols

\(\Omega\): asymptotic lower bound.

\(n\): number of items being sorted.

\(\log_2 n\): logarithmic growth measured in binary decisions.

Why It Matters

Algorithms such as mergesort and heapsort are not just fast in practice; for comparison sorting, their \(O(n\log n)\) scaling is asymptotically optimal.
