Comparison Sort Lower Bound

Equation

$$ \Omega(n\log_2 n) $$

Literal Reading

omega of n log n.

Common Reading

This is an information limit: sorting must distinguish among \(n!\) possible input orders, and comparisons reveal limited information.

Symbols

\(\Omega\): asymptotic lower bound.

\(n\): number of items being sorted.

\(\log_2 n\): logarithmic growth measured in binary decisions.

Meaning of the Equation

There are too many possible orderings for fewer comparisons to distinguish in the worst case. This is why comparison sorting cannot generally beat n log n scaling.
