Neutron Quark Composition

Equation

$$n = udd$$

What the Formula Says

n equals u d d.

What It Means in Practice

This means a neutron is composed of one up quark and two down quarks.

Symbols

\(n\): neutron.

\(u\): up quark.

\(d\): down quark.

Why It Matters

This is the quark-content shorthand for the neutron. Like the proton, it is a baryon made of three valence quarks held together by the strong interaction.
