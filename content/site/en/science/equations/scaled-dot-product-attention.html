Scaled Dot Product Attention

Equation

$$ \operatorname{Attention}(Q,K,V)=\operatorname{softmax}\!\left(\frac{QK^T}{\sqrt{d_k}}\right)V $$

Literal Reading

Read it as: attention equals softmax of Q K transpose divided by the square root of d k, multiplied by V.

Common Reading

The model compares queries with keys to decide how strongly each value should contribute to the next representation.

Symbols

\(Q\): query matrix.

\(K\): key matrix.

\(V\): value matrix.

\(d_k\): key dimension, used to stabilize the scale of dot products.

\(\operatorname{softmax}\): converts scores into weights that sum to one.

Meaning of the Equation

This relation turns an algorithmic step into a precise rule. It identifies what is being updated, counted, or bounded, so the process can be checked by its state, cost, error, or learning behavior instead of only by a visual animation.
