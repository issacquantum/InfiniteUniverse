Morris Thorne Wormhole Metric

Equation

$$ ds^2=-e^{2\Phi(r)}c^2dt^2+\frac{dr^2}{1-b(r)/r}+r^2d\Omega^2 $$

Literal Reading

The spacetime interval is built from a time term controlled by \(\Phi(r)\), a radial term controlled by \(b(r)\), and the angular spherical term.

Common Reading

This is a static spherical wormhole geometry. The functions \(\Phi(r)\) and \(b(r)\) determine redshift and throat shape.

Symbols

\(ds^2\): spacetime interval.

\(\Phi(r)\): redshift function.

\(b(r)\): shape function.

\(r\): radial coordinate.

\(d\Omega^2\): angular part of a sphere.
