Morris Thorne Wormhole Metric

Equation

$$ ds^2=-e^{2\Phi(r)}c^2dt^2+\frac{dr^2}{1-b(r)/r}+r^2d\Omega^2 $$

Literal Reading

ds to the power of 2 equals minus e to the power of 2Φ of r c to the power of 2dt to the power of 2 plus dr to the power of 2 over of 1 minus b of r over r plus r to the power of 2d omega to the power of 2.

Common Reading

This is a static spherical wormhole geometry.

Symbols

\(ds^2\): spacetime interval.

\(\Phi(r)\): redshift function.

\(b(r)\): shape function.

\(r\): radial coordinate.

\(d\Omega^2\): angular part of a sphere.

Meaning of the Equation

This metric is a static spherical ansatz for describing a wormhole throat. Its functions control redshift, throat shape, and whether horizons appear.
