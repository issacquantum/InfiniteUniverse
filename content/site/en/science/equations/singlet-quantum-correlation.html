Singlet Quantum Correlation

Equation

$$ E_Q(a,b)=-\mathbf{a}\cdot\mathbf{b}=-\cos\theta_{ab} $$

Literal Reading

Read it as: the quantum correlation E sub Q for detector directions a and b equals negative a dot b, which equals negative cosine of the angle between a and b.

Common Reading

The exact quantum prediction for spin or polarization correlations in the Bell singlet state.

Symbols

\(E_Q(a,b)\): the quantum-mechanical expectation value of the product of the two measurement results.

\(\mathbf{a},\mathbf{b}\): unit vectors pointing along the two detector directions.

\(\mathbf{a}\cdot\mathbf{b}\): the dot product measuring how aligned the two directions are.

\(\theta_{ab}\): the angle between the two detector directions.

\(-\cos\theta_{ab}\): the singlet anticorrelation law.

Meaning of the Equation

This relation describes a quantum state, transformation, probability, or correlation. It matters because quantum systems are not described by one definite classical value; the equation tells what can be predicted from amplitudes, operators, or density matrices.
