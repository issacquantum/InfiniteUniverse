Singlet Quantum Correlation

Equation

$$ E_Q(a,b)=-\mathbf{a}\cdot\mathbf{b}=-\cos\theta_{ab} $$

Literal Reading

The quantum correlation E sub Q for detector directions a and b equals negative a dot b, which equals negative cosine of the angle between a and b.

Physicist's Reading

A physicist would read it as the exact quantum prediction for spin or polarization correlations in the Bell singlet state.

Symbol Breakdown

\(E_Q(a,b)\): the quantum-mechanical expectation value of the product of the two measurement results.

\(\mathbf{a},\mathbf{b}\): unit vectors pointing along the two detector directions.

\(\mathbf{a}\cdot\mathbf{b}\): the dot product measuring how aligned the two directions are.

\(\theta_{ab}\): the angle between the two detector directions.

\(-\cos\theta_{ab}\): the singlet anticorrelation law.

Physical Meaning

When both observers measure along the same direction, \(\theta_{ab}=0\), so \(E_Q=-1\): the results are perfectly opposite. At a right angle, \(E_Q=0\): the results are uncorrelated. For carefully chosen intermediate angles, this correlation pattern violates Bell inequalities. That violation is Bell nonlocality: the joint statistics cannot be reproduced by local hidden instructions, even though no usable faster-than-light signal is sent.
