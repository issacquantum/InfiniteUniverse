Electromagnetic Wave Speed

Equation

$$c=\frac{1}{\sqrt{\mu_0\epsilon_0}}$$

Literal Reading

c equals 1 over square root of mu 0 times epsilon 0.

Common Reading

In vacuum, the electric and magnetic constants combine to give the speed of electromagnetic waves.

Symbols

  • \(c\): speed of light in vacuum.
  • \(\mu_0\): vacuum permeability.
  • \(\epsilon_0\): vacuum permittivity.

Meaning of the Equation

The constants set how strongly electric and magnetic fields respond to each other in empty space. Their combination fixes a propagation speed, showing why light is not separate from electromagnetism.
