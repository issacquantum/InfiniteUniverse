FLRW (Friedmann-Lemaitre-Robertson-Walker) Comoving Proper Time

Equation

$$ dr=d\theta=d\phi=0,\qquad ds^2=-c^2dt^2=-c^2d\tau^2,\qquad d\tau=dt $$

What the Formula Says

If an observer stays at fixed comoving spatial coordinates in an FLRW universe, the spacetime interval reduces to the same form as the proper-time interval.

What It Means in Practice

Cosmic time in FLRW cosmology is not universal Newtonian time. It is the proper time measured by observers moving with the average cosmic expansion.

Symbols

\(dr=d\theta=d\phi=0\): the observer has no motion through the comoving spatial coordinates.

\(ds^2\): invariant spacetime interval.

\(d\tau\): proper time measured by the observer's own clock.

\(dt\): FLRW cosmic-time coordinate.

Equation Meaning

The equation explains why FLRW cosmology can use a clean cosmic clock: symmetry selects a preferred family of comoving observers whose proper time matches the cosmic time coordinate.
