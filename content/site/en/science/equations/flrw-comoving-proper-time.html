FLRW (Friedmann-Lemaitre-Robertson-Walker) Comoving Proper Time

Equation

$$ dr=d\theta=d\phi=0,\qquad ds^2=-c^2dt^2=-c^2d\tau^2,\qquad d\tau=dt $$

Literal Reading

Read it as: if an observer stays at fixed comoving spatial coordinates in an FLRW universe, the spacetime interval reduces to the same form as the proper-time interval.

Common Reading

Cosmic time in FLRW cosmology is not universal Newtonian time.

Symbols

\(dr=d\theta=d\phi=0\): the observer has no motion through the comoving spatial coordinates.

\(ds^2\): invariant spacetime interval.

\(d\tau\): proper time measured by the observer's own clock.

\(dt\): FLRW cosmic-time coordinate.

Meaning of the Equation

This relation connects geometry, motion, horizons, or gravitational sources. It tells what clocks, distances, curvature, or observers would measure, so the physical claim is tied to spacetime structure rather than to a picture or metaphor.
