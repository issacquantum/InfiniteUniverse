FLRW (Friedmann-Lemaitre-Robertson-Walker) Comoving Proper Time

Equation

$$ dr=d\theta=d\phi=0,\qquad ds^2=-c^2dt^2=-c^2d\tau^2,\qquad d\tau=dt $$

Literal Reading

Read it as: if the comoving spatial coordinates do not change, the FLRW interval reduces so that proper time equals cosmic time.

Common Reading

Cosmic time in FLRW cosmology is not universal Newtonian time.

Symbols

\(dr=d\theta=d\phi=0\): the observer has no motion through the comoving spatial coordinates.

\(ds^2\): invariant spacetime interval.

\(d\tau\): proper time measured by the observer's own clock.

\(dt\): FLRW cosmic-time coordinate.

Meaning of the Equation

For observers carried with the cosmic expansion, the FLRW time coordinate equals the time measured by their own ideal local clocks. This is why cosmic time is useful in that model, but it depends on the symmetry of FLRW spacetime and on choosing comoving observers.
