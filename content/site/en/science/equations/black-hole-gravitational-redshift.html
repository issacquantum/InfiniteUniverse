Black Hole Gravitational Redshift

Equation

$$\nu_\infty = \nu_{\text{local}}\sqrt{1 - \frac{2GM}{rc^2}}$$

What the Formula Says

The frequency measured far away equals the local frequency times the square root of one minus two G M over r c squared.

What It Means in Practice

This means light losing frequency as it climbs out of the Schwarzschild gravitational field.

Symbols

\(\nu_\infty\): frequency measured far from the black hole.

\(\nu_{\text{local}}\): frequency measured near the emitting source.

\(r\): radius of emission.

\(M\): black hole mass.

Why It Matters

As emission happens closer to the horizon, the signal received far away is increasingly redshifted. At the horizon in these coordinates, the far observer sees the signal fade toward zero frequency.

Scope and Limits

This is the static Schwarzschild redshift for an emitter held at fixed radius. It does not describe all Doppler shifts from orbiting plasma, infalling matter, or rotating Kerr geometry.
