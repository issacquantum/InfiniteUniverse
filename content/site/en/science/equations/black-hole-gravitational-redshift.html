Black Hole Gravitational Redshift

Equation

$$\nu_\infty = \nu_{\text{local}}\sqrt{1 - \frac{2GM}{rc^2}}$$

Literal Reading

Read it as: 1 plus z equals 1 over square root open parenthesis 1 minus r sub s over r close parenthesis.

Common Reading

Light losing frequency as it climbs out of the Schwarzschild gravitational field.

Symbols

\(\nu_\infty\): frequency measured far from the black hole.

\(\nu_{\text{local}}\): frequency measured near the emitting source.

\(r\): radius of emission.

\(M\): black hole mass.

Meaning of the Equation

Light climbing out of gravity loses frequency and is redshifted.

Scope and Limits

This is the static Schwarzschild redshift for an emitter held at fixed radius. It does not describe all Doppler shifts from orbiting plasma, infalling matter, or rotating Kerr geometry.
