Molecular Formation

Equation

$$\mathrm{H}+\mathrm{H}\rightarrow \mathrm{H}_2\quad\text{(schematic)}$$

Literal Reading

H plus H goes to H two, schematically.

Common Reading

Hydrogen atoms can form molecular hydrogen, shown here as a simplified net picture.

Symbols

\(\mathrm{H}\): atomic hydrogen.

\(\mathrm{H}_2\): molecular hydrogen.

schematic: the displayed reaction suppresses intermediate steps.

Meaning of the Equation

This is schematic; actual primordial \(H_2\) formation proceeds mainly through intermediate ions such as \(H^-\) or \(H_2^+\).
