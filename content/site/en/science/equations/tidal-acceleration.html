Tidal Acceleration

Equation

$$\Delta a \sim \frac{2GM}{r^3}\Delta r$$

Literal Reading

The difference in acceleration is approximately two G M over r cubed times the separation distance.

Physicist's Reading

A physicist reads this as an estimate of how differently gravity pulls on two nearby points.

Symbol Breakdown

\(\Delta a\): difference in acceleration.

\(\Delta r\): separation between two points.

\(M\): mass causing the gravitational field.

\(r\): distance from the mass.

Physical Meaning

Tidal acceleration is what stretches and compresses infalling matter. It grows rapidly as radius decreases, which is why small black holes can be violently destructive near the horizon.

Scope and Limit

This is an order-of-magnitude Newtonian tidal estimate. Precise relativistic tidal effects are described by curvature tensors and depend on the observer's frame.
