Tidal Acceleration

Equation

$$\Delta a \sim \frac{2GM}{r^3}\Delta r$$

Literal Reading

Delta a approximately equal to 2GMℓ over r to the power of 3.

Common Reading

An estimate of how differently gravity pulls on two nearby points.

Symbols

\(\Delta a\): difference in acceleration.

\(\Delta r\): separation between two points.

\(M\): mass causing the gravitational field.

\(r\): distance from the mass.

Meaning of the Equation

A body is stretched when gravity is noticeably stronger on one side than the other. The equation captures gravity's gradient, which matters near compact objects even when a small object feels weightless at its center.

Scope and Limits

This is an order-of-magnitude Newtonian tidal estimate. Precise relativistic tidal effects are described by curvature tensors and depend on the observer's frame.
