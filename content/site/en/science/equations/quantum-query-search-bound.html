Quantum Query Search Bound

Equation

$$ Q_{\mathrm{search}}(N)=\Theta(\sqrt{N}) $$

Literal Reading

omega of square root of N.

Common Reading

Grover search gives a quadratic speedup, and that speedup is optimal for black-box search.

Symbols

\(Q_{\mathrm{search}}(N)\): number of oracle queries needed by a quantum search algorithm.

\(N\): number of possible items.

\(\Theta(\sqrt{N})\): both upper and lower bounds scale like \(\sqrt{N}\), up to constant factors.

Meaning of the Equation

Unstructured quantum search still needs on the order of square root of N queries.
