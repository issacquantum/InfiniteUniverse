Quantum Query Search Bound

Equation

$$ Q_{\mathrm{search}}(N)=\Theta(\sqrt{N}) $$

Literal Reading

Read it as: the quantum query complexity of unstructured search over \(N\) items grows on the order of the square root of \(N\).

Common Reading

Grover search gives a quadratic speedup, and that speedup is optimal for black-box search.

Symbols

\(Q_{\mathrm{search}}(N)\): number of oracle queries needed by a quantum search algorithm.

\(N\): number of possible items.

\(\Theta(\sqrt{N})\): both upper and lower bounds scale like \(\sqrt{N}\), up to constant factors.

Meaning of the Equation

Quantum interference can reduce an unstructured search from linear query cost to square-root query cost. The equation also says there is no hidden exponential improvement in this black-box setting: any quantum algorithm still needs on the order of \(\sqrt{N}\) queries.
