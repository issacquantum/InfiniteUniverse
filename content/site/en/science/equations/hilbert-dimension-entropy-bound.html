Hilbert Dimension Entropy Bound

Equation

$$ \dim \mathcal{H}_R \lesssim e^{S_{\max}/k_B} $$

Literal Reading

Read it as: the dimension of the Hilbert space for region R is less than or approximately bounded by e to the maximum entropy divided by Boltzmann constant.

Common Reading

Entropy counting the number of distinguishable quantum states available to a region.

Symbols

\(\dim \mathcal{H}_R\): the number of independent quantum states available to region \(R\).

\(S_{\max}\): the maximum entropy allowed for that region.

\(k_B\): Boltzmann constant.

\(\lesssim\): less than or approximately bounded by.

Meaning of the Equation

If entropy bounds count the maximum number of distinguishable states in a finite region, then the independent Hilbert-space states available to that region are also bounded. This is an information-capacity statement about a region, not a proof that the whole of reality is finite.
