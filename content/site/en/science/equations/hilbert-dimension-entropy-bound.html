Hilbert Dimension Entropy Bound

Equation

$$ \dim \mathcal{H}_R \lesssim e^{S_{\max}/k_B} $$

Literal Reading

The dimension of the Hilbert space for region R is less than or approximately bounded by the exponential of the maximum entropy divided by Boltzmann's constant.

Common Reading

Short version: Entropy counting the number of distinguishable quantum states available to a region. If a region has a maximum entropy, it cannot contain arbitrarily many independent states.

Symbols

\(\dim \mathcal{H}_R\): the number of independent quantum states available to region \(R\).

\(S_{\max}\): the maximum entropy allowed for that region.

\(k_B\): Boltzmann constant.

\(\lesssim\): less than or approximately bounded by.

Meaning of the Equation
