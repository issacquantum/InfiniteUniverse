Holevo Information

Equation

$$ \chi = S\!\left(\sum_x p_x\rho_x\right) - \sum_x p_x S(\rho_x) $$

What the Formula Says

Chi equals the entropy of the average state minus the average entropy of the states.

What It Means in Practice

This means the maximum classical information available from an ensemble of quantum states is bounded by the difference between the entropy of the average state and the average entropy of the individual states.

Symbols

\(\chi\): the Holevo information.

\(p_x\): the probability of preparing state \(\rho_x\).

\(\rho_x\): one possible quantum state in the ensemble.

\(\sum_x p_x\rho_x\): the average quantum state.

\(S\): von Neumann entropy.

Why It Matters

Quantum states can encode rich structure, but measurement limits how much classical information can be extracted. Holevo information gives a precise upper bound for that accessible information, making it central to quantum communication theory.
