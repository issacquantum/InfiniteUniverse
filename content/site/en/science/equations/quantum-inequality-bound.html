Quantum Inequality Bound

Equation

$$ \int_{-\infty}^{\infty} \rho(\tau) f(\tau)\,d\tau \ge -\frac{C}{\tau_0^4} $$

Literal Reading

integral rho open parenthesis τ close parenthesis f open parenthesis τ close parenthesis dτ greater than or equal to minus C over τ0 to the power of 4.

Common Reading

Quantum field theory allows local negative energy in special states, but it places strict magnitude-duration limits on it.

Symbols

\(\rho(\tau)\): energy density measured along an observer's worldline.

\(f(\tau)\): sampling function over a timescale \(\tau_0\).

\(C\): positive constant depending on the field and sampling choice.

Meaning of the Equation

Quantum theory restricts the amount and duration of negative energy.
