Definite Integral

Equation

$$\int_a^b f(x)\,dx$$

Literal Reading

integral sub a to the power of b f of x dx.

Common Reading

This adds up many tiny contributions of \(f(x)\) from \(a\) to \(b\).

Symbols

  • \(a,b\): the starting and ending limits.
  • \(f(x)\): the quantity being accumulated.
  • \(dx\): a tiny piece of the input axis.

Meaning of the Equation

A definite integral turns a changing rate or density into a total over an interval. It is the continuous version of adding many small pieces, so it appears in area, work, probability, charge, and accumulated change.
