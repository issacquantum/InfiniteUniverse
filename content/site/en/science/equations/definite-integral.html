Definite Integral

Equation

$$\int_a^b f(x)\,dx$$

Literal Reading

Read it as: integral sub a to the power of b f open parenthesis x close parenthesis dx.

Common Reading

This adds up many tiny contributions of \(f(x)\) from \(a\) to \(b\).

Symbols

  • \(a,b\): the starting and ending limits.
  • \(f(x)\): the quantity being accumulated.
  • \(dx\): a tiny piece of the input axis.

Meaning of the Equation

A definite integral adds up continuously many contributions over an interval.
