Definite Integral

Equation

$$\int_a^b f(x)\,dx$$

Literal Reading

Read it as: the integral from a to b of f of x, d x.

Common Reading

This adds up many tiny contributions of \(f(x)\) from \(a\) to \(b\).

Symbols

  • \(a,b\): the starting and ending limits.
  • \(f(x)\): the quantity being accumulated.
  • \(dx\): a tiny piece of the input axis.

Meaning of the Equation

This relation defines how one mathematical quantity is built from another. It gives a rule for change, accumulation, transformation, or iteration, which is why the same structure can be used inside physics, computation, and modeling.
