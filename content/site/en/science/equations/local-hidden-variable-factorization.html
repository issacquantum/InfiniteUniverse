Local Hidden Variable Factorization

Equation

$$ P(A,B|a,b,\lambda)=P(A|a,\lambda)P(B|b,\lambda) $$

Literal Reading

P of A comma B given a comma b comma lambda equals P of A given a comma lambda times P of B given b comma lambda.

Common Reading

A local hidden-variable model factorizes distant outcomes once the local settings and shared hidden variable are fixed.

Symbols

\(A,B\): the two measurement outcomes recorded by the distant observers.

\(a,b\): the detector settings or measurement directions chosen on each side.

\(\lambda\): hidden information shared by the pair before measurement.

\(P(A,B|a,b,\lambda)\): the joint probability of getting both outcomes after fixing the settings and hidden variable.

\(P(A|a,\lambda)P(B|b,\lambda)\): the product form required by locality.

Meaning of the Equation

This factorization assumes locality plus measurement independence, with outcomes conditioned on the shared hidden variable \(\lambda\).
