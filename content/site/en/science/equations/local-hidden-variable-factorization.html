Local Hidden Variable Factorization

Equation

$$ P(A,B|a,b,\lambda)=P(A|a,\lambda)P(B|b,\lambda) $$

Literal Reading

The probability of outcomes A and B, given detector settings a and b and hidden variable lambda, equals the probability of A given a and lambda times the probability of B given b and lambda.

Physicist's Reading

A physicist would read this as the locality condition for hidden-variable theories: once the shared hidden information is specified, the result on one side does not depend on the distant detector setting.

Symbol Breakdown

\(A,B\): the two measurement outcomes recorded by the distant observers.

\(a,b\): the detector settings or measurement directions chosen on each side.

\(\lambda\): hidden information shared by the pair before measurement.

\(P(A,B|a,b,\lambda)\): the joint probability of getting both outcomes after fixing the settings and hidden variable.

\(P(A|a,\lambda)P(B|b,\lambda)\): the product form required by locality.

Physical Meaning

This equation is the clean mathematical statement of local hidden variables. It says that any correlation between A and B must come from shared information \(\lambda\), not from one measurement depending on the distant setting. Bell's theorem shows that this assumption forces Bell inequalities. Experiments violate those inequalities, so nature cannot be described by this local factorized model.
