Photoelectric Equation

Equation

$$ K_{\max} = h\nu - \phi $$

Literal Reading

K max equals h nu minus phi.

Physicist's Reading

A physicist would read it as the maximum kinetic energy of the emitted electron equals the photon energy minus the work function.

Symbol Breakdown

\(K_{\max}\): the maximum kinetic energy of the emitted electron.

\(h\nu\): the energy of the incoming photon.

\(\phi\): the work function, the minimum energy required to free the electron from the material.

Physical Meaning

A photon gives its energy to one electron. First the electron pays the escape cost, and any remaining energy becomes kinetic energy.
