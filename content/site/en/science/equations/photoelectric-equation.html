Photoelectric Equation

Equation

$$ K_{\max} = h\nu - \phi $$

Literal Reading

K max equals h nu minus phi.

Common Reading

Short version: The maximum kinetic energy of the emitted electron equals the photon energy minus the work function.

Symbols

\(K_{\max}\): the maximum kinetic energy of the emitted electron.

\(h\nu\): the energy of the incoming photon.

\(\phi\): the work function, the minimum energy required to free the electron from the material.

Meaning of the Equation

The maximum kinetic energy of the emitted electron equals the photon energy minus the work function.
