Hardy-Weinberg Equilibrium

Equation

$$p^2+2pq+q^2=1$$

Plain Meaning

If assumptions are ideal, allele frequencies predict genotype frequencies in the next generation.

Symbols

  • \(p\): frequency of one allele.
  • \(q\): frequency of the other allele.
  • \(p^2,2pq,q^2\): genotype frequencies.

How to Read It

It is a null model: useful because deviations reveal selection, drift, migration, mutation, or nonrandom mating.

Meaning in Context

The equation gives genotype frequencies expected under ideal mating and inheritance assumptions. Deviations become informative about selection, drift, migration, mutation, or nonrandom mating.
