Zero-Point Position Variance

Equation

$$ \langle 0|\hat{x}^2|0\rangle = \frac{\hbar}{2m\omega} $$

Literal Reading

The expectation value of x squared in the ground state equals h bar over two m omega.

Common Reading

Even in the harmonic oscillator ground state, position has nonzero spread: \(\langle x^2\rangle_0=\hbar/(2m\omega)\).

Symbols

\(|0\rangle\): the oscillator ground state.

\(\hat{x}\): the position operator.

\(\hbar\): reduced Planck constant.

\(m\): oscillator mass.

\(\omega\): angular frequency of the oscillator.

Meaning of the Equation

Even the lowest-energy oscillator cannot sit at one exact position. Quantum uncertainty leaves an irreducible spread, which is different from thermal motion caused by heat.
