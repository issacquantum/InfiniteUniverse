Zero-Point Position Variance

Equation

$$ \langle 0|\hat{x}^2|0\rangle = \frac{\hbar}{2m\omega} $$

What the Formula Says

The ground-state expectation value of x squared equals h-bar divided by two m omega.

What It Means in Practice

This means the irreducible position spread of a quantum harmonic oscillator in its ground state.

Symbols

\(|0\rangle\): the oscillator ground state.

\(\hat{x}\): the position operator.

\(\hbar\): reduced Planck constant.

\(m\): oscillator mass.

\(\omega\): angular frequency of the oscillator.

Why It Matters

The ground state is not motionless in the classical sense. Even at the lowest allowed energy, the oscillator has a nonzero spread in position. Field modes inherit this structure in quantum field theory.
