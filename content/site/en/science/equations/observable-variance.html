Observable Variance

Equation

$$ (\Delta A)^2 = \langle A^2\rangle - \langle A\rangle^2 $$

Literal Reading

open parenthesis Delta A close parenthesis to the power of 2 equals < A to the power of 2 > minus < A > to the power of 2.

Common Reading

The quantitative definition of a quantum fluctuation in an observable.

Symbols

\(A\): an observable represented by an operator.

\(\langle A\rangle\): the expectation value of that observable in a given quantum state.

\(\langle A^2\rangle\): the expectation value of the squared observable.

\(\Delta A\): the standard deviation, or spread, of possible measurement outcomes.

Meaning of the Equation

Variance measures the spread of possible measurement outcomes.
