Observable Variance

Equation

$$ (\Delta A)^2 = \langle A^2\rangle - \langle A\rangle^2 $$

What the Formula Says

The variance of A equals the expectation value of A squared minus the square of the expectation value of A.

What It Means in Practice

This means the quantitative definition of a quantum fluctuation in an observable.

Symbols

\(A\): an observable represented by an operator.

\(\langle A\rangle\): the expectation value of that observable in a given quantum state.

\(\langle A^2\rangle\): the expectation value of the squared observable.

\(\Delta A\): the standard deviation, or spread, of possible measurement outcomes.

Why It Matters

A quantum state can be perfectly prepared and still have nonzero variance. That is what a fluctuation means here: not experimental sloppiness, but irreducible spread in possible outcomes.
