Dijkstra Relaxation

Equation

$$ d[v] > d[u]+w(u,v) $$

Literal Reading

Read it as: the current distance to v is greater than the distance to u plus the edge weight from u to v.

Common Reading

If passing through \(u\) gives a shorter route to \(v\), the algorithm updates the best known distance.

Symbols

\(d[v]\): best known distance to node \(v\).

\(d[u]\): best known distance to node \(u\).

\(w(u,v)\): weight of the edge from \(u\) to \(v\).

Meaning of the Equation

This relation turns an algorithmic step into a precise rule. It identifies what is being updated, counted, or bounded, so the process can be checked by its state, cost, error, or learning behavior instead of only by a visual animation.
