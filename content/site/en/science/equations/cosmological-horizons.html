Cosmological Horizons

Equation

$$d_p(t)=a(t)\int_0^t\frac{c\,dt'}{a(t')},\qquad d_e(t)=a(t)\int_t^\infty\frac{c\,dt'}{a(t')}$$

Literal Reading

the particle horizon and event horizon are the scale factor times integrals of light travel through the expansion history.

Common Reading

These are limits on what could have influenced us and what we may ever influence or observe.

Symbols

\(d_p(t)\): particle horizon distance.

\(d_e(t)\): event horizon distance.

\(a(t)\): scale factor.

Meaning of the Equation

These integrals fold the travel of light through cosmic expansion. The particle horizon asks how far signals could have reached us by time t; the event horizon asks what signals can ever reach us in the future, depending on the later expansion history.
