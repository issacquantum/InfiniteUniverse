Cosmological Horizons

Equation

$$d_p(t)=a(t)\int_0^t\frac{c\,dt'}{a(t')},\qquad d_e(t)=a(t)\int_t^\infty\frac{c\,dt'}{a(t')}$$

Literal Reading

Read it as: the particle horizon and event horizon are scale factor times integrals over light travel through expansion.

Common Reading

These are limits on what could have influenced us and what we may ever influence or observe.

Symbols

\(d_p(t)\): particle horizon distance.

\(d_e(t)\): event horizon distance.

\(a(t)\): scale factor.

Meaning of the Equation

This relation connects geometry, motion, horizons, or gravitational sources. It tells what clocks, distances, curvature, or observers would measure, so the physical claim is tied to spacetime structure rather than to a picture or metaphor.
