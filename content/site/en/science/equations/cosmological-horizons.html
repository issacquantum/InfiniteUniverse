Cosmological Horizons

Equation

$$d_p(t)=a(t)\int_0^t\frac{c\,dt'}{a(t')},\qquad d_e(t)=a(t)\int_t^\infty\frac{c\,dt'}{a(t')}$$

What the Formula Says

The particle horizon and event horizon are scale factor times integrals over light travel through expansion.

What It Means in Practice

These are limits on what could have influenced us and what we may ever influence or observe.

Symbols

\(d_p(t)\): particle horizon distance.

\(d_e(t)\): event horizon distance.

\(a(t)\): scale factor.

Equation Meaning

Horizons separate global reality from observable reality. A universe can be larger than what any observer can ever access.
