Bayes' Theorem

Equation

$$P(A\mid B)=\frac{P(B\mid A)P(A)}{P(B)}$$

Literal Reading

Read it as: the probability of \(A\) given \(B\) equals the probability of \(B\) given \(A\), times the probability of \(A\), divided by the probability of \(B\).

Common Reading

Posterior belief equals likelihood times prior, normalized by evidence.

Symbols

\(P(A\mid B)\): updated probability of \(A\) after knowing \(B\).

\(P(B\mid A)\): likelihood of seeing \(B\) if \(A\) is true.

\(P(A)\): prior probability.

\(P(B)\): evidence normalization.

Meaning of the Equation

Bayes' theorem is a rule for changing a probability after evidence arrives. It separates the prior belief, the chance of seeing the evidence if the hypothesis were true, and the normalization needed so all possible answers still add up consistently.
