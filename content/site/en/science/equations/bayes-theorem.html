Bayes' Theorem

Equation

$$P(A\mid B)=\frac{P(B\mid A)P(A)}{P(B)}$$

Literal Reading

P of A vertical bar B equals P of B vertical bar A P of A over P of B.

Common Reading

The probability of a hypothesis after evidence equals the likelihood times the prior, normalized by the evidence probability.

Symbols

\(P(A\mid B)\): updated probability of \(A\) after knowing \(B\).

\(P(B\mid A)\): likelihood of seeing \(B\) if \(A\) is true.

\(P(A)\): prior probability.

\(P(B)\): evidence normalization.

Meaning of the Equation

It updates belief when new evidence arrives.
