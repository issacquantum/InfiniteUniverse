Bayes' Theorem

Equation

$$P(A\mid B)=\frac{P(B\mid A)P(A)}{P(B)}$$

Literal Reading

The probability of \(A\) given \(B\) equals the probability of \(B\) given \(A\), times the probability of \(A\), divided by the probability of \(B\).

Mathematical Reading

Bayes' theorem updates belief in a hypothesis after new evidence is observed.

Symbol Breakdown

\(P(A\mid B)\): updated probability of \(A\) after knowing \(B\).

\(P(B\mid A)\): likelihood of seeing \(B\) if \(A\) is true.

\(P(A)\): prior probability.

\(P(B)\): evidence normalization.

Meaning

It is central to inference, statistics, data analysis, machine learning, and scientific reasoning under uncertainty.
