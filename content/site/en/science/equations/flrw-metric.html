FLRW (Friedmann-Lemaitre-Robertson-Walker) Metric

Equation

$$ds^2 = -c^2dt^2 + a^2(t)\left[\frac{dr^2}{1-kr^2}+r^2d\Omega^2\right]$$

Literal Reading

Read it as: the interval equals a time part plus an expanding spatial geometry controlled by the scale factor a of t.

Common Reading

The standard metric for a homogeneous and isotropic universe.

Symbols

\(a(t)\): scale factor.

\(k\): spatial curvature parameter.

\(d\Omega^2\): angular part of the spatial metric.

Meaning of the Equation

This relation connects geometry, motion, horizons, or gravitational sources. It tells what clocks, distances, curvature, or observers would measure, so the physical claim is tied to spacetime structure rather than to a picture or metaphor.
