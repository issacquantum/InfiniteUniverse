FLRW (Friedmann-Lemaitre-Robertson-Walker) Metric

Equation

$$ds^2 = -c^2dt^2 + a^2(t)\left[\frac{dr^2}{1-kr^2}+r^2d\Omega^2\right]$$

What the Formula Says

The interval equals a time part plus an expanding spatial geometry controlled by the scale factor a of t.

What It Means in Practice

This means the standard metric for a homogeneous and isotropic universe.

Symbols

\(a(t)\): scale factor.

\(k\): spatial curvature parameter.

\(d\Omega^2\): angular part of the spatial metric.

Why It Matters

FLRW cosmology gives a clean cosmic time only because the universe is modeled as smooth on very large scales. That time is the proper time of comoving observers.
