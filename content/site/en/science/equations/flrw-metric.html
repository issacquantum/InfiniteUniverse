FLRW (Friedmann-Lemaitre-Robertson-Walker) Metric

Equation

$$ds^2 = -c^2dt^2 + a^2(t)\left[\frac{dr^2}{1-kr^2}+r^2d\Omega^2\right]$$

Literal Reading

d s squared equals negative c squared d t squared plus a of t squared multiplying the homogeneous and isotropic spatial line element.

Common Reading

The standard metric for a homogeneous and isotropic universe.

Symbols

\(a(t)\): scale factor.

\(k\): spatial curvature parameter.

\(d\Omega^2\): angular part of the spatial metric.

Meaning of the Equation

The metric encodes the idealized large-scale geometry of a universe that is homogeneous and isotropic. The scale factor carries the expansion, while the curvature parameter sets the spatial shape. It is not a general metric for every spacetime; it is the symmetric cosmological model used after those assumptions are made.
