Polynomial Matrix-Vector Transform

Equation

$$ p(A)b $$

Literal Reading

Apply the polynomial or matrix function \(p\) to matrix \(A\), then apply the result to vector \(b\).

Physicist's Reading

This is a compact way of describing matrix-function transformations, which are central to quantum singular value transformation and to classical dequantized analogs.

Symbol Breakdown

\(A\): matrix or linear operator.

\(p\): polynomial or matrix function applied to \(A\).

\(b\): vector acted on by the transformed matrix.

Physical Meaning

Many quantum linear-algebra algorithms can be understood as applying useful functions to operators. Tang's work asks when classical sampling and sketching can reproduce the relevant output without constructing the full quantum procedure.
