Data Processing Inequality

Equation

$$ S(\mathcal{E}(\rho)\,\|\,\mathcal{E}(\sigma)) \le S(\rho\,\|\,\sigma) $$

Literal Reading

The relative entropy after applying channel E is less than or equal to the original relative entropy.

Physicist's Reading

A physicist would read it as a physical channel cannot increase how distinguishable two quantum states are.

Symbol Breakdown

\(S(\rho\,\|\,\sigma)\): quantum relative entropy between two states.

\(\mathcal{E}\): a quantum channel or allowed physical process.

\(\le\): the monotonicity statement: processing cannot increase this information measure.

Physical Meaning

The inequality says noisy processing cannot create usable distinguishability from nothing. Once information is degraded by a physical channel, later processing cannot fully recover what was lost unless the channel preserved it in the first place.
