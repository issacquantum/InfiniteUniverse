Reduced Density Matrix

Equation

$$ \rho_A = \operatorname{Tr}_B\!\left(|\Psi^{-}\rangle\langle\Psi^{-}|\right) = \frac{1}{2}I $$

Literal Reading

rho sub A equals trace sub B open parenthesis rho sub AB close parenthesis.

Common Reading

The reduced state of subsystem A is obtained by tracing out B, and for the Bell singlet it is the maximally mixed state one half I.

Symbols

\(\rho_A\): the reduced density matrix describing only subsystem A.

\(\operatorname{Tr}_B\): the partial trace over subsystem B, which removes B from the description.

\(|\Psi^{-}\rangle\langle\Psi^{-}|\): the projector built from the Bell singlet state.

\(\tfrac{1}{2}I\): one half times the identity matrix, meaning every local outcome is equally likely.

Meaning of the Equation

Tracing out one subsystem gives the state accessible to the remaining subsystem.
