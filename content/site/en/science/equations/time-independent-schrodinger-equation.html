Time-Independent Schrödinger Equation

Equation

$$ \hat{H}\psi(\mathbf{r}) = E\psi(\mathbf{r}) $$

What the Formula Says

H hat psi of r equals E psi of r.

What It Means in Practice

This means the Hamiltonian acting on an energy eigenstate returns the same state multiplied by its energy eigenvalue.

Symbols

\(\hat{H}\): the Hamiltonian operator.

\(\psi(\mathbf{r})\): a stationary spatial wavefunction.

\(E\): the allowed energy eigenvalue.

\(\mathbf{r}\): the spatial position vector.

Equation Meaning

This is an eigenvalue equation. It selects the special states whose time dependence is only a phase and whose energies are definite.
