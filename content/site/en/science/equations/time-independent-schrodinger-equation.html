Time-Independent Schrödinger Equation

Equation

$$ \hat{H}\psi(\mathbf{r}) = E\psi(\mathbf{r}) $$

Literal Reading

Read it as: h hat psi of r equals E psi of r.

Common Reading

Allowed quantum states have definite energies.

Symbols

\(\hat{H}\): the Hamiltonian operator.

\(\psi(\mathbf{r})\): a stationary spatial wavefunction.

\(E\): the allowed energy eigenvalue.

\(\mathbf{r}\): the spatial position vector.

Meaning of the Equation

This relation connects the mathematical state of a physical system to what can happen next or what can be measured. It is not just notation: it states how energy, fields, waves, or probabilities constrain the behavior of the system.
