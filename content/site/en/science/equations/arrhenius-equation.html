Arrhenius Equation

Equation

$$k=Ae^{-E_a/(RT)}$$

Plain Meaning

Reaction rate usually increases with temperature because more molecules can cross the activation barrier.

Symbols

  • \(k\): rate constant.
  • \(A\): prefactor related to collision or attempt frequency.
  • \(E_a\): activation energy.
  • \(R\): gas constant.
  • \(T\): absolute temperature.

How to Read It

A high barrier makes the exponential small. Raising temperature makes barrier crossing more likely.

What It Does Not Say

It is a simplified empirical form. Real mechanisms can require more detailed kinetics.
