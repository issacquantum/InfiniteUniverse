Arrhenius Equation

Equation

$$k=Ae^{-E_a/(RT)}$$

Literal Reading

k equals A e to the power of minus E sub a over of RT.

Common Reading

Reaction rate usually increases with temperature because more molecules can cross the activation barrier.

Symbols

  • \(k\): rate constant.
  • \(A\): prefactor related to collision or attempt frequency.
  • \(E_a\): activation energy.
  • \(R\): gas constant.
  • \(T\): absolute temperature.

Meaning of the Equation

Temperature affects the rate exponentially, so small thermal changes can produce large reaction-rate changes. The activation energy sets how hard it is for molecules to cross the barrier.
