BQP Bounded-Error Definition

Equation

$$ x\in L \Rightarrow \Pr[C_x\ \mathrm{accepts}]\ge \frac{2}{3}, \qquad x\notin L \Rightarrow \Pr[C_x\ \mathrm{accepts}]\le \frac{1}{3} $$

Literal Reading

Read it as: if \(x\) is in the language, the quantum circuit accepts with probability at least two thirds; if \(x\) is not in the language, it accepts with probability at most one third.

Common Reading

A BQP algorithm is allowed to be wrong sometimes, but only with a controlled probability gap.

Symbols

\(L\): the decision problem or language being solved.

\(x\): the input string.

\(C_x\): the quantum circuit generated for input \(x\) or its input length.

\(\Pr\): probability after quantum measurement.

Meaning of the Equation

BQP does not require perfect certainty. It requires an efficient quantum circuit whose measurement outcome separates yes-instances from no-instances by a reliable gap. Repetition can reduce the error, so the exact constants matter less than the existence of a bounded-error separation using polynomial resources.
