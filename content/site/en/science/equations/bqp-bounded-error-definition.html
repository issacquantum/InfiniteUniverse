BQP Bounded-Error Definition

Equation

$$ x\in L \Rightarrow \Pr[C_x\ \mathrm{accepts}]\ge \frac{2}{3}, \qquad x\notin L \Rightarrow \Pr[C_x\ \mathrm{accepts}]\le \frac{1}{3} $$

Literal Reading

P sub accept greater than or equal to 2 over 3 for yes comma P sub accept less than or equal to 1 over 3 for no.

Common Reading

A BQP algorithm is allowed to be wrong sometimes, but only with a controlled probability gap.

Symbols

\(L\): the decision problem or language being solved.

\(x\): the input string.

\(C_x\): the quantum circuit generated for input \(x\) or its input length.

\(\Pr\): probability after quantum measurement.

Meaning of the Equation

The gap separates useful quantum algorithms from random guessing. Repeating the computation can reduce error, so bounded error is compatible with reliable decision making.
