Newtonian Gravitational Force

Equation

$$F = G \frac{m_1 m_2}{r^2}$$

Literal Reading

F equals G m1 m2 over r to the power of 2.

Common Reading

The gravitational force between two masses equals G times their mass product divided by the square of their separation.

Symbols

\(F\): gravitational force magnitude.

\(G\): gravitational constant.

\(m_1, m_2\): the two masses.

\(r\): separation between their centers.

Meaning of the Equation

Every pair of masses attracts with a force proportional to their masses and inverse-square in distance.
