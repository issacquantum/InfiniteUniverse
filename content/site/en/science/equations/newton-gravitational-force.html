Newtonian Gravitational Force

Equation

$$F = G \frac{m_1 m_2}{r^2}$$

Literal Reading

Read it as: f equals G times m one m two over r squared.

Common Reading

The gravitational force between two masses equals G times their mass product divided by the square of their separation.

Symbols

\(F\): gravitational force magnitude.

\(G\): gravitational constant.

\(m_1, m_2\): the two masses.

\(r\): separation between their centers.

Meaning of the Equation

This relation connects geometry, motion, horizons, or gravitational sources. It tells what clocks, distances, curvature, or observers would measure, so the physical claim is tied to spacetime structure rather than to a picture or metaphor.
