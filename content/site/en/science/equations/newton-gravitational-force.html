Newtonian Gravitational Force

Equation

$$F = G \frac{m_1 m_2}{r^2}$$

What the Formula Says

F equals G times m one m two over r squared.

What It Means in Practice

This means the gravitational force between two masses equals G times their mass product divided by the square of their separation.

Symbols

\(F\): gravitational force magnitude.

\(G\): gravitational constant.

\(m_1, m_2\): the two masses.

\(r\): separation between their centers.

Equation Meaning

This is Newton's law of universal gravitation. It describes how strongly two masses attract one another and explains why gravity becomes weaker with distance. In formation models, it gives the basic attraction that drives accretion and orbital structure.
