Heat Stability Condition

Equation

$$ \lambda=\alpha\frac{\Delta t}{\Delta x^2}\le\frac{1}{2} $$

Literal Reading

Read it as: the diffusion number lambda equals diffusivity times the time step divided by grid spacing squared, and it must stay below a limit for this explicit one-dimensional update.

Common Reading

A grid cannot move heat too far in one time step.

Symbols

\(\lambda\): diffusion number.

\(\alpha\): thermal diffusivity.

\(\Delta t\): time step.

\(\Delta x\): grid spacing.

Meaning of the Equation

This relation turns an algorithmic step into a precise rule. It identifies what is being updated, counted, or bounded, so the process can be checked by its state, cost, error, or learning behavior instead of only by a visual animation.
