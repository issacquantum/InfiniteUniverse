Heat Stability Condition

Equation

$$ \lambda=\alpha\frac{\Delta t}{\Delta x^2}\le\frac{1}{2}\quad\text{(1D FTCS)} $$
$$ \lambda_x+\lambda_y\le\frac{1}{2},\qquad \lambda_x=\lambda_y=\lambda\Rightarrow \lambda\le\frac{1}{4} $$

Literal Reading

alpha Delta t over Delta x to the power of 2 less than or equal to 1 over 2.

Common Reading

The allowed time step gets smaller when heat can spread in more directions.

Symbols

\(\lambda\): diffusion number for one direction.

\(\lambda_x,\lambda_y\): diffusion numbers in the horizontal and vertical grid directions.

\(\alpha\): thermal diffusivity.

\(\Delta t\): time step.

\(\Delta x\): grid spacing.

Meaning of the Equation

The discrete heat equation is stable only when the time step is small enough relative to spatial resolution.
