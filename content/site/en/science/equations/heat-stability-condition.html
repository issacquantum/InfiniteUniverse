Heat Stability Condition

Equation

$$ \lambda=\alpha\frac{\Delta t}{\Delta x^2}\le\frac{1}{2} $$

What the Formula Says

The diffusion number lambda equals diffusivity times the time step divided by grid spacing squared, and it must stay below a limit for this explicit one-dimensional update.

What It Means in Practice

A grid cannot move heat too far in one time step. If the step is too aggressive, the numerical solution becomes unstable instead of physical.

Symbols

\(\lambda\): diffusion number.

\(\alpha\): thermal diffusivity.

\(\Delta t\): time step.

\(\Delta x\): grid spacing.

Equation Meaning

The sandbox uses a two-dimensional grid, so its slider is kept more conservative: \(\lambda \le 0.25\).
