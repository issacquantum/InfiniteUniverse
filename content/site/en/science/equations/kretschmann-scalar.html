Kretschmann Scalar

Equation

$$K = R_{\mu\nu\rho\sigma}R^{\mu\nu\rho\sigma} = \frac{48G^2M^2}{c^4r^6}$$

What the Formula Says

K equals the Riemann tensor contracted with itself, which equals forty eight G squared M squared divided by c to the fourth times r to the sixth.

What It Means in Practice

This means a coordinate independent measure of curvature for the Schwarzschild geometry.

Symbols

\(K\): Kretschmann scalar.

\(R_{\mu\nu\rho\sigma}\): Riemann curvature tensor.

\(M\): black hole mass.

\(r\): radial coordinate.

Equation Meaning

The scalar stays finite at the horizon but diverges at \(r = 0\). That is why the horizon is not the real singularity, while the center is a true curvature breakdown in classical relativity.

Scope and Limits

This scalar is coordinate independent, so it separates a coordinate problem from a real curvature blowup. It still belongs to classical general relativity and is expected to fail where quantum gravity becomes essential.
