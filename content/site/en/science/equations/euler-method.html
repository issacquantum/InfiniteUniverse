Euler Method

Equation

$$ y_{n+1}=y_n+h f(t_n,y_n) $$

Literal Reading

Read it as: the next value of y equals the current value plus the step size times the slope at the current point.

Common Reading

This advances a system forward using a local derivative as an approximation to the next state.

Symbols

\(y_n\): current state.

\(y_{n+1}\): next state.

\(h\): step size.

\(f(t_n,y_n)\): derivative or slope at the current point.

Meaning of the Equation

This relation turns an algorithmic step into a precise rule. It identifies what is being updated, counted, or bounded, so the process can be checked by its state, cost, error, or learning behavior instead of only by a visual animation.
