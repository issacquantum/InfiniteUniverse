Hamilton's Equations

Equation

$$\dot q=\frac{\partial H}{\partial p},\qquad \dot p=-\frac{\partial H}{\partial q}$$

Literal Reading

q sub dot equals partial H over partial p comma p sub dot equals minus partial H over partial q.

Common Reading

Hamilton’s equations give time evolution in phase space from derivatives of the Hamiltonian.

Symbols

  • \(q\): coordinate.
  • \(p\): momentum.
  • \(H\): Hamiltonian, often total energy.
  • \(\dot q,\dot p\): time rates of change.

Meaning of the Equation

The Hamiltonian defines the flow through phase space. Its slopes with respect to momentum and position determine how the system's state moves from one instant to the next.
