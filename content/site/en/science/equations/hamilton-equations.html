Hamilton's Equations

Equation

$$\dot q=\frac{\partial H}{\partial p},\qquad \dot p=-\frac{\partial H}{\partial q}$$

Plain Meaning

These equations say how position-like and momentum-like quantities move through phase space.

Symbols

  • ▣\(q\): coordinate.
  • ▣\(p\): momentum.
  • ▣\(H\): Hamiltonian, often total energy.
  • ▣\(\dot q,\dot p\): time rates of change.

How to Read It

Read them as a pair: one updates position from momentum; the other updates momentum from the energy landscape.

What It Does Not Say

They do not require every system to be simple or exactly solvable. Many Hamiltonian systems are hard or chaotic.
