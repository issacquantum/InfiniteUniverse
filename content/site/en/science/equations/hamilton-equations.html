Hamilton's Equations

Equation

$$\dot q=\frac{\partial H}{\partial p},\qquad \dot p=-\frac{\partial H}{\partial q}$$

Literal Reading

Read it as: q sub dot equals partial H over partial p comma p sub dot equals minus partial H over partial q.

Common Reading

These equations say how position-like and momentum-like quantities move through phase space.

Symbols

  • \(q\): coordinate.
  • \(p\): momentum.
  • \(H\): Hamiltonian, often total energy.
  • \(\dot q,\dot p\): time rates of change.

Meaning of the Equation

It rewrites motion in terms of position and momentum, showing how energy generates time evolution.
