Photon Sphere Radius

Equation

$$r_{\text{ph}} = \frac{3GM}{c^2}$$

Literal Reading

Read it as: the photon sphere radius equals three G M divided by c squared.

Common Reading

The unstable circular light orbit around a non rotating black hole.

Symbols

\(r_{\text{ph}}\): photon sphere radius.

\(M\): black hole mass.

\(G\): gravitational constant.

\(c\): speed of light.

Meaning of the Equation

This relation connects geometry, motion, horizons, or gravitational sources. It tells what clocks, distances, curvature, or observers would measure, so the physical claim is tied to spacetime structure rather than to a picture or metaphor.

Scope and Limits

This radius is for a Schwarzschild black hole. Spin splits the light-orbit structure into direction-dependent photon regions, so real rotating black holes need the Kerr treatment.
