Photon Sphere Radius

Equation

$$r_{\text{ph}} = \frac{3GM}{c^2}$$

What the Formula Says

The photon sphere radius equals three G M divided by c squared.

What It Means in Practice

This means the unstable circular light orbit around a non rotating black hole.

Symbols

\(r_{\text{ph}}\): photon sphere radius.

\(M\): black hole mass.

\(G\): gravitational constant.

\(c\): speed of light.

Equation Meaning

The photon sphere helps shape the black hole shadow. Light can orbit there only unstably, so small disturbances send it either outward or inward.

Scope and Limits

This radius is for a Schwarzschild black hole. Spin splits the light-orbit structure into direction-dependent photon regions, so real rotating black holes need the Kerr treatment.
