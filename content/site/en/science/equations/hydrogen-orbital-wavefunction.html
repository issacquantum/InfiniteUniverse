Hydrogen Orbital Wavefunction

Equation

$$ \psi_{n\ell m}(r, \theta, \phi) = R_{n\ell}(r) \cdot Y_\ell^m(\theta, \phi) $$

Literal Reading

Psi n l m of r, theta, phi equals R n l of r times Y l m of theta, phi.

Physicist's Reading

A physicist would read it as the hydrogen orbital separates into a radial part and an angular part.

Symbol Breakdown

\(\psi_{n\ell m}\): the full hydrogen eigenfunction labeled by three quantum numbers.

\(R_{n\ell}(r)\): the radial function, which controls how the state changes with distance from the nucleus.

\(Y_\ell^m(\theta,\phi)\): the spherical harmonic, which controls the angular shape of the orbital.

\(n,\ell,m\): the principal, orbital, and magnetic quantum numbers.

Physical Meaning

Hydrogen orbitals can be factorized because the Coulomb problem has spherical symmetry. Distance and angular shape separate cleanly.
