Shannon Channel Capacity

Equation

$$ C=\max_{p(x)} I(X;Y) $$

Literal Reading

C equals max sub p of x I of X;Y.

Common Reading

Channel capacity is the maximum reliable information rate.

Symbols

\(C\): channel capacity, usually measured in bits per channel use.

\(p(x)\): probability distribution chosen for the channel input.

\(I(X;Y)\): mutual information between sent and received variables.

Meaning of the Equation

The capacity is the ceiling for reliable communication through a noisy channel. Below it, coding can make errors arbitrarily rare in idealized limits; above it, reliable transmission cannot be guaranteed.
