Shannon Channel Capacity

Equation

$$ C=\max_{p(x)} I(X;Y) $$

What the Formula Says

C equals the maximum mutual information between input X and output Y over all input distributions p of x.

What It Means in Practice

This is the highest reliable communication rate allowed by a noisy channel, assuming optimal coding.

Symbols

\(C\): channel capacity, usually measured in bits per channel use.

\(p(x)\): probability distribution chosen for the channel input.

\(I(X;Y)\): mutual information between sent and received variables.

Equation Meaning

Noise does not destroy communication completely. It sets a limit, and coding theory studies how close a real system can get to that limit.
