RLHF (reinforcement learning from human feedback) Objective

Equation

$$ \max_\theta\;\mathbb{E}[r_\phi(x,y)]-\beta D_{\rm KL}(\pi_\theta(y|x)\|\pi_{\rm ref}(y|x)) $$

What the Formula Says

Maximize reward-model score while penalizing deviation from a reference policy.

What It Means in Practice

RLHF pushes a language model toward outputs humans prefer, while a KL (Kullback-Leibler) penalty prevents the policy from drifting too far from the base model.

Symbols

\(r_\phi(x,y)\): learned reward model score for prompt \(x\) and output \(y\).

\(\pi_\theta\): tuned policy.

\(\pi_{\rm ref}\): reference model.

\(\beta\): strength of the KL penalty.

Why It Matters

This is alignment pressure, not guaranteed truth. The reward model captures preferences imperfectly, so evaluation remains necessary.
