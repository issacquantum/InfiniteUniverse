Density Matrix State

Equation

$$ \rho = |\psi\rangle\langle\psi|,\qquad \rho = \sum_i p_i|\psi_i\rangle\langle\psi_i|,\qquad \operatorname{Tr}(\rho)=1 $$

Literal Reading

Common Reading

The density matrix form of a quantum state.

Symbols

\(\rho\): the density matrix describing the quantum state.

\(|\psi\rangle\langle\psi|\): the projector for a pure state.

\(p_i\): the classical probability of preparing state \(|\psi_i\rangle\).

\(\sum_i p_i|\psi_i\rangle\langle\psi_i|\): a mixed state built from possible preparations.

\(\operatorname{Tr}(\rho)=1\): the normalization condition that total probability is one.

Meaning of the Equation

A density matrix represents pure states, mixed states, and statistical uncertainty.
