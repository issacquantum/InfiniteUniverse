Hubble Time

Equation

$$t_H = \frac{1}{H_0}$$

Literal Reading

t sub H equals 1 over H0.

Common Reading

The Hubble time is the inverse of the present-day Hubble constant.

Symbols

\(t_H\): Hubble time.

\(H_0\): present-day Hubble constant.

Meaning of the Equation

The Hubble time is a rough expansion timescale from today's Hubble constant. It is useful for scale, but the true age of the universe depends on the full expansion history.
