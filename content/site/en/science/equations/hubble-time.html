Hubble Time

Equation

$$t_H = \frac{1}{H_0}$$

Literal Reading

t H equals one over H naught.

Physicist's Reading

A physicist would read it as the Hubble time is the inverse of the present-day Hubble constant.

Symbol Breakdown

\(t_H\): Hubble time.

\(H_0\): present-day Hubble constant.

Physical Meaning

This is a rough timescale for the age of the universe obtained by inverting the present expansion rate. It is useful as a first estimate, but it is not the exact cosmic age because the expansion rate has changed over time.
