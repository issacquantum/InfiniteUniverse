Perfect Secrecy Condition

Equation

$$ P(M=m\mid C=c)=P(M=m) $$

Literal Reading

Read it as: the probability of message \(m\) after seeing ciphertext \(c\) is the same as the probability of message \(m\) before seeing it.

Common Reading

The ciphertext carries no usable information about the message.

Symbols

\(M\): the message random variable.

\(C\): the ciphertext random variable.

\(\mid\): conditioning on what an observer has seen.

Meaning of the Equation

This relation states how the named quantities depend on each other. Its purpose is to turn a conceptual statement into a rule that can be calculated, compared, or tested inside the model where it is used.
