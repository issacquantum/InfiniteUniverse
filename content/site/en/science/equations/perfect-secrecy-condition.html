Perfect Secrecy Condition

Equation

$$ P(M=m\mid C=c)=P(M=m) $$

Literal Reading

The probability of message \(m\) after seeing ciphertext \(c\) is the same as the probability of message \(m\) before seeing it.

Physicist's Reading

The ciphertext carries no usable information about the message.

Symbol Breakdown

\(M\): the message random variable.

\(C\): the ciphertext random variable.

\(\mid\): conditioning on what an observer has seen.

Physical Meaning

Perfect secrecy is an information-theoretic standard, not just a computational assumption. In Shannon's sense, observing the ciphertext does not reduce uncertainty about the message.
