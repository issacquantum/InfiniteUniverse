Perfect Secrecy Condition

Equation

$$ P(M=m\mid C=c)=P(M=m) $$

Literal Reading

P of M vertical bar C equals P of M.

Common Reading

The ciphertext carries no usable information about the message.

Symbols

\(M\): the message random variable.

\(C\): the ciphertext random variable.

\(\mid\): conditioning on what an observer has seen.

Meaning of the Equation

Perfect secrecy means seeing the ciphertext does not change the probability of any message. The encrypted output carries no statistical clue about the plaintext.
