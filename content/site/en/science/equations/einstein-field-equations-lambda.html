Einstein Field Equations with Lambda

Equation

$$G_{\mu\nu}+\Lambda g_{\mu\nu}=\frac{8\pi G}{c^4}T_{\mu\nu}$$

Literal Reading

Read it as: g mu nu plus Lambda times g mu nu equals eight pi G over c to the fourth times T mu nu.

Common Reading

Matter, energy, and the cosmological constant determine spacetime curvature.

Symbols

\(G_{\mu\nu}\): Einstein tensor, encoding curvature.

\(\Lambda\): cosmological constant.

\(g_{\mu\nu}\): metric tensor.

\(T_{\mu\nu}\): stress energy tensor.

Meaning of the Equation

This version adds the cosmological constant to the curvature side of Einstein's equation. It keeps the same idea that matter and energy shape geometry, while also allowing spacetime itself to have a large-scale expansion effect.
