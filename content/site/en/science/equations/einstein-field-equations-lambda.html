Einstein Field Equations with Lambda

Equation

$$G_{\mu\nu}+\Lambda g_{\mu\nu}=\frac{8\pi G}{c^4}T_{\mu\nu}$$

Literal Reading

G mu nu plus Lambda times g mu nu equals eight pi G over c to the fourth times T mu nu.

Physicist's Reading

A physicist reads this as spacetime curvature, including the cosmological constant term, being related to matter and energy.

Symbol Breakdown

\(G_{\mu\nu}\): Einstein tensor, encoding curvature.

\(\Lambda\): cosmological constant.

\(g_{\mu\nu}\): metric tensor.

\(T_{\mu\nu}\): stress energy tensor.

Physical Meaning

This form is the version used when the cosmological constant is included. It is central to modern cosmology because \(\Lambda\) is connected to accelerated cosmic expansion in the base cosmological model.
