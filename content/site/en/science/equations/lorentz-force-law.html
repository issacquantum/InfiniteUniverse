Lorentz Force Law

Equation

$$\mathbf{F}=q(\mathbf{E}+\mathbf{v}\times\mathbf{B})$$

Literal Reading

The force on a charge equals charge times the electric field plus the velocity cross the magnetic field.

Physicist's Reading

Electric fields push charges directly. Magnetic fields act on moving charges perpendicular to both velocity and magnetic-field direction.

Symbol Breakdown

\(q\): electric charge.

\(\mathbf{E}\): electric field.

\(\mathbf{v}\): charge velocity.

\(\mathbf{B}\): magnetic field.

Physical Meaning

This is the basic bridge between electromagnetic fields and the motion of charged particles.
