Lorentz Force Law

Equation

$$\mathbf{F}=q(\mathbf{E}+\mathbf{v}\times\mathbf{B})$$

Literal Reading

Read it as: force equals charge times electric field plus velocity cross magnetic field.

Common Reading

A short way to say it: A charged particle is pushed by electric fields and deflected by magnetic fields when it moves.

Symbols

  • \(q\): electric charge.
  • \(\mathbf{E}\): electric field.
  • \(\mathbf{v}\): particle velocity.
  • \(\mathbf{B}\): magnetic field.

Meaning of the Equation

A charged particle is pushed by electric fields and deflected by magnetic fields when it moves.
