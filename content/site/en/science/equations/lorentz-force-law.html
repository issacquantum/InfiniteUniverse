Lorentz Force Law

Equation

$$\mathbf{F}=q(\mathbf{E}+\mathbf{v}\times\mathbf{B})$$

Literal Reading

Read it as: force equals charge times electric field plus velocity cross magnetic field.

Common Reading

Electric fields push charges; magnetic fields bend moving charges.

Symbols

  • \(q\): electric charge.
  • \(\mathbf{E}\): electric field.
  • \(\mathbf{v}\): particle velocity.
  • \(\mathbf{B}\): magnetic field.

Meaning of the Equation

This equation gives the force on a charged particle from electromagnetic fields. The electric part can push a charge even when it is not moving; the magnetic part depends on velocity and changes the direction of motion rather than acting like an ordinary straight pull.
