Lorentz Force Law

Equation

$$\mathbf{F}=q(\mathbf{E}+\mathbf{v}\times\mathbf{B})$$

Plain Meaning

A charged particle is pushed by electric fields and deflected by magnetic fields when it moves.

Symbols

  • ▣\(q\): electric charge.
  • ▣\(\mathbf{E}\): electric field.
  • ▣\(\mathbf{v}\): particle velocity.
  • ▣\(\mathbf{B}\): magnetic field.

How to Read It

The electric part can push along the field. The magnetic part is sideways because of the cross product.

What It Does Not Say

It does not include quantum effects, radiation reaction, or the full field equations by itself.
