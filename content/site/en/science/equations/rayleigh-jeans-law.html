Rayleigh-Jeans Law

Equation

$$ B_\nu(\nu,T)=\frac{2\nu^2}{c^2}k_BT $$

Literal Reading

B sub nu of nu and T equals two nu squared over c squared times k sub B T.

Common Reading

This is the frequency form of the Rayleigh-Jeans law for blackbody spectral radiance.

Symbols

\(B_\nu(\nu,T)\): spectral radiance per unit frequency.

\(\nu\): the frequency of the radiation.

\(T\): the absolute temperature of the emitting body.

\(c\): the speed of light in vacuum.

\(k_B\): Boltzmann's constant, which connects temperature to energy.

Meaning of the Equation

The frequency label \(B_\nu\) matters: this is not the wavelength form \(B_\lambda\).
