Rayleigh-Jeans Law

Equation

$$ B(\nu, T) = \frac{2\nu^2}{c^2} k_B T $$

Literal Reading

B of nu, T equals two nu squared over c squared times k sub B times T.

Common Reading

Short version: The classical spectral radiance equals two nu squared over c squared times Boltzmann's constant times temperature.

Symbols

\(B(\nu,T)\): the spectral radiance, meaning the energy emitted per unit area, per unit solid angle, per unit frequency.

\(\nu\): the frequency of the radiation.

\(T\): the absolute temperature of the emitting body.

\(c\): the speed of light in vacuum.

\(k_B\): Boltzmann's constant, which connects temperature to energy.

Meaning of the Equation

The classical spectral radiance equals two nu squared over c squared times Boltzmann's constant times temperature.
