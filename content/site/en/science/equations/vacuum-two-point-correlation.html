Vacuum Two-Point Correlation

Equation

$$ G(x,y)=\langle 0|\hat{\phi}(x)\hat{\phi}(y)|0\rangle $$

What the Formula Says

G of x and y equals the vacuum expectation value of the field at x times the field at y.

What It Means in Practice

This means a vacuum correlation function: how field fluctuations at two spacetime points are related.

Symbols

\(G(x,y)\): two-point correlation function.

\(|0\rangle\): the vacuum state of the field.

\(\hat{\phi}(x)\): field operator evaluated at spacetime point \(x\).

\(x,y\): spacetime points.

Why It Matters

The quantum vacuum is the lowest-energy state, not classical emptiness. Its field observables have correlations and nonzero variance even when no real particles are present.
