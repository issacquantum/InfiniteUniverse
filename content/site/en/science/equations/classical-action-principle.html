Classical Action Principle

Equation

$$S=\int L(q,\dot q,t)\,dt$$

Plain Meaning

The action adds up the Lagrangian along a possible path. The real path makes this total stationary.

Symbols

  • \(S\): action.
  • \(L\): Lagrangian, usually kinetic minus potential energy.
  • \(q\): generalized coordinate.
  • \(\dot q\): rate of change of that coordinate.

How to Read It

Instead of guessing forces directly, write the energy structure and derive the motion.

What It Does Not Say

Stationary does not always mean smallest. It means small changes to the path do not change the action to first order.
