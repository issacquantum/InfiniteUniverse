Classical Action Principle

Equation

$$S=\int L(q,\dot q,t)\,dt$$

Literal Reading

Read it as: action S equals the time integral of the Lagrangian L.

Common Reading

Action equals the Lagrangian accumulated over time.

Symbols

  • \(S\): action.
  • \(L\): Lagrangian, usually kinetic minus potential energy.
  • \(q\): generalized coordinate.
  • \(\dot q\): rate of change of that coordinate.

Meaning of the Equation

This equation assigns one number, the action, to an entire possible motion. It does not judge the system at only one instant; it accumulates the Lagrangian through time so different possible histories can be compared by their total action.
