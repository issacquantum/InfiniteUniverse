Classical Action Principle

Equation

$$S=\int L(q,\dot q,t)\,dt$$

Literal Reading

S equals integral L open parenthesis q comma q sub dot comma t close parenthesis dt.

Common Reading

Action equals the Lagrangian accumulated over time.

Symbols

  • \(S\): action.
  • \(L\): Lagrangian, usually kinetic minus potential energy.
  • \(q\): generalized coordinate.
  • \(\dot q\): rate of change of that coordinate.

Meaning of the Equation

Nature selects the path whose action is stationary. This is a compact way to derive equations of motion.
