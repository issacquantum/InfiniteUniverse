Classical Action Principle

Equation

$$S=\int L(q,\dot q,t)\,dt$$

Literal Reading

The action is the time integral of the Lagrangian.

Physicist's Reading

The path a classical system follows is the path that makes the action stationary under small variations.

Symbol Breakdown

\(S\): action.

\(L\): Lagrangian, usually kinetic energy minus potential energy.

\(q\): generalized coordinate.

\(\dot q\): time derivative of the coordinate.

Physical Meaning

This formulation connects classical mechanics to symmetry, field theory, relativity, and quantum path integrals.
