Classical Action Principle

Equation

$$S=\int L(q,\dot q,t)\,dt$$

Literal Reading

Read it as: action S equals the time integral of the Lagrangian L.

Common Reading

A short way to say it: The action adds up the Lagrangian along a possible path. The real path makes this total stationary.

Symbols

  • \(S\): action.
  • \(L\): Lagrangian, usually kinetic minus potential energy.
  • \(q\): generalized coordinate.
  • \(\dot q\): rate of change of that coordinate.

Meaning of the Equation
