Classical Action Principle

Equation

$$S=\int L(q,\dot q,t)\,dt$$

Literal Reading

S equals integral L of q comma q sub dot comma t dt.

Common Reading

The action is the time integral of the Lagrangian along a path.

Symbols

  • \(S\): action.
  • \(L\): Lagrangian, usually kinetic minus potential energy.
  • \(q\): generalized coordinate.
  • \(\dot q\): rate of change of that coordinate.

Meaning of the Equation

The physical path makes the action stationary, not always a minimum.
