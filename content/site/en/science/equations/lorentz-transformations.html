Lorentz Transformations

Equation

$$x' = \gamma(x - vt),\qquad t' = \gamma\left(t - \frac{vx}{c^2}\right)$$

Literal Reading

x' equals gamma times the quantity x minus vt comma t' equals gamma times the quantity t minus vx over c to the power of 2.

Common Reading

Space and time coordinates transform between inertial frames while preserving the speed of light.

Symbols

\(x,t\): position and time in one inertial frame.

\(x',t'\): position and time in another inertial frame.

\(v\): relative velocity between the frames.

\(\gamma\): Lorentz factor.

\(c\): speed of light.

Meaning of the Equation

It connects the quantities in the formula so the relation can be used to compute or interpret a situation in Relativity & Spacetime.
