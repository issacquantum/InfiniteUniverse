Lorentz Transformations

Equation

$$x' = \gamma(x - vt),\qquad t' = \gamma\left(t - \frac{vx}{c^2}\right)$$

Literal Reading

x prime equals gamma times x minus v t, and t prime equals gamma times t minus v x over c squared.

Physicist's Reading

A physicist reads this as the transformation that preserves the speed of light and mixes space with time between inertial observers.

Symbol Breakdown

\(x,t\): position and time in one inertial frame.

\(x',t'\): position and time in another inertial frame.

\(v\): relative velocity between the frames.

\(\gamma\): Lorentz factor.

\(c\): speed of light.

Physical Meaning

The equation shows why relativity is not only a correction to motion. Space and time coordinates transform into each other. Observers moving relative to one another do not share the same split between space and time.
