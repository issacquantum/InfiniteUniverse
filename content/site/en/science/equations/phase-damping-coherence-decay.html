Phase Damping Coherence Decay

Equation

$$ \rho_{01}(t) = \rho_{01}(0)\, e^{-t/T_2} $$

Literal Reading

The off-diagonal coherence at time \(t\) equals its initial value multiplied by an exponential decay factor.

Physicist's Reading

A physicist would read it as a simple model of dephasing: phase information fades over time even when population probabilities do not necessarily change.

Symbol Breakdown

\(\rho_{01}(t)\): the off-diagonal element of the density matrix at time \(t\).

\(\rho_{01}(0)\): the initial coherence.

\(e^{-t/T_2}\): exponential decay factor.

\(T_2\): dephasing time.

Physical Meaning

Phase damping destroys coherence, which is one of the main reasons quantum information is fragile. The state may still exist, but the phase relationships needed for interference fade away.
