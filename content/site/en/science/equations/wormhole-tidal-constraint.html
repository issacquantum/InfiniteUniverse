Traversability Tidal Constraint

Equation

$$ |\Delta a| \lesssim g_\oplus $$

Literal Reading

The magnitude of the differential acceleration is less than or roughly equal to Earth gravity.

Common Reading

Traversability requires tidal forces small enough for a traveler or object to survive.

Symbols

\(\Delta a\): differential acceleration between nearby parts of the traveler.

\(g_\oplus\): gravitational acceleration at Earth's surface, used as a human-scale reference.

Meaning of the Equation

A traversable wormhole would need tidal gradients small enough not to destroy a traveler or object. This condition is about survivability, not just geometric passage.
