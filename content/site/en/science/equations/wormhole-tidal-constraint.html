Traversability Tidal Constraint

Equation

$$ |\Delta a| \lesssim g_\oplus $$

What the Formula Says

The tidal acceleration across the traveler should be no larger than roughly Earth gravity.

What It Means in Practice

Passing through is not enough. A usable wormhole must keep tidal stretching and compression survivable.

Symbols

\(\Delta a\): differential acceleration between nearby parts of the traveler.

\(g_\oplus\): gravitational acceleration at Earth's surface, used as a human-scale reference.

Why It Matters

This is an engineering-style condition layered on top of the geometry. A mathematical throat can be traversable in principle and still be lethal in practice.
