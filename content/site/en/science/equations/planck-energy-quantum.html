Planck Energy Quantum

Equation

$$ E = h\nu $$

Literal Reading

E equals h f.

Common Reading

The energy of one quantum equals Planck's constant times the radiation frequency.

Symbols

\(E\): the energy carried by one quantum of radiation.

\(h\): Planck's constant, the basic quantum of action.

\(\nu\): the frequency of the radiation.

Meaning of the Equation

Frequency sets the size of each light quantum. This is why blue or ultraviolet photons can trigger processes that red photons cannot, even when the total brightness is large.
