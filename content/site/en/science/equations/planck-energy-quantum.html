Planck Energy Quantum

Equation

$$ E = h\nu $$

Literal Reading

Read it as: e equals h times nu.

Common Reading

The energy of one quantum equals Planck's constant times the radiation frequency.

Symbols

\(E\): the energy carried by one quantum of radiation.

\(h\): Planck's constant, the basic quantum of action.

\(\nu\): the frequency of the radiation.

Meaning of the Equation

This relation describes a quantum state, transformation, probability, or correlation. It matters because quantum systems are not described by one definite classical value; the equation tells what can be predicted from amplitudes, operators, or density matrices.
