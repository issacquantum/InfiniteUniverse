Logistic Growth

Equation

$$\frac{dN}{dt}=rN\left(1-\frac{N}{K}\right)$$

Plain Meaning

A population grows quickly when small, then slows as it approaches a carrying capacity.

Symbols

  • \(N\): population size.
  • \(r\): growth rate.
  • \(K\): carrying capacity.
  • \(dN/dt\): rate of population change.

How to Read It

The factor \((1-N/K)\) reduces growth as resources become limiting.

What It Does Not Say

Real populations can have age structure, migration, predation, stochasticity, and changing environments.
