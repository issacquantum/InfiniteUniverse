Fluctuation Field Sample

Equation

$$ \phi_\Lambda(\mathbf r,t)\propto \sum_{\mathbf{k}} \frac{e^{-(|\mathbf{k}|/\Lambda)^4}}{\sqrt{2\omega_{\mathbf{k}}}} \Big[ (Q_{\mathbf{k}}\cos(\mathbf{k}\cdot\mathbf r)+\widetilde Q_{\mathbf{k}}\sin(\mathbf{k}\cdot\mathbf r)) \cos(\omega_{\mathbf{k}}t) +(P_{\mathbf{k}}\cos(\mathbf{k}\cdot\mathbf r)+\widetilde P_{\mathbf{k}}\sin(\mathbf{k}\cdot\mathbf r)) \sin(\omega_{\mathbf{k}}t) \Big],\quad \omega_{\mathbf{k}}=\sqrt{|\mathbf{k}|^2+m^2},\quad m\approx\frac{1}{\xi} $$

Common Reading

A finite, cutoff, visualization-level sample of a free real scalar field vacuum.

Symbols

\(\phi_\Lambda(\mathbf r,t)\): the rendered cutoff field amplitude at position \(\mathbf r=(x,z)\) and time \(t\).

\(\mathbf{k}\): wave vector for a field mode.

\(\Lambda\): ultraviolet cutoff that damps short-wavelength modes for readability and stability.

\(\omega_{\mathbf{k}}\): mode angular frequency.

\(m\): mass-like parameter controlling correlation length.

\(\xi\): correlation length; larger \(\xi\) produces broader, smoother field structure.

\(Q_{\mathbf{k}},\widetilde Q_{\mathbf{k}},P_{\mathbf{k}},\widetilde P_{\mathbf{k}}\): independent standard Gaussian coefficients. They select one possible vacuum sample for the finite model.

Meaning of the Equation

A fluctuating field can be decomposed into wave modes.
