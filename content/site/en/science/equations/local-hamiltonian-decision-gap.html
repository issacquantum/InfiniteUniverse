Local Hamiltonian Decision Gap

Equation

$$ H=\sum_{j=1}^{m}H_j, \qquad \lambda_{\min}(H)\le a \quad\text{or}\quad \lambda_{\min}(H)\ge b, \qquad b-a\ge \frac{1}{\mathrm{poly}(n)} $$

Literal Reading

Read it as: the Hamiltonian is a sum of local terms, and the smallest eigenvalue is promised to be either at most \(a\) or at least \(b\), with an inverse-polynomial gap between \(a\) and \(b\).

Common Reading

Decide whether a local quantum system has low energy or is separated from low energy by a promised gap.

Symbols

\(H\): the full Hamiltonian of the system.

\(H_j\): a local term acting on only a few qubits or particles.

\(\lambda_{\min}(H)\): the ground-state energy, the smallest eigenvalue of \(H\).

\(a,b\): threshold energies defining the two promised cases.

Meaning of the Equation

The problem is not asking for the exact ground-state energy. It asks for a decision between two separated cases: definitely low enough or definitely high enough. This promise gap makes the task a complexity-theory problem and is the reason the local Hamiltonian problem can serve as a quantum analogue of satisfiability.
