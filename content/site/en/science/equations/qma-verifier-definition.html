QMA Verifier Definition

Equation

$$ x\in L \Rightarrow \exists|\psi\rangle:\Pr[V_x(|\psi\rangle)=1]\ge a, \qquad x\notin L \Rightarrow \forall|\psi\rangle:\Pr[V_x(|\psi\rangle)=1]\le b, \qquad a-b\ge \frac{1}{\mathrm{poly}(n)} $$

Literal Reading

Read it as: for yes-instances there exists a quantum witness that makes the verifier accept with probability at least \(a\); for no-instances every quantum witness is accepted with probability at most \(b\); the gap between \(a\) and \(b\) is at least inverse polynomial.

Common Reading

QMA is efficient quantum proof checking with a guaranteed acceptance gap.

Symbols

\(|\psi\rangle\): the quantum witness or proof state.

\(V_x\): the verifier circuit for input \(x\).

\(a\): completeness threshold for accepting true statements.

\(b\): soundness threshold limiting false statements.

\(\mathrm{poly}(n)\): a polynomial in the input size.

Meaning of the Equation

A QMA verifier must be convinced by some valid quantum proof when the statement is true, but no quantum proof may fool it too often when the statement is false. The inverse-polynomial gap makes the distinction amplifiable, so repeated verification can separate the two cases more strongly.
