Quantum Circuit Complexity

Equation

$$ C_{\epsilon}(U)=\min\left\{m:\left\|U-G_m\cdots G_2G_1\right\|\le\epsilon\right\} $$

Literal Reading

Read it as: the epsilon-accuracy circuit complexity of \(U\) is the smallest number \(m\) of gates whose product approximates \(U\) within error \(\epsilon\).

Common Reading

Circuit complexity is the shortest gate recipe for building a target operation to a chosen accuracy.

Symbols

\(U\): the target unitary operation.

\(G_1,\ldots,G_m\): elementary gates from an allowed gate set.

\(\epsilon\): tolerated approximation error.

\(\|\cdot\|\): a norm measuring distance between operators.

Meaning of the Equation

The equation counts implementation difficulty, not just mathematical size. If a transformation has a short gate sequence, it is low complexity even if it acts on a huge Hilbert space. If no short sequence approximates it, then the operation is complex in the circuit sense.
