Quark-Antiquark Pair Production

Equation

$$\gamma + \gamma \rightarrow q + \bar{q}$$

What the Formula Says

Gamma plus gamma goes to q plus q bar.

What It Means in Practice

This means two photons can produce a quark-antiquark pair if the available energy exceeds the threshold.

Symbols

\(\gamma\): photon.

\(q\): quark.

\(\bar{q}\): antiquark.

Equation Meaning

This is the same energy-to-matter logic as electron-positron creation, but for quarks. In the very early universe, extreme temperatures made such reactions common, feeding the particle plasma from which later matter emerged.
