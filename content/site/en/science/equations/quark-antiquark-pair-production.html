Quark-Antiquark Pair Production

Equation

$$\gamma + \gamma \rightarrow q + \bar{q}$$

Literal Reading

g plus g goes to q plus q sub bar.

Common Reading

Two photons can produce a quark-antiquark pair if the available energy exceeds the threshold.

Symbols

\(\gamma\): photon.

\(q\): quark.

\(\bar{q}\): antiquark.

Meaning of the Equation

Enough field energy can produce a quark-antiquark pair, but isolated quarks do not emerge freely because of confinement. The products appear inside hadrons or jets.
