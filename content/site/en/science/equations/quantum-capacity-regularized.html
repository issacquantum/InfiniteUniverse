Regularized Quantum Capacity

Equation

$$ Q(\mathcal{E})=\lim_{n\to\infty}\frac{1}{n}\max_{\rho} I_c(\rho,\mathcal{E}^{\otimes n}) $$

Literal Reading

Read it as: q of E equals the limit as n goes to infinity of one over n times the maximum coherent information over n parallel uses of the channel.

Common Reading

The best asymptotic rate for reliably transmitting unknown quantum states through a noisy quantum channel.

Symbols

\(Q(\mathcal{E})\): quantum capacity of the channel.

\(\mathcal{E}^{\otimes n}\): \(n\) uses of the channel treated jointly.

\(I_c\): coherent information.

\(\max_\rho\): optimization over possible input states.

\(\lim_{n\to\infty}\frac{1}{n}\): the asymptotic rate per channel use.

Meaning of the Equation

This relation measures uncertainty, correlation, capacity, or distinguishability. It tells how much information is present, lost, shared, or recoverable, so the concept becomes a quantity that can be compared rather than just described in words.
