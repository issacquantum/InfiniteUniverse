Regularized Quantum Capacity

Equation

$$ Q(\mathcal{E})=\lim_{n\to\infty}\frac{1}{n}\max_{\rho} I_c(\rho,\mathcal{E}^{\otimes n}) $$

Common Reading

The best asymptotic rate for reliably transmitting unknown quantum states through a noisy quantum channel.

Symbols

\(Q(\mathcal{E})\): quantum capacity of the channel.

\(\mathcal{E}^{\otimes n}\): \(n\) uses of the channel treated jointly.

\(I_c\): coherent information.

\(\max_\rho\): optimization over possible input states.

\(\lim_{n\to\infty}\frac{1}{n}\): the asymptotic rate per channel use.

Meaning of the Equation

Quantum capacity measures how reliably quantum information can be transmitted through a channel.
