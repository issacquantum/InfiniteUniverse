Regularized Quantum Capacity

Equation

$$ Q(\mathcal{E})=\lim_{n\to\infty}\frac{1}{n}\max_{\rho} I_c(\rho,\mathcal{E}^{\otimes n}) $$

What the Formula Says

Q of E equals the limit as n goes to infinity of one over n times the maximum coherent information over n parallel uses of the channel.

What It Means in Practice

This means the best asymptotic rate for reliably transmitting unknown quantum states through a noisy quantum channel.

Symbols

\(Q(\mathcal{E})\): quantum capacity of the channel.

\(\mathcal{E}^{\otimes n}\): \(n\) uses of the channel treated jointly.

\(I_c\): coherent information.

\(\max_\rho\): optimization over possible input states.

\(\lim_{n\to\infty}\frac{1}{n}\): the asymptotic rate per channel use.

Equation Meaning

Quantum capacity is harder than classical capacity because many channel uses may need to be encoded together. The regularized formula captures the best long-run rate for preserving quantum states, including their entanglement with reference systems.
