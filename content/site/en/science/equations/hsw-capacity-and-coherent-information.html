HSW (Holevo-Schumacher-Westmoreland) Capacity and Coherent Information

Equation

$$ C(\mathcal{E}) = \max_{\{p_x, \rho_x\}} \chi(\mathcal{E}) $$ $$ I_c(\rho, \mathcal{E}) = S(\mathcal{E}(\rho)) - S((\mathcal{E} \otimes \mathrm{id})(|\psi\rangle\langle\psi|)) $$

Literal Reading

Read it as: the classical capacity of the channel is the maximum Holevo information over possible input ensembles. Coherent information compares output entropy with the entropy kept by a reference system.

Common Reading

The classical capacity of the channel is the maximum Holevo information over possible input ensembles.

Symbols

\(\mathcal{E}\): the quantum channel.

\(C(\mathcal{E})\): classical capacity of the channel.

\(\chi(\mathcal{E})\): Holevo information after the channel acts on an ensemble.

\(I_c\): coherent information.

\(\mathrm{id}\): the identity channel applied to a reference system.

Meaning of the Equation

This relation measures uncertainty, correlation, capacity, or distinguishability. It tells how much information is present, lost, shared, or recoverable, so the concept becomes a quantity that can be compared rather than just described in words.
