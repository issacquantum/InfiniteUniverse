HSW (Holevo-Schumacher-Westmoreland) Capacity and Coherent Information

Equation

$$ C^{(1)}(\mathcal{E}) = \max_{\{p_x, \rho_x\}} \chi(\{p_x, \mathcal{E}(\rho_x)\}) $$ $$ I_c(\rho, \mathcal{E}) = S(\mathcal{E}(\rho)) - S((\mathcal{E} \otimes \mathrm{id})(|\psi\rangle\langle\psi|)) $$

Literal Reading

C one of E equals the maximum, over input ensembles p sub x and rho sub x, of the Holevo quantity of the output ensemble p sub x and E of rho sub x. I sub c equals the output entropy minus the entropy of the channel acting on one half of a purification.

Common Reading

The one-shot Holevo quantity describes how much classical information one use of a quantum channel can carry under an optimized input ensemble.

Symbols

\(C^{(1)}(\mathcal{E})\): one-shot classical capacity expression based on the Holevo quantity.

\(\mathcal{E}\): the quantum channel.

\(\{p_x,\rho_x\}\): input ensemble of probabilities and states.

\(\chi\): Holevo information of the output ensemble.

\(I_c\): coherent information.

\(\mathrm{id}\): the identity channel applied to a reference system.

Meaning of the Equation

For a general channel, full classical capacity may require regularization over many channel uses; coherent information is the quantity used in quantum-capacity analysis.
