Neural Scaling Law

Equation

$$ L(N,D)\approx L_\infty+aN^{-\alpha}+bD^{-\beta} $$

Literal Reading

L of N equals A N to the power of minus alpha plus B.

Common Reading

For many language-model regimes, performance changes predictably as parameters, data, and compute scale.

Symbols

\(N\): model parameter count.

\(D\): dataset size.

\(L_\infty\): irreducible loss floor.

\(\alpha,\beta\): empirical scaling exponents.

Meaning of the Equation

The relation is an empirical trend inside a training regime. It does not guarantee understanding; it says loss often follows predictable scaling with resources.
