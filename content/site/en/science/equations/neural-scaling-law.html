Neural Scaling Law

Equation

$$ L(N,D)\approx L_\infty+aN^{-\alpha}+bD^{-\beta} $$

Literal Reading

Loss approaches an irreducible floor plus power-law terms from model size and dataset size.

Physicist's Reading

For many language-model regimes, performance changes predictably as parameters, data, and compute scale.

Symbol Breakdown

\(N\): model parameter count.

\(D\): dataset size.

\(L_\infty\): irreducible loss floor.

\(\alpha,\beta\): empirical scaling exponents.

Physical Meaning

Scaling laws explain why compute became central to modern model development, but they do not remove limits from data quality, evaluation, safety, or deployment cost.
