Kerr Horizon Radius

Equation

$$r_+ = \frac{GM}{c^2} + \sqrt{\left(\frac{GM}{c^2}\right)^2 - \left(\frac{J}{Mc}\right)^2}$$

Literal Reading

Read it as: r plus equals G M over c squared plus the square root of a mass term squared minus a spin term squared.

Common Reading

The outer event horizon radius of a rotating uncharged black hole.

Symbols

\(r_+\): outer Kerr horizon radius.

\(M\): black hole mass.

\(J\): angular momentum.

\(c\): speed of light.

Meaning of the Equation

This relation connects geometry, motion, horizons, or gravitational sources. It tells what clocks, distances, curvature, or observers would measure, so the physical claim is tied to spacetime structure rather than to a picture or metaphor.

Scope and Limits

The square root requires the angular momentum not to exceed the extremal Kerr bound. If the spin term were too large, the classical horizon would disappear, signaling that the assumed black-hole solution is no longer valid.
