Kerr Horizon Radius

Equation

$$r_+ = \frac{GM}{c^2} + \sqrt{\left(\frac{GM}{c^2}\right)^2 - \left(\frac{J}{Mc}\right)^2}$$

Literal Reading

r plus equals G M over c squared plus the square root of a mass term squared minus a spin term squared.

Physicist's Reading

A physicist reads this as the outer event horizon radius of a rotating uncharged black hole.

Symbol Breakdown

\(r_+\): outer Kerr horizon radius.

\(M\): black hole mass.

\(J\): angular momentum.

\(c\): speed of light.

Physical Meaning

Spin changes the horizon structure. A rotating black hole is not just a heavier version of the Schwarzschild case; its rotation reshapes the geometry around it.

Scope and Limit

The square root requires the angular momentum not to exceed the extremal Kerr bound. If the spin term were too large, the classical horizon would disappear, signaling that the assumed black-hole solution is no longer valid.
