Quantum Channel Kraus Representation

Equation

$$ \mathcal{E}(\rho) = \sum_k K_k \rho K_k^\dagger,\qquad \sum_k K_k^\dagger K_k = I $$

What the Formula Says

E of rho equals the sum over k of K k rho K k dagger, with the sum of K k dagger K k equal to the identity.

What It Means in Practice

This means the general form of a quantum channel acting on a density matrix while preserving total probability.

Symbols

\(\mathcal{E}\): the quantum channel or physical process.

\(\rho\): the input density matrix.

\(K_k\): Kraus operators representing possible effects of the channel.

\(K_k^\dagger\): the conjugate transpose of \(K_k\).

\(I\): the identity operator.

Why It Matters

This equation describes how quantum states change when they pass through a real physical process. It can represent noise, loss, measurement like interactions, or controlled operations. The second condition makes sure probabilities remain consistent.
