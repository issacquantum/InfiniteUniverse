Quantum Channel Kraus Representation

Equation

$$ \mathcal{E}(\rho) = \sum_k K_k \rho K_k^\dagger,\qquad \sum_k K_k^\dagger K_k = I $$

Literal Reading

E of rho equals the sum over k of K sub k rho K sub k dagger, with the sum over k of K sub k dagger K sub k equal to I.

Common Reading

A quantum channel maps an input density matrix to an output density matrix by summing over unobserved Kraus-operation paths.

Symbols

\(\mathcal{E}\): the quantum channel or physical process.

\(\rho\): the input density matrix.

\(K_k\): Kraus operators representing possible effects of the channel.

\(K_k^\dagger\): the conjugate transpose of \(K_k\).

\(I\): the identity operator.

Meaning of the Equation

The condition \(\sum_k K_k^\dagger K_k=I\) makes the channel trace-preserving, so total probability is conserved.
