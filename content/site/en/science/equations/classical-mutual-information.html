Classical Mutual Information

Equation

$$ I(X;Y)=H(X)+H(Y)-H(X,Y) $$

Literal Reading

I of X and Y equals the entropy of X plus the entropy of Y minus their joint entropy.

Physicist's Reading

A physicist would read this as the amount of uncertainty removed about one variable when the other variable is known.

Symbol Breakdown

\(I(X;Y)\): mutual information between random variables \(X\) and \(Y\).

\(H(X)\) and \(H(Y)\): individual Shannon entropies.

\(H(X,Y)\): joint entropy of the pair.

Physical Meaning

Mutual information measures shared structure. If \(X\) and \(Y\) are independent, it is zero. If one strongly predicts the other, it is larger.
