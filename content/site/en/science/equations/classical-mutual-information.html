Classical Mutual Information

Equation

$$ I(X;Y)=H(X)+H(Y)-H(X,Y) $$

Literal Reading

I of X;Y equals H of X plus H of Y minus H of X comma Y.

Common Reading

The amount of uncertainty removed about one variable when the other variable is known.

Symbols

\(I(X;Y)\): mutual information between random variables \(X\) and \(Y\).

\(H(X)\) and \(H(Y)\): individual Shannon entropies.

\(H(X,Y)\): joint entropy of the pair.

Meaning of the Equation

Mutual information detects statistical dependence between variables. It does not by itself say which variable causes the other or whether an external common cause exists.
