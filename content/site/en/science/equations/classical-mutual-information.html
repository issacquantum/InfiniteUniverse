Classical Mutual Information

Equation

$$ I(X;Y)=H(X)+H(Y)-H(X,Y) $$

Literal Reading

I of X and Y equals the entropy of X plus the entropy of Y minus their joint entropy.

Common Reading

Short version: The amount of uncertainty removed about one variable when the other variable is known.

Symbols

\(I(X;Y)\): mutual information between random variables \(X\) and \(Y\).

\(H(X)\) and \(H(Y)\): individual Shannon entropies.

\(H(X,Y)\): joint entropy of the pair.

Meaning of the Equation

The amount of uncertainty removed about one variable when the other variable is known.
