Classical Mutual Information

Equation

$$ I(X;Y)=H(X)+H(Y)-H(X,Y) $$

Literal Reading

Read it as: I open parenthesis X;Y close parenthesis equals H open parenthesis X close parenthesis plus H open parenthesis Y close parenthesis minus H open parenthesis X comma Y close parenthesis.

Common Reading

The amount of uncertainty removed about one variable when the other variable is known.

Symbols

\(I(X;Y)\): mutual information between random variables \(X\) and \(Y\).

\(H(X)\) and \(H(Y)\): individual Shannon entropies.

\(H(X,Y)\): joint entropy of the pair.

Meaning of the Equation

It measures how much knowing one system tells you about another.
