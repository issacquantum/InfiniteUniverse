Quantum Harmonic Oscillator Energy Levels

Equation

$$ E_n = \hbar\omega\left(n + \frac{1}{2}\right), \quad n = 0, 1, 2, \ldots $$

What the Formula Says

E sub n equals h bar omega times n plus one half, with n equal to zero, one, two, and so on.

What It Means in Practice

This means the oscillator has equally spaced quantum energies with a nonzero zero-point ground state.

Symbols

\(E_n\): the allowed energy of level \(n\).

\(\hbar\): the reduced Planck constant.

\(\omega\): the angular frequency of the oscillator.

\(n\): the excitation level, starting at zero.

\(1/2\): the zero-point contribution that never disappears.

Equation Meaning

Unlike a classical oscillator, the quantum oscillator cannot have zero energy. Even its ground state fluctuates.
