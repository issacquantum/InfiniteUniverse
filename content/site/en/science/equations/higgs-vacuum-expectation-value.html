Higgs Vacuum Expectation Value

Equation

$$\langle H \rangle \neq 0$$

What the Formula Says

The expectation value of H is not equal to zero.

What It Means in Practice

This means the Higgs field acquires a nonzero vacuum expectation value.

Symbols

\(\langle H \rangle\): vacuum expectation value of the Higgs field.

\(\neq 0\): not zero.

Why It Matters

This compact statement marks electroweak symmetry breaking. Once the Higgs field settles into a nonzero vacuum value, many elementary particles acquire mass through their interaction with that field.
