Lorentz Factor

Equation

$$\gamma = \frac{1}{\sqrt{1 - v^2/c^2}}$$

What the Formula Says

Gamma equals one divided by the square root of one minus v squared over c squared.

What It Means in Practice

This means the scale factor that controls relativistic time dilation, length contraction, and energy growth.

Symbols

\(\gamma\): Lorentz factor.

\(v\): relative speed between inertial frames.

\(c\): speed of light in vacuum.

Equation Meaning

When speeds are small compared with light speed, \(\gamma\) is almost one and classical physics is recovered. As speed approaches light speed, \(\gamma\) grows without bound, making it impossible for a massive object to reach light speed.
