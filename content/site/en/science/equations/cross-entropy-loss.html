Cross Entropy Loss

Equation

$$ L=-\sum_i y_i\log(\hat{y}_i) $$

Literal Reading

L equals minus sum sub i y sub i log of p sub i.

Common Reading

Cross-entropy loss is the negative log-likelihood of the target labels under the model’s predicted probability distribution.

Symbols

\(L\): loss value.

\(y_i\): true label indicator or target probability.

\(\hat{y}_i\): predicted probability for class \(i\).

Meaning of the Equation

It penalizes the model when it assigns low probability to the correct target, rather than being generic thermodynamic entropy.
