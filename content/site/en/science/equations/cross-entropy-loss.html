Cross Entropy Loss

Equation

$$ L=-\sum_i y_i\log(\hat{y}_i) $$

Literal Reading

L equals minus sum sub i y sub i log of p sub i.

Common Reading

Entropy measures uncertainty, disorder, or missing information in the relevant physical or informational state.

Symbols

\(L\): loss value.

\(y_i\): true label indicator or target probability.

\(\hat{y}_i\): predicted probability for class \(i\).

Meaning of the Equation

The loss becomes small when the model assigns high probability to the correct answer and large when it is confidently wrong. It trains probability predictions, not truth itself.
