Matrix-Vector Transformation

Equation

$$\mathbf{v}^{\prime}=A\mathbf{v}$$

Plain Meaning

A rule \(A\) takes one vector and produces a transformed vector.

Symbols

  • \(\mathbf{v}\): the original vector.
  • \(A\): the matrix representing the transformation.
  • \(\mathbf{v}^{\prime}\): the vector after the transformation.

How to Read It

Read it as a controlled change of coordinates, direction, scale, or state. This is what the foundational mathematics model is showing.

Meaning in Context

The equation means a matrix transforms a vector into a new vector. Depending on context, the transformation may represent physical motion, scaling, rotation, projection, or a change of representation.
