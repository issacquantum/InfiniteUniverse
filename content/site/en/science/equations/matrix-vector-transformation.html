Matrix-Vector Transformation

Equation

$$\mathbf{v}^{\prime}=A\mathbf{v}$$

Literal Reading

Read it as: the transformed vector v prime equals matrix A times vector v.

Common Reading

A rule \(A\) takes one vector and produces a transformed vector.

Symbols

  • \(\mathbf{v}\): the original vector.
  • \(A\): the matrix representing the transformation.
  • \(\mathbf{v}^{\prime}\): the vector after the transformation.

Meaning of the Equation

This relation turns an algorithmic step into a precise rule. It identifies what is being updated, counted, or bounded, so the process can be checked by its state, cost, error, or learning behavior instead of only by a visual animation.
