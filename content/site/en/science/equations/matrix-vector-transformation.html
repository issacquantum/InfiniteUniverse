Matrix-Vector Transformation

Equation

$$\mathbf{v}^{\prime}=A\mathbf{v}$$

Literal Reading

y equals A x.

Common Reading

A rule \(A\) takes one vector and produces a transformed vector.

Symbols

  • \(\mathbf{v}\): the original vector.
  • \(A\): the matrix representing the transformation.
  • \(\mathbf{v}^{\prime}\): the vector after the transformation.

Meaning of the Equation

A matrix-vector product combines input components into new output components. It is the basic linear operation behind rotations, projections, networks, and numerical algorithms.
