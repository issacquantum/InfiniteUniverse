Explicit Stationary Schrödinger Equation

Equation

$$ -\frac{\hbar^2}{2m}\nabla^2\psi(\mathbf{r}) + V(\mathbf{r})\psi(\mathbf{r}) = E\psi(\mathbf{r}) $$

Literal Reading

open bracket minus h minus bar to the power of 2 over of 2m nabla to the power of 2 plus V of r close bracket psi of r equals E psi of r.

Common Reading

The quantum state is governed by the Hamiltonian, which determines its time evolution or allowed energies.

Symbols

\(\hbar\): the reduced Planck constant.

\(m\): the mass of the particle.

\(\nabla^2\): the Laplacian, which measures spatial curvature of the wavefunction.

\(\psi(\mathbf{r})\): the spatial wavefunction.

\(V(\mathbf{r})\): the potential energy function.

\(E\): the total energy eigenvalue.

Meaning of the Equation

The wavefunction contains the quantum state; the Hamiltonian determines how that state behaves.
