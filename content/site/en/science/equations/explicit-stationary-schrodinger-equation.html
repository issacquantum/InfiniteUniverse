Explicit Stationary Schrödinger Equation

Equation

$$ -\frac{\hbar^2}{2m}\nabla^2\psi(\mathbf{r}) + V(\mathbf{r})\psi(\mathbf{r}) = E\psi(\mathbf{r}) $$

Literal Reading

Minus h bar squared over two m, nabla squared psi of r, plus V of r psi of r, equals E psi of r.

Physicist's Reading

A physicist would read it as kinetic energy plus potential energy acting on the wavefunction equals the total energy times the wavefunction.

Symbol Breakdown

\(\hbar\): the reduced Planck constant.

\(m\): the mass of the particle.

\(\nabla^2\): the Laplacian, which measures spatial curvature of the wavefunction.

\(\psi(\mathbf{r})\): the spatial wavefunction.

\(V(\mathbf{r})\): the potential energy function.

\(E\): the total energy eigenvalue.

Physical Meaning

This is the stationary Schrödinger equation written with the Hamiltonian expanded into kinetic and potential terms.
