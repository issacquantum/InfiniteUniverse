Von Neumann Entropy

Equation

$$ S(\rho) = -\operatorname{Tr}(\rho \log_2 \rho) $$

What the Formula Says

S of rho equals minus the trace of rho times log base two of rho.

What It Means in Practice

This means the quantum entropy of a state is computed from its density matrix and measures how mixed or uncertain that state is.

Symbols

\(S(\rho)\): the von Neumann entropy of the quantum state.

\(\rho\): the density matrix describing the state.

\(\operatorname{Tr}\): the trace operation, which sums the diagonal values of an operator in a basis independent way.

\(\log_2\): logarithm base two, used so entropy is measured in bits.

Why It Matters

This equation is the quantum version of entropy for information. A pure state has zero von Neumann entropy. A mixed state has nonzero entropy. In entanglement, a full system can be pure while each subsystem has entropy, showing that information can live in relationships between parts.
