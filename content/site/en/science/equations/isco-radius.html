ISCO (innermost stable circular orbit) Radius

Equation

$$r_{\text{ISCO}} = \frac{6GM}{c^2}$$

Literal Reading

The ISCO radius equals six G M divided by c squared.

Physicist's Reading

A physicist reads this as the innermost stable circular orbit for matter around a non rotating black hole.

Symbol Breakdown

\(r_{\text{ISCO}}\): innermost stable circular orbit radius.

\(M\): black hole mass.

\(G\): gravitational constant.

\(c\): speed of light.

Physical Meaning

Inside this radius, stable circular motion is no longer possible for ordinary matter in the Schwarzschild geometry. This helps set the inner edge of an accretion disk.

Scope and Limit

The value \(6GM/c^2\) is the Schwarzschild result. Around rotating Kerr black holes, the innermost stable orbit depends on spin and on whether the orbit moves with or against the rotation.
