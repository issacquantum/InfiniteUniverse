Length Contraction

Equation

$$L' = \frac{L}{\gamma}$$

What the Formula Says

L prime equals L divided by gamma.

What It Means in Practice

This means a moving object's length along the direction of motion being measured shorter than its rest length.

Symbols

\(L\): proper length measured in the object's rest frame.

\(L'\): contracted length measured by a relatively moving observer.

\(\gamma\): Lorentz factor.

Why It Matters

Length contraction is a coordinate effect tied to simultaneity. Measuring the endpoints of a moving object requires deciding what counts as the same time, and simultaneity itself changes between inertial frames.
