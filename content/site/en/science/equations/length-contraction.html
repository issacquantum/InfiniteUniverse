Length Contraction

Equation

$$L' = \frac{L}{\gamma}$$

Literal Reading

Read it as: l prime equals L divided by gamma.

Common Reading

A moving object's length along the direction of motion being measured shorter than its rest length.

Symbols

\(L\): proper length measured in the object's rest frame.

\(L'\): contracted length measured by a relatively moving observer.

\(\gamma\): Lorentz factor.

Meaning of the Equation

This relation connects geometry, motion, horizons, or gravitational sources. It tells what clocks, distances, curvature, or observers would measure, so the physical claim is tied to spacetime structure rather than to a picture or metaphor.
