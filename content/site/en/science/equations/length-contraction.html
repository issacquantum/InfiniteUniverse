Length Contraction

Equation

$$L' = \frac{L}{\gamma}$$

Literal Reading

L equals L0 over gamma.

Common Reading

A moving object's length along the direction of motion being measured shorter than its rest length.

Symbols

\(L\): proper length measured in the object's rest frame.

\(L'\): contracted length measured by a relatively moving observer.

\(\gamma\): Lorentz factor.

Meaning of the Equation

Objects moving relative to an observer are measured shorter along the direction of motion. This is a spacetime measurement effect, not physical compression in the object's own rest frame.
