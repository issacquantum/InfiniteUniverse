Quantum State-Space Dimension

Equation

$$ \dim \mathcal{H}_n = 2^n $$

Literal Reading

dim of ℋ equals 2 to the power of n.

Common Reading

Each extra qubit doubles the dimension of the state space.

Symbols

\(\mathcal{H}_n\): the Hilbert space of an \(n\)-qubit system.

\(\dim\): dimension, meaning the number of independent basis directions.

\(2^n\): exponential growth from \(n\) two-level systems.

Meaning of the Equation

A system of n qubits needs 2^n basis amplitudes for a general state. That exponential growth is why large quantum states are hard to store directly on classical computers.
