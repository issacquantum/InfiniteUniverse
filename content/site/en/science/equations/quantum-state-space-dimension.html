Quantum State-Space Dimension

Equation

$$ \dim \mathcal{H}_n = 2^n $$

Literal Reading

Read it as: the dimension of the Hilbert space for \(n\) qubits is two to the power \(n\).

Common Reading

Each extra qubit doubles the dimension of the state space.

Symbols

\(\mathcal{H}_n\): the Hilbert space of an \(n\)-qubit system.

\(\dim\): dimension, meaning the number of independent basis directions.

\(2^n\): exponential growth from \(n\) two-level systems.

Meaning of the Equation

An \(n\)-qubit pure state needs amplitudes for \(2^n\) computational-basis states. This explains why direct classical simulation becomes hard quickly. It does not mean a quantum computer can read out \(2^n\) answers; measurement still returns limited classical information.
