Monte Carlo Standard Error

Equation

$$ SE(\hat{\mu}_N)=\frac{\sigma_f}{\sqrt{N}} $$

Literal Reading

The standard error of the Monte Carlo estimate equals the standard deviation of the sampled quantity divided by the square root of the number of samples.

Physicist's Reading

Random sampling improves slowly but predictably: reducing uncertainty by a factor of 10 usually requires about 100 times more samples.

Symbol Breakdown

\(SE\): standard error of the estimate.

\(\hat{\mu}_N\): sample average after \(N\) samples.

\(\sigma_f\): standard deviation of the sampled function values.

\(N\): number of samples.

Physical Meaning

The equation explains why Monte Carlo is powerful in high dimensions but noisy: convergence scales as \(1/\sqrt{N}\), not \(1/N\).
