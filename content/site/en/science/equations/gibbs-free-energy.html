Gibbs Free Energy

Equation

$$\Delta G=\Delta H-T\Delta S$$

Literal Reading

Read it as: delta G equals delta H minus T delta S.

Common Reading

This estimates whether a process is thermodynamically favored at constant temperature and pressure.

Symbols

  • \(\Delta G\): Gibbs free-energy change.
  • \(\Delta H\): enthalpy change.
  • \(T\): absolute temperature.
  • \(\Delta S\): entropy change.

Meaning of the Equation

This relation connects microscopic structure, energy, probability, or reaction behavior to a measurable physical result. It shows which conditions control the process and what changes when temperature, energy, particles, or available states change.
