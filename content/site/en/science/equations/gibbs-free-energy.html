Gibbs Free Energy

Equation

$$\Delta G=\Delta H-T\Delta S$$

Literal Reading

The change in Gibbs free energy equals the change in enthalpy minus temperature times the change in entropy.

Chemist's Reading

It helps judge whether a process is thermodynamically favored under constant temperature and pressure.

Symbol Breakdown

\(\Delta G\): Gibbs free-energy change.

\(\Delta H\): enthalpy change.

\(T\): absolute temperature.

\(\Delta S\): entropy change.

Physical Meaning

Negative \(\Delta G\) means the process is thermodynamically favored, but reaction speed still depends on kinetics and barriers.
