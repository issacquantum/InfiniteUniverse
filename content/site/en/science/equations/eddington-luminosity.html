Eddington Luminosity

Equation

$$L_{\rm Edd} = \frac{4\pi GMm_p c}{\sigma_T}$$

Literal Reading

The Eddington luminosity equals four pi times G, M, proton mass, and c divided by the Thomson cross section.

Physicist's Reading

A physicist reads this as the luminosity where outward radiation pressure balances inward gravity for ionized hydrogen.

Symbol Breakdown

\(L_{\rm Edd}\): Eddington luminosity.

\(M\): mass of the compact object.

\(m_p\): proton mass.

\(\sigma_T\): Thomson scattering cross section.

Physical Meaning

This sets a natural growth scale. If accretion shines too strongly, radiation can push gas away and slow further feeding.

Scope and Limit

The formula assumes spherical ionized hydrogen and Thomson scattering. Disks, magnetic fields, anisotropic radiation, and composition can shift the practical limit, so it is a benchmark rather than an absolute wall.
