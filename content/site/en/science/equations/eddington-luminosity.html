Eddington Luminosity

Equation

$$L_{\rm Edd} = \frac{4\pi GMm_p c}{\sigma_T}$$

Literal Reading

Read it as: the Eddington luminosity equals four pi times G, M, proton mass, and c divided by the Thomson cross section.

Common Reading

The luminosity where outward radiation pressure balances inward gravity for ionized hydrogen.

Symbols

\(L_{\rm Edd}\): Eddington luminosity.

\(M\): mass of the compact object.

\(m_p\): proton mass.

\(\sigma_T\): Thomson scattering cross section.

Meaning of the Equation

This relation connects geometry, motion, horizons, or gravitational sources. It tells what clocks, distances, curvature, or observers would measure, so the physical claim is tied to spacetime structure rather than to a picture or metaphor.

Scope and Limits

The formula assumes spherical ionized hydrogen and Thomson scattering. Disks, magnetic fields, anisotropic radiation, and composition can shift the practical limit, so it is a benchmark rather than an absolute wall.
