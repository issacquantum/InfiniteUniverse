Grover Algorithm Quadratic Runtime

Equation

$$ \mathcal{O}(\sqrt{N}) $$

What the Formula Says

Big O of square root of N.

What It Means in Practice

This means the query complexity scaling of Grover's search algorithm over an unstructured search space of size \(N\).

Symbols

\(\mathcal{O}\): big-O notation, used to summarize asymptotic growth.

\(N\): the total number of candidate entries in the search space.

\(\sqrt{N}\): the quadratic improvement over a linear classical scan.

Why It Matters

Grover's algorithm does not give an exponential speedup, but it is still important because it systematically reduces the number of oracle queries from order \(N\) to order \(\sqrt{N}\). That makes it one of the clearest examples of a broad quantum advantage in search complexity.
