First Friedmann Equation

Equation

$$\left(\frac{\dot{a}}{a}\right)^2 = \frac{8\pi G}{3}\rho - \frac{k}{a^2} + \frac{\Lambda}{3}$$

Literal Reading

Read it as: H to the power of 2 equals open parenthesis 8 pi G over 3 close parenthesis rho minus k c to the power of 2 over a to the power of 2 plus Lambda c to the power of 2 over 3.

Common Reading

The expansion rate of the universe is determined by energy density, curvature, pressure, and the cosmological constant.

Symbols

\(a\): scale factor of the universe.

\(\dot{a}\): time derivative of the scale factor.

\(\rho\): total energy density.

\(G\): gravitational constant.

\(k\): spatial curvature parameter.

\(\Lambda\): cosmological constant.

Meaning of the Equation

It connects the quantities in the formula so the relation can be used to compute or interpret a situation in Cosmology & the Early Universe.
