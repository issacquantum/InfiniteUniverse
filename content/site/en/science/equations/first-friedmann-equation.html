First Friedmann Equation

Equation

$$\left(\frac{\dot{a}}{a}\right)^2 = \frac{8\pi G}{3}\rho - \frac{k}{a^2} + \frac{\Lambda}{3}$$

Literal Reading

The square of a dot over a equals eight pi G over three times rho minus k over a squared plus lambda over three.

Physicist's Reading

A physicist would read it as the expansion rate squared is determined by the energy density, spatial curvature, and cosmological constant.

Symbol Breakdown

\(a\): scale factor of the universe.

\(\dot{a}\): time derivative of the scale factor.

\(\rho\): total energy density.

\(G\): gravitational constant.

\(k\): spatial curvature parameter.

\(\Lambda\): cosmological constant.

Physical Meaning

This equation is one of the central dynamical equations of cosmology. It tells us how fast the universe expands at a given moment and how that rate depends on matter, radiation, curvature, and dark energy. It is the mathematical backbone of modern large-scale cosmology.
