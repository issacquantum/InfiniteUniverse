First Friedmann Equation

Equation

$$\left(\frac{\dot{a}}{a}\right)^2 = \frac{8\pi G}{3}\rho - \frac{k}{a^2} + \frac{\Lambda}{3}$$

Literal Reading

Read it as: the square of a dot over a equals eight pi G over three times rho minus k over a squared plus lambda over three.

Common Reading

The expansion rate squared is determined by the energy density, spatial curvature, and cosmological constant.

Symbols

\(a\): scale factor of the universe.

\(\dot{a}\): time derivative of the scale factor.

\(\rho\): total energy density.

\(G\): gravitational constant.

\(k\): spatial curvature parameter.

\(\Lambda\): cosmological constant.

Meaning of the Equation

This relation connects geometry, motion, horizons, or gravitational sources. It tells what clocks, distances, curvature, or observers would measure, so the physical claim is tied to spacetime structure rather than to a picture or metaphor.
