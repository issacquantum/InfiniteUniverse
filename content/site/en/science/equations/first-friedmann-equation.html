First Friedmann Equation

Equation

$$\left(\frac{\dot{a}}{a}\right)^2 = \frac{8\pi G}{3}\rho - \frac{k c^2}{a^2} + \frac{\Lambda c^2}{3}$$

Literal Reading

the square of dot a over a equals 8 pi G over 3 times rho minus k c squared over a squared plus Lambda c squared over 3.

Common Reading

The squared expansion rate is determined by energy density, spatial curvature, and the cosmological constant.

Symbols

\(a\): scale factor of the universe.

\(\dot{a}\): time derivative of the scale factor.

\(\rho\): mass density / energy density divided by \(c^2\).

\(G\): gravitational constant.

\(k\): spatial curvature parameter.

\(c\): speed of light.

\(\Lambda\): cosmological constant.

Meaning of the Equation

Pressure does not appear in the first Friedmann equation; pressure enters the acceleration equation.
