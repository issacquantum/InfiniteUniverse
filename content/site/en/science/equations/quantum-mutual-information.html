Quantum Mutual Information

Equation

$$ I(A:B) = S(A) + S(B) - S(AB) $$

What the Formula Says

I of A colon B equals entropy of A plus entropy of B minus entropy of A B.

What It Means in Practice

This means the total correlation between systems A and B equals the entropy of each part minus the entropy of the combined system.

Symbols

\(I(A:B)\): the quantum mutual information between subsystems A and B.

\(S(A)\): the von Neumann entropy of subsystem A.

\(S(B)\): the von Neumann entropy of subsystem B.

\(S(AB)\): the entropy of the joint system containing A and B together.

Why It Matters

This quantity measures how strongly two quantum systems are correlated. If the parts tell us nothing about each other, the mutual information is zero. If the parts are related, the value grows. In quantum information, those relationships can include both ordinary correlation and entanglement.
