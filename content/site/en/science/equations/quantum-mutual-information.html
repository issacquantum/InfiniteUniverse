Quantum Mutual Information

Equation

$$ I(A:B) = S(A) + S(B) - S(AB) $$

Literal Reading

Read it as: i of A colon B equals entropy of A plus entropy of B minus entropy of A B.

Common Reading

Mutual information measures total correlation between quantum subsystems.

Symbols

\(I(A:B)\): the quantum mutual information between subsystems A and B.

\(S(A)\): the von Neumann entropy of subsystem A.

\(S(B)\): the von Neumann entropy of subsystem B.

\(S(AB)\): the entropy of the joint system containing A and B together.

Meaning of the Equation

This relation measures uncertainty, correlation, capacity, or distinguishability. It tells how much information is present, lost, shared, or recoverable, so the concept becomes a quantity that can be compared rather than just described in words.
