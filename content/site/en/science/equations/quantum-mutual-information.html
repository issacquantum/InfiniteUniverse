Quantum Mutual Information

Equation

$$ I(A:B) = S(A) + S(B) - S(AB) $$

Literal Reading

I of A colon B equals S of A plus S of B minus S of AB.

Common Reading

Quantum mutual information measures total correlation between A and B, including classical correlation and quantum correlation.

Symbols

\(I(A:B)\): the quantum mutual information between subsystems A and B.

\(S(A)\): the von Neumann entropy of subsystem A.

\(S(B)\): the von Neumann entropy of subsystem B.

\(S(AB)\): the entropy of the joint system containing A and B together.

Meaning of the Equation

It is not only an entanglement measure; mixed states can have mutual information from classical correlations as well.
