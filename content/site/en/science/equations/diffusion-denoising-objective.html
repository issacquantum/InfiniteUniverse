Diffusion Denoising Objective

Equation

$$ L_{\rm simple}=\mathbb{E}_{t,x_0,\epsilon}\!\left[\|\epsilon-\epsilon_\theta(x_t,t)\|^2\right] $$

What the Formula Says

The model minimizes the squared difference between the noise that was added and the noise it predicts.

What It Means in Practice

A diffusion model learns to reverse a gradual noising process by predicting how to denoise each intermediate state.

Symbols

\(x_0\): original clean data.

\(x_t\): noised data at timestep \(t\).

\(\epsilon\): sampled noise.

\(\epsilon_\theta\): neural network prediction of that noise.

Why It Matters

The model does not create from nothing. It learns a controlled reverse process from noise toward structure.
