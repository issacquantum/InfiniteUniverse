Born Rule and POVM (positive operator-valued measure) Measurement

Equation

$$ P(m) = \langle \psi | M_m^\dagger M_m | \psi \rangle $$ $$ |\psi_m\rangle = \frac{M_m|\psi\rangle}{\sqrt{P(m)}},\qquad \sum_m E_m = I $$

Literal Reading

The probability of outcome \(m\) is the expectation value of \(M_m^\dagger M_m\). After the outcome, the state is updated by the corresponding measurement operator and normalized.

Physicist's Reading

A physicist would read this as the operational rule that connects quantum amplitudes to measurable classical outcomes.

Symbol Breakdown

\(P(m)\): probability of measurement result \(m\).

\(|\psi\rangle\): the state before measurement.

\(M_m\): the measurement operator for outcome \(m\).

\(M_m^\dagger\): the Hermitian adjoint of \(M_m\).

\(E_m\): a POVM effect, equal to \(M_m^\dagger M_m\) in this representation.

\(I\): the identity operator.

Physical Meaning

This is where quantum information becomes a protocol. A quantum state carries amplitudes, but only measurement produces a classical record, and the probabilities must follow the Born rule.
