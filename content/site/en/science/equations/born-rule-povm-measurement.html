Born Rule and POVM (positive operator-valued measure) Measurement

Equation

$$ P(m) = \langle \psi | M_m^\dagger M_m | \psi \rangle $$ $$ |\psi_m\rangle = \frac{M_m|\psi\rangle}{\sqrt{P(m)}},\qquad \sum_m E_m = I $$

Literal Reading

p of i equals trace of M sub i rho.

Common Reading

Quantum probabilities come from measurement operators acting on the state.

Symbols

\(P(m)\): probability of measurement result \(m\).

\(|\psi\rangle\): the state before measurement.

\(M_m\): the measurement operator for outcome \(m\).

\(M_m^\dagger\): the Hermitian adjoint of \(M_m\).

\(E_m\): a POVM effect, equal to \(M_m^\dagger M_m\) in this representation.

\(I\): the identity operator.

Meaning of the Equation

The trace turns the quantum state and the measurement effect into an ordinary probability. It is the bridge from a density matrix to predicted measurement statistics.
