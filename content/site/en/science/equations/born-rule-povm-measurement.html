Born Rule and POVM (positive operator-valued measure) Measurement

Equation

$$ E_m=M_m^\dagger M_m,\qquad \sum_m E_m=I $$ $$ P(m)=\operatorname{Tr}(E_m\rho),\qquad P(m)=\langle\psi|E_m|\psi\rangle\ \text{for a pure state} $$

Literal Reading

E sub m equals M sub m dagger M sub m, and the sum over m of E sub m equals I. P of m equals the trace of E sub m rho; for a pure state, P of m equals bra psi E sub m ket psi.

Common Reading

A POVM effect turns a quantum state into the probability of a measurement outcome.

Symbols

\(P(m)\): probability of measurement result \(m\).

\(|\psi\rangle\): the state before measurement.

\(M_m\): the measurement operator for outcome \(m\).

\(M_m^\dagger\): the Hermitian adjoint of \(M_m\).

\(E_m\): a POVM effect, equal to \(M_m^\dagger M_m\) in this representation.

\(I\): the identity operator.

Meaning of the Equation

The effects \(E_m\) are positive operators that add to the identity, so the probabilities of all possible outcomes add to one.
