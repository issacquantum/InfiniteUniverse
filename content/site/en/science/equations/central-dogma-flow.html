Central Dogma Flow

Equation

$$\mathrm{DNA}\rightarrow\mathrm{RNA}\rightarrow\mathrm{protein}$$

Literal Reading

Read it as: DNA goes to RNA, and RNA goes to protein.

Common Reading

Genetic sequence information is commonly copied from DNA to RNA and then used to build proteins.

Symbols

  • DNA: long-term hereditary sequence storage.
  • RNA: working transcript or regulatory molecule.
  • Protein: folded molecule that often performs cellular work.

Meaning of the Equation

This relation connects microscopic structure, energy, probability, or reaction behavior to a measurable physical result. It shows which conditions control the process and what changes when temperature, energy, particles, or available states change.
