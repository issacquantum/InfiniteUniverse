Hawking Temperature

Equation

$$T_H = \frac{\hbar \kappa}{2\pi c k_B} = \frac{\hbar c^3}{8\pi G M k_B}$$

Literal Reading

Hawking temperature equals h bar times surface gravity over two pi c k sub B, and for a Schwarzschild black hole it equals h bar c cubed over eight pi G M k sub B.

Physicist's Reading

A physicist reads this as the temperature associated with quantum radiation from a black hole horizon.

Symbol Breakdown

\(T_H\): Hawking temperature.

\(\hbar\): reduced Planck constant.

\(\kappa\): surface gravity.

\(k_B\): Boltzmann constant.

\(M\): black hole mass.

Physical Meaning

The temperature decreases as mass increases. Astrophysical black holes are therefore extremely cold, but the equation proves that horizons are quantum objects, not only classical boundaries.
