Hawking Temperature

Equation

$$T_H = \frac{\hbar \kappa}{2\pi c k_B} = \frac{\hbar c^3}{8\pi G M k_B}$$

Literal Reading

Hawking temperature equals h bar times surface gravity over two pi c k sub B, and for a Schwarzschild black hole it equals h bar c cubed over eight pi G M k sub B.

Common Reading

Short version: The temperature associated with quantum radiation from a black hole horizon.

Symbols

\(T_H\): Hawking temperature.

\(\hbar\): reduced Planck constant.

\(\kappa\): surface gravity.

\(k_B\): Boltzmann constant.

\(M\): black hole mass.

Meaning of the Equation

The temperature associated with quantum radiation from a black hole horizon.

Scope and Limits

The second equality is the Schwarzschild case. Rotation, charge, and dynamical horizons require modified surface gravity, and the final Planck-scale endpoint of evaporation is not described by this semiclassical formula.
