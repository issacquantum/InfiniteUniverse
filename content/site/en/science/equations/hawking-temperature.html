Hawking Temperature

Equation

$$T_H = \frac{\hbar \kappa}{2\pi c k_B} = \frac{\hbar c^3}{8\pi G M k_B}$$

Literal Reading

Read it as: T sub H equals h minus bar c to the power of 3 over open parenthesis 8 pi GMk sub B close parenthesis.

Common Reading

The black-hole temperature is inversely proportional to its mass.

Symbols

\(T_H\): Hawking temperature.

\(\hbar\): reduced Planck constant.

\(\kappa\): surface gravity.

\(k_B\): Boltzmann constant.

\(M\): black hole mass.

Meaning of the Equation

It connects the quantities in the formula so the relation can be used to compute or interpret a situation in Black Holes.

Scope and Limits

The second equality is the Schwarzschild case. Rotation, charge, and dynamical horizons require modified surface gravity, and the final Planck-scale endpoint of evaporation is not described by this semiclassical formula.
