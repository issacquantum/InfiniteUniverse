Time-Dependent Hubble Parameter

Equation

$$H(t) = \frac{\dot{a}(t)}{a(t)}$$

Literal Reading

H of t equals a dot of t over a of t.

Common Reading

Short version: The Hubble parameter is the fractional expansion rate of the scale factor.

Symbols

\(H(t)\): Hubble parameter at time \(t\).

\(a(t)\): scale factor as a function of time.

\(\dot{a}(t)\): time derivative of the scale factor.

Meaning of the Equation

The Hubble parameter is the fractional expansion rate of the scale factor.
