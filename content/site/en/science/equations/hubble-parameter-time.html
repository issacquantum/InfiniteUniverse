Time-Dependent Hubble Parameter

Equation

$$H(t) = \frac{\dot{a}(t)}{a(t)}$$

What the Formula Says

H of t equals a dot of t over a of t.

What It Means in Practice

This means the Hubble parameter is the fractional expansion rate of the scale factor.

Symbols

\(H(t)\): Hubble parameter at time \(t\).

\(a(t)\): scale factor as a function of time.

\(\dot{a}(t)\): time derivative of the scale factor.

Why It Matters

This equation defines the expansion rate of the universe at each moment. It measures how rapidly the scale factor changes relative to its current size, so it is a local-in-time measure of expansion, not just a single constant.
