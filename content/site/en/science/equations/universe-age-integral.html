Universe Age Integral

Equation

$$t_0 = \int_{0}^{\infty} \frac{dz}{(1+z)H(z)}$$

Literal Reading

Read it as: t naught equals the integral from zero to infinity of d z over open parenthesis one plus z close parenthesis H of z.

Common Reading

The age of the universe is obtained by integrating the inverse expansion rate over redshift.

Symbols

\(t_0\): present age of the universe.

\(z\): redshift.

\(H(z)\): Hubble parameter as a function of redshift.

\(\int_0^\infty\): integration from the present back toward the earliest observable epochs.

Meaning of the Equation

This relation connects geometry, motion, horizons, or gravitational sources. It tells what clocks, distances, curvature, or observers would measure, so the physical claim is tied to spacetime structure rather than to a picture or metaphor.
