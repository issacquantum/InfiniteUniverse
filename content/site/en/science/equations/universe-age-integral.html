Universe Age Integral

Equation

$$t_0 = \int_{0}^{\infty} \frac{dz}{(1+z)H(z)}$$

Literal Reading

t0 equals integral sub 0 to the power of ∞ dz over open bracket open parenthesis 1 plus z close parenthesis H open parenthesis z close parenthesis close bracket.

Common Reading

The age of the universe is obtained by integrating the inverse expansion rate over redshift.

Symbols

\(t_0\): present age of the universe.

\(z\): redshift.

\(H(z)\): Hubble parameter as a function of redshift.

\(\int_0^\infty\): integration from the present back toward the earliest observable epochs.

Meaning of the Equation

A definite integral adds up continuously many contributions over an interval.
