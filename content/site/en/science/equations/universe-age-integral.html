Universe Age Integral

Equation

$$t_0 = \int_{0}^{\infty} \frac{dz}{(1+z)H(z)}$$

Literal Reading

t0 equals integral sub 0 to the power of ∞ dz over open bracket of 1 plus z H of z close bracket.

Common Reading

This computes the age of an FLRW universe from its expansion history \(H(z)\).

Symbols

\(t_0\): present age of the universe.

\(z\): redshift.

\(H(z)\): Hubble parameter as a function of redshift.

\(\int_0^\infty\): integration from the present back toward the earliest observable epochs.

Meaning of the Equation

The result is model-dependent because it depends on the assumed expansion history and cosmological parameters.
