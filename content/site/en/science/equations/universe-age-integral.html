Universe Age Integral

Equation

$$t_0 = \int_{0}^{\infty} \frac{dz}{(1+z)H(z)}$$

What the Formula Says

t naught equals the integral from zero to infinity of d z over open parenthesis one plus z close parenthesis H of z.

What It Means in Practice

This means the age of the universe is obtained by integrating the inverse expansion rate over redshift.

Symbols

\(t_0\): present age of the universe.

\(z\): redshift.

\(H(z)\): Hubble parameter as a function of redshift.

\(\int_0^\infty\): integration from the present back toward the earliest observable epochs.

Why It Matters

This expression computes the age of the universe by summing how cosmic expansion behaves across its full history. It is more accurate than the simple Hubble-time estimate because it includes the changing influence of matter, radiation, and dark energy.
