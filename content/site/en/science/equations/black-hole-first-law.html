Black Hole First Law

Equation

$$dM = \frac{\kappa}{8\pi G}dA + \Omega_H dJ + \Phi_H dQ$$

Literal Reading

Read it as: the change in mass equals an area term plus a rotation term plus a charge term.

Common Reading

The black hole mechanics analogue of the first law of thermodynamics.

Symbols

\(dM\): change in black hole mass.

\(\kappa\): surface gravity.

\(dA\): change in horizon area.

\(\Omega_H\): angular velocity of the horizon.

\(dJ\): change in angular momentum.

\(\Phi_H dQ\): electric potential term.

Meaning of the Equation

This relation connects geometry, motion, horizons, or gravitational sources. It tells what clocks, distances, curvature, or observers would measure, so the physical claim is tied to spacetime structure rather than to a picture or metaphor.

Scope and Limits

This is a relation between nearby stationary black-hole states. It is not a full time-evolution equation for violent mergers, turbulent accretion, or the microscopic origin of entropy.
