Black Hole First Law

Equation

$$dM = \frac{\kappa}{8\pi G}dA + \Omega_H dJ + \Phi_H dQ$$

What the Formula Says

The change in mass equals an area term plus a rotation term plus a charge term.

What It Means in Practice

This means the black hole mechanics analogue of the first law of thermodynamics.

Symbols

\(dM\): change in black hole mass.

\(\kappa\): surface gravity.

\(dA\): change in horizon area.

\(\Omega_H\): angular velocity of the horizon.

\(dJ\): change in angular momentum.

\(\Phi_H dQ\): electric potential term.

Why It Matters

The equation shows that a black hole behaves like a thermodynamic system. Horizon area plays the role that entropy will later take in the quantum description.

Scope and Limits

This is a relation between nearby stationary black-hole states. It is not a full time-evolution equation for violent mergers, turbulent accretion, or the microscopic origin of entropy.
