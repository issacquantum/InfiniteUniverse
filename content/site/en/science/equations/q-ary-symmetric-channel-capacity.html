Symmetric Noisy Channel Capacity

Equation

$$ C=\log_2 q+(1-p)\log_2(1-p)+p\log_2\frac{p}{q-1} $$

Literal Reading

Read it as: capacity equals log base two of \(q\), plus one minus \(p\) times log base two of one minus \(p\), plus \(p\) times log base two of \(p\) divided by \(q-1\).

Common Reading

The maximum reliable information rate for a q-ary symmetric noisy channel.

Symbols

\(C\): channel capacity in bits per channel use.

\(q\): alphabet size, meaning the number of possible symbols the channel can send.

\(p\): total symbol error probability, meaning the chance that the received symbol is not the one sent.

\(\log_2\): logarithm base 2, so the result is measured in bits.

Meaning of the Equation

This formula says how much information can be sent per use of a symmetric noisy channel if the code is designed well enough and made long enough. A larger alphabet raises the possible information per symbol, while a larger error probability lowers it because the receiver has less certainty about what was sent.
