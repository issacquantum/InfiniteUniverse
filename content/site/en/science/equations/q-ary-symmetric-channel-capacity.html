Symmetric Noisy Channel Capacity

Equation

$$ C=\log_2 q+(1-p)\log_2(1-p)+p\log_2\frac{p}{q-1} $$

Literal Reading

Read it as: C equals log q minus H sub q open parenthesis p close parenthesis.

Common Reading

The maximum reliable information rate for a q-ary symmetric noisy channel.

Symbols

\(C\): channel capacity in bits per channel use.

\(q\): alphabet size, meaning the number of possible symbols the channel can send.

\(p\): total symbol error probability, meaning the chance that the received symbol is not the one sent.

\(\log_2\): logarithm base 2, so the result is measured in bits.

Meaning of the Equation

For an alphabet with \(q\) possible symbols, the ideal information per channel use is reduced by the uncertainty caused by symbol errors. The formula measures how many bits per use can still be transmitted reliably after accounting for that noise.
