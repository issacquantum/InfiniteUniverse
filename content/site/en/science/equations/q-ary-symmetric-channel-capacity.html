Symmetric Noisy Channel Capacity

Equation

$$ C=\log_2 q+(1-p)\log_2(1-p)+p\log_2\frac{p}{q-1} $$

What the Formula Says

Capacity equals the alphabet size term plus the weighted log probabilities of correct and incorrect transmission.

What It Means in Practice

This is the maximum reliable rate for a symmetric channel with \(q\) possible symbols and error probability \(p\).

Symbols

\(C\): channel capacity in bits per channel use.

\(q\): number of possible input symbols.

\(p\): probability that a sent symbol is changed into one of the other \(q-1\) symbols.

Why It Matters

Noise reduces usable rate, but it does not make communication hopeless. Below capacity, sufficiently long error-correcting codes can make the probability of failure arbitrarily small.
