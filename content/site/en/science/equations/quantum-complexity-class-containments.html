Quantum Complexity Class Containments

Equation

$$ P \subseteq BPP \subseteq BQP \subseteq PP \subseteq PSPACE $$

Literal Reading

Read it as: \(P\) is contained in \(BPP\), \(BPP\) is contained in \(BQP\), \(BQP\) is contained in \(PP\), and \(PP\) is contained in \(PSPACE\).

Common Reading

Efficient quantum computation fits inside a larger hierarchy of known complexity classes.

Symbols

\(P\): deterministic polynomial time.

\(BPP\): randomized polynomial time with bounded error.

\(BQP\): quantum polynomial time with bounded error.

\(PP\): probabilistic polynomial time with majority acceptance.

\(PSPACE\): problems solvable with polynomial memory.

Meaning of the Equation

This line gives known containments, not proven separations. It says quantum polynomial-time computation is at least as broad as efficient randomized computation, but still mathematically contained inside very large classical classes. It does not imply that \(BQP=NP\), and it does not prove quantum computers solve all hard problems.
