Black Hole Evaporation Time

Equation

$$\tau = \frac{5120\pi G^2M^3}{\hbar c^4}$$

Literal Reading

tau equals 5120 pi G squared M cubed over h bar c to the fourth.

Common Reading

The approximate lifetime of a non rotating uncharged black hole evaporating through Hawking radiation.

Symbols

\(\tau\): evaporation time.

\(M\): black hole mass.

\(G\): gravitational constant.

\(\hbar\): reduced Planck constant.

\(c\): speed of light.

Meaning of the Equation

This is an approximate lifetime for an isolated, nonrotating, uncharged Schwarzschild black hole; accretion, spin, charge, and the final quantum-gravity phase are excluded.

Scope and Limits

This estimate assumes an isolated Schwarzschild black hole and a semiclassical evaporation calculation. It ignores accretion, spin, charge, and the unknown final quantum-gravity phase.
