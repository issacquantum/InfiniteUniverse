Black Hole Evaporation Time

Equation

$$\tau = \frac{5120\pi G^2M^3}{\hbar c^4}$$

Literal Reading

Tau equals five thousand one hundred twenty pi G squared M cubed divided by h bar c to the fourth.

Physicist's Reading

A physicist reads this as the approximate lifetime of a non rotating uncharged black hole evaporating through Hawking radiation.

Symbol Breakdown

\(\tau\): evaporation time.

\(M\): black hole mass.

\(G\): gravitational constant.

\(\hbar\): reduced Planck constant.

\(c\): speed of light.

Physical Meaning

The lifetime grows with the cube of mass. Stellar and supermassive black holes last vastly longer than the current age of the universe.
