Feynman Propagator

Equation

$$D_F(x-y)=\langle 0|T\{\phi(x)\phi(y)\}|0\rangle$$

Literal Reading

D sub F of x minus y equals vacuum expectation value of the time-ordered product phi of x phi of y.

Common Reading

The Feynman propagator is the vacuum expectation value of the time-ordered product and gives the amplitude used for propagation or internal lines in perturbation theory.

Symbols

\(D_F\): Feynman propagator.

\(T\): time ordering operator.

\(|0\rangle\): vacuum state.

Meaning of the Equation

It is not a simple picture of a field influence traveling like a classical signal; it is a covariant quantum object used inside amplitude calculations.
