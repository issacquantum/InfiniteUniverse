Feynman Propagator

Equation

$$D_F(x-y)=\langle 0|T\{\phi(x)\phi(y)\}|0\rangle$$

What the Formula Says

The Feynman propagator equals the vacuum expectation value of the time ordered product of two fields.

What It Means in Practice

This means the amplitude structure describing how field disturbances correlate between spacetime points.

Symbols

\(D_F\): Feynman propagator.

\(T\): time ordering operator.

\(|0\rangle\): vacuum state.

Why It Matters

The propagator is central in perturbative QFT (quantum field theory). It is not a classical signal traveling faster than light; it is part of the quantum amplitude calculation.
