Feynman Propagator

Equation

$$D_F(x-y)=\langle 0|T\{\phi(x)\phi(y)\}|0\rangle$$

Literal Reading

Read it as: the Feynman propagator equals the vacuum expectation value of the time ordered product of two fields.

Common Reading

The amplitude structure describing how field disturbances correlate between spacetime points.

Symbols

\(D_F\): Feynman propagator.

\(T\): time ordering operator.

\(|0\rangle\): vacuum state.

Meaning of the Equation

This relation connects the mathematical state of a physical system to what can happen next or what can be measured. It is not just notation: it states how energy, fields, waves, or probabilities constrain the behavior of the system.
