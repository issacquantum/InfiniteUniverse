Euler-Lagrange Equation

Equation

$$\frac{d}{dt}\frac{\partial L}{\partial \dot q}-\frac{\partial L}{\partial q}=0$$

Literal Reading

d over dt of partial L over partial q sub dot minus partial L over partial q equals 0.

Common Reading

This is the rule that turns the action principle into an equation of motion.

Symbols

  • \(L\): Lagrangian.
  • \(q\): coordinate.
  • \(\dot q\): velocity-like rate of change.
  • \(t\): time.

Meaning of the Equation

Instead of starting from forces directly, the equation uses the action to derive motion. It works for particles, fields, and generalized coordinates when the right Lagrangian is chosen.
