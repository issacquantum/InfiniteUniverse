Kullback Leibler Divergence

Equation

$$ D_{\mathrm{KL}}(P\|Q)=\sum_x P(x)\log_2\frac{P(x)}{Q(x)} $$

Literal Reading

Read it as: d K L of P relative to Q equals the sum over x of P of x times the base two logarithm of P of x over Q of x.

Common Reading

It measures the information cost of treating data from distribution \(P\) as if it came from distribution \(Q\).

Symbols

\(P(x)\): the reference or true distribution.

\(Q(x)\): the comparison distribution or model.

\(\log_2\): base two logarithm, giving the result in bits.

Meaning of the Equation

This relation measures uncertainty, correlation, capacity, or distinguishability. It tells how much information is present, lost, shared, or recoverable, so the concept becomes a quantity that can be compared rather than just described in words.
