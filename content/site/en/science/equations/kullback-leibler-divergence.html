Kullback Leibler Divergence

Equation

$$ D_{\mathrm{KL}}(P\|Q)=\sum_x P(x)\log_2\frac{P(x)}{Q(x)} $$

Literal Reading

D sub KL of P norm Q equals sum sub x P of x log of P of x over Q of x.

Common Reading

It measures the information cost of treating data from distribution \(P\) as if it came from distribution \(Q\).

Symbols

\(P(x)\): the reference or true distribution.

\(Q(x)\): the comparison distribution or model.

\(\log_2\): base two logarithm, giving the result in bits.

Meaning of the Equation

KL divergence is not symmetric and is not a metric.
