Kullback Leibler Divergence

Equation

$$ D_{\mathrm{KL}}(P\|Q)=\sum_x P(x)\log_2\frac{P(x)}{Q(x)} $$

What the Formula Says

D K L of P relative to Q equals the sum over x of P of x times the base two logarithm of P of x over Q of x.

What It Means in Practice

It measures the information cost of treating data from distribution \(P\) as if it came from distribution \(Q\).

Symbols

\(P(x)\): the reference or true distribution.

\(Q(x)\): the comparison distribution or model.

\(\log_2\): base two logarithm, giving the result in bits.

Equation Meaning

Kullback Leibler divergence is not a distance because it is not symmetric. It is a directed measure of mismatch between a model and a distribution.
