L2-Norm (Euclidean Norm) Vector Sampling

Equation

$$ \Pr[i]=\frac{|x_i|^2}{\|x\|_2^2} $$

Literal Reading

Read it as: the probability of sampling index \(i\) equals the squared magnitude of \(x_i\) divided by the squared \(\ell^2\) norm of \(x\).

Common Reading

The same probability law produced by measuring an amplitude-encoded quantum state in the computational basis.

Symbols

\(\Pr[i]\): probability that the sampler returns index \(i\).

\(|x_i|^2\): squared magnitude of entry \(x_i\).

\(\|x\|_2^2\): sum of all squared magnitudes, \(\sum_i |x_i|^2\).

Meaning of the Equation

This relation states how the named quantities depend on each other. Its purpose is to turn a conceptual statement into a rule that can be calculated, compared, or tested inside the model where it is used.
