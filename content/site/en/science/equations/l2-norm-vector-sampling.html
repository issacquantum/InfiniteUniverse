L2-Norm (Euclidean Norm) Vector Sampling

Equation

$$ \Pr[i]=\frac{|x_i|^2}{\|x\|_2^2} $$

What the Formula Says

The probability of sampling index \(i\) equals the squared magnitude of \(x_i\) divided by the squared \(\ell^2\) norm of \(x\).

What It Means in Practice

This means the same probability law produced by measuring an amplitude-encoded quantum state in the computational basis.

Symbols

\(\Pr[i]\): probability that the sampler returns index \(i\).

\(|x_i|^2\): squared magnitude of entry \(x_i\).

\(\|x\|_2^2\): sum of all squared magnitudes, \(\sum_i |x_i|^2\).

Why It Matters

Length-square sampling gives a classical algorithm access to high-weight coordinates in a way that resembles quantum measurement. This is why Tang could compare certain quantum machine-learning algorithms against stronger classical baselines.
