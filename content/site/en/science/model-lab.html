Model Lab

This section gathers the interactive models in one place. Each model still belongs to its original science page, where the surrounding explanation gives it context. This page is the map: it tells you what each model is for, what it should not be taken to mean, and what to try changing first.

The purpose is educational clarity. A strong visual model should not ask the visitor to guess what is being shown. It should identify the idea, the boundary, the principle behind it, and the safest way to explore it.

Model Types

Physical model

Closely tied to a real equation or physical system.

Toy physical model

Simplified, but still built from real physical ideas.

Conceptual model

Teaches relationships and structure more than exact physics.

Analogy model

A useful teaching picture, not a literal physical object.

Artistic scientific visualization

Inspired by science, but not a numerical simulation or observational dataset.

Speculative conceptual model

Based on serious ideas, but not established physical reality.

Interactive Models

Double-Slit Interference

Type: Toy physical model.

Shows: How wavelength, slit spacing, and screen distance affect bright-band spacing.

Does not show: A full laboratory detector or a complete measurement theory.

Principle: \(y_m \approx m\lambda L/d\).

Try: Increase one variable at a time and watch the first bright band move.

Quantum Fluctuation Field

Type: Toy physical model.

Shows: A changing field sample drawn from a controlled random distribution.

Does not show: Particles appearing from nothing.

Principle: Vacuum variance and two-point correlation.

Try: Pause the field, change the seed, and compare the histogram.

Hydrogen Orbital Selector

Type: Physical model.

Shows: Hydrogen probability density \(|\psi|^2\) for allowed quantum numbers.

Does not show: A planet-like electron path.

Principle: \(0 \leq \ell < n\) and \(-\ell \leq m \leq \ell\).

Try: Start with presets, then compare density view with phase view.

Hydrogen 3dz² Case Study

Type: Physical model.

Shows: One worked orbital state sampled from its probability density.

Does not show: A separate theory from the orbital selector.

Principle: \(dP = |\psi_{320}|^2 r^2\sin\theta\,dr\,d\theta\,d\phi\).

Try: Compare this detailed case with the 3dz² preset.

Bell Singlet Entanglement
