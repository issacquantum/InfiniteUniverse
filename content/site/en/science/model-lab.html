Model Lab

This section gathers the interactive models in one place. Each model still belongs to its original science page, where the surrounding explanation gives it context. This page is the map: it tells you what each model is for, what it should not be taken to mean, and what to try changing first.

The purpose is educational clarity. A strong visual model should not ask the visitor to guess what is being shown. It should identify the idea, the boundary, the principle behind it, and the safest way to explore it.

Model Types

Physical model

Closely tied to a real equation or physical system.

Toy physical model

Simplified, but still built from real physical ideas.

Conceptual model

Teaches relationships and structure more than exact physics.

Analogy model

A useful teaching picture, not a literal physical object.

Artistic scientific visualization

Inspired by science, but not a numerical simulation or observational dataset.

Speculative conceptual model

Based on serious ideas, but not established physical reality.

Interactive Models

Double-Slit Interference

Type: Toy physical model.

Shows: How wavelength, slit spacing, and screen distance affect bright-band spacing.

Does not show: A full laboratory detector or a complete measurement theory.

Principle: \(y_m \approx m\lambda L/d\).

Try: Increase one variable at a time and watch the first bright band move.

Quantum Fluctuation Field

Type: Toy physical model.

Shows: A changing field sample drawn from a controlled random distribution.

Does not show: Particles appearing from nothing.

Principle: Vacuum variance and two-point correlation.

Try: Pause the field, change the seed, and compare the histogram.

Hydrogen Orbital Selector

Type: Physical model.

Shows: Hydrogen probability density \(|\psi|^2\) for allowed quantum numbers.

Does not show: A planet-like electron path.

Principle: \(0 \leq \ell < n\) and \(-\ell \leq m \leq \ell\).

Try: Start with presets, then compare density view with phase view.

Hydrogen 3dz² Case Study

Type: Physical model.

Shows: One worked orbital state sampled from its probability density.

Does not show: A separate theory from the orbital selector.

Principle: \(dP = |\psi_{320}|^2 r^2\sin\theta\,dr\,d\theta\,d\phi\).

Try: Compare this detailed case with the 3dz² preset.

Bell Singlet Entanglement

Type: Physical model.

Shows: Local randomness and statistical correlation across many trials.

Does not show: Faster-than-light messaging.

Principle: \(E(a,b)=-\cos\theta_{ab}\) and CHSH comparison.

Try: Change detector angles and watch correlations build statistically.

Quantum Channel and Decoherence

Type: Toy physical model.

Shows: How a one-qubit density matrix changes under simple noise channels.

Does not show: A full quantum network or real hardware calibration.

Principle: \(\rho'=\mathcal{E}(\rho)=\sum_i K_i\rho K_i^\dagger\).

Try: Compare before and after matrices while increasing noise.

Gravity Fabric

Type: Analogy model.

Shows: A teaching picture for mass changing geometry.

Does not show: A literal fabric or hidden downward direction.

Principle: Geometry responds to stress-energy in general relativity.

Try: Increase mass strength while keeping the analogy boundary in mind.

Gravitational Lensing

Type: Toy physical model.

Shows: Apparent position shifts, multiple-image behavior, and an Einstein-ring case.

Does not show: Full relativistic ray tracing or telescope data.

Principle: \(\beta = \theta - \theta_E^2/\theta\).

Try: Use the ring preset, then compare source and lensed positions.

Black Hole Shadow

Type: Artistic scientific visualization.

Shows: Shadow, photon-ring-like glow, disk asymmetry, inclination, and spin cues.

Does not show: Telescope data, numerical relativity, plasma turbulence, or the interior.

Principle: Strong gravity bends null paths near compact objects.

Try: Change inclination and spin to compare the disk asymmetry.

Wormhole Geometry

Type: Speculative conceptual model.

Shows: Throat, mouths, flare-out shape, redshift cue, and exotic-stress cue.

Does not show: A travel simulation or evidence for human-scale traversable wormholes.

Principle: Traversability would require severe geometry and energy-condition constraints.

Try: Change throat radius and stress while treating the marker as visual only.

Neural Architecture

Type: Conceptual model.

Shows: Network flow, attention weights, and gradient-based optimization as separate lessons.

Does not show: A real language model, real training data, or actual learned weights.

Principle: Vector transformations, attention, and gradient descent.

Try: Use one mode at a time before combining the ideas.

Entropy Channel

Type: Conceptual model.

Shows: How source probabilities and channel noise affect entropy and mutual information.

Does not show: A real modem or a complete error-correcting code.

Principle: \(H(X)=-\sum p\log_2p\) and \(I(X;Y)=H(X)-H(X|Y)\).

Try: Compare a clean channel with a noisy channel.

3D Algorithm Visualizer

Type: Conceptual model.

Shows: Algorithm state, current operation, and growth of computational cost.

Does not show: Production code or hardware benchmarking.

Principle: Dijkstra relaxation, sorting by comparison, and Big O growth.

Try: Step slowly before running the animation.

Numerical Methods Sandbox

Type: Toy physical model.

Shows: Time stepping, sampling error, and heat-grid stability.

Does not show: A full engineering solver or exact climate/fluid simulation.

Principle: Euler, RK4, Monte Carlo error, and heat-equation stability.

Try: Compare Euler vs RK4, then push heat diffusion near the stability limit.
